# Case Study Format

Every Bangladeshi startup case study follows this 15-field structure. Source-backed only: separate facts from anecdotes, do not overclaim, cite every factual claim.

| # | Field | Question / prompt |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Company | Startup name and basic category. |
| 2 | Problem | What problem did the startup solve? |
| 3 | Bangladesh-specific problem | Why was the problem Bangladesh-specific? |
| 4 | Founding insight | What did the founders see before others did? |
| 5 | First wedge | What narrow entry point helped them start? |
| 6 | Early customers | How did they get early customers? |
| 7 | Trust-building | How did they build trust? |
| 8 | Local lesson | What did they do that generic startup advice would miss? |
| 9 | Constraints | What regulatory, logistics, payment, or talent constraints did they face? |
| 10 | Business model | How did they make money? |
| 11 | Capital | How did they raise capital, if they did? |
| 12 | What to copy | What can a new founder copy? |
| 13 | What not to copy | What should a new founder not copy? |
| 14 | Unknowns | What remains unknown? |
| 15 | Sources | Founder interviews, official announcements, funding articles, reports. |
