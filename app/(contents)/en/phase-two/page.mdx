# Phase 2 Roadmap

> **Summary:** Phase 1 was the stage for MVP, validation, and launching initial operations. Phase 2 is about making your business stronger, compliant, and growth-ready.

## Why is Phase 2 Important?

In Phase 1, you started quickly. In Phase 2, you will sustain that start:

- Establish the correct legal and tax foundation for the business.
- Manage growth and customer retention.
- Become funding-ready or cash-flow-ready.
- Learn to manage sales and distribution on a larger scale.
- Formalize the team, operations, and processes.

## Goals for Phase 2

- Retain the first 100-1000 customers while maintaining your business model.
- Start annual compliance, audit, and tax reporting.
- Make the sales, delivery, and payment process reliable.
- Plan for local/regional expansion.
- Present a professional financial picture to investors or lenders.

## Core Pillars of Phase 2

### 1. Legal & Compliance
- **Tax & Audit:** Filing yearly audits and corporate tax returns on time. Regular VAT/BIN and TIN reporting.
- **Contracts:** Creating supplier/customer contracts and vendor agreements.
- **Digital Policies:** Having a Privacy Policy and Terms of Service for the website.
- **Intellectual Property (IP):** Registering trademarks to protect your brand name or logo.
- **Trade License:** Regularly renewing the trade license.

### 2. Funding & Investment Readiness
- **Financial Model:** Create a simple financial model (Revenue, Cost, Margin).
- **Unit Economics:** Calculate Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV).
- **Pitch Deck:** Prepare a professional Pitch Deck or One-pager for investors.
- **Investor Outreach:** Create a list of grants, accelerators, and angel investors and start outreach. Get a preliminary understanding of Valuation.

### 3. Marketing & Growth
- **Paid Ads Transition:** Starting budget planning for paid marketing (Facebook Ads, Google Ads) from organic growth.
- **Customer Retention:** Implementing Email/SMS marketing or loyalty programs to bring customers back.
- **Brand Building:** Creating a consistent brand identity on social media.

### 4. Product & Customer Operations
- **Feedback Loop:** Formalizing the customer feedback loop.
- **Support SOP:** Creating a Support SOP (WhatsApp/Messenger/Call).
- **Fulfillment:** Standardizing order fulfillment and delivery tracking.
- **Payment Optimization:** Reviewing the payment mix — COD, bKash, Nagad, bank transfer. Professionalizing the website or product listing.

### 5. Team & Operations
- **Hiring:** Hiring the first core team member or outsourcing (e.g., Marketing, Fulfillment, Finance).
- **Payroll & HR:** Creating basic salary structure and HR rules.
- **Bookkeeping:** Starting bookkeeping and bank reconciliation. Using software (e.g., Tally, QuickBooks, or Excel) for daily accounting.
- **Reviews:** Running daily/weekly operation review cycles.

### 6. Scaling and Expansion (outside Dhaka)
- **Regional Expansion:** Running regional customer acquisition. Working in Zonal Facebook groups and marketplaces.
- **Partnerships:** Identifying local courier/agent partners.
- **Logistics:** Monitoring logistics cost and delivery lead time.

## Phase 2 Action Checklist

- [ ] Start annual audit and corporate tax return.
- [ ] Update trade license renewal and local permits.
- [ ] Conduct monthly cash reviews while maintaining a bookkeeping system.
- [ ] Create the first pitch deck or business one-pager.
- [ ] Build a growth metrics tracking spreadsheet.
- [ ] Run paid marketing campaigns and measure ROI.
- [ ] Hire the first local team member or freelance operator.
- [ ] Create core legal documents (contract, terms, privacy).

## Criteria for Success in Phase 2

- Business costs and revenue are tracked regularly, and unit economics are positive.
- Your customers are returning (Repeat rate is increasing).
- Legal and tax compliance documents are up-to-date.
- The team or partner network can operate independently.
- You have a ready pitch deck to speak with investors.

> Phase 2 is the stage where your startup transforms from an “experiment” into a “business.” Structure, trust, and repeatability are more important here.
