# Phase 2 Roadmap – Product, Team, Accounts and Operations

> **In short:** Phase 1 helped you learn whether the problem was real. Now turn that learning into a business you can run consistently. Make the product or service more reliable, keep accounts, understand why customers return, define the first employee or freelancer's work, and meet the rules that apply to you.

At this stage the business moves from “we have started” to “we can keep this running.” You may have completed a pilot, received initial orders or seen some customers return. A major risk now is pushing for more sales before the work is organised, placing pressure on trust, cash, the team or product quality.

Write down the steps for work you repeat. Could someone else follow those instructions tomorrow and complete a task you handle personally today? The answer shows where the business still needs structure.

---

## What to organise in Phase 2

| Area | Goal |
|---|---|
| Product or service | Improve it based on actual use |
| Customer support | Set rules for inboxes, calls, complaints and refunds |
| Payments and accounts | Reconcile orders, payments, couriers and bank records |
| Team | Define work for the first employee, freelancer, intern or operator |
| Law and compliance | Handle applicable licenses, tax, contracts, data and labour requirements |
| Growth | Separate channels that work from those that only create noise |

## Learn from product use

After the first customers arrive, change the product or service based on what they do. Do not rely only on what they say. Note which features they use, where they get stuck, why they ask for refunds and why they do not buy again.

## Accounts and compliance

Without organised accounts, it is hard to know whether the business is making money. Funding, lending, tax and VAT work also become harder. At least monthly, review sales, costs, revenue per customer, delivery cost, refunds, payroll and cash on hand.

Compliance means following the legal, tax and operating rules that apply to your business. When the right records are ready, it is easier to answer requests from banks, corporate customers, payment gateways, suppliers or investors.

Read more: [Audit, accounts and bookkeeping setup](phase-one/audit-accounts-and-bookkeeping-setup), [Payroll process](phase-two/payroll-process-salary-sheet-bank-transfer-and-tax)

## Team and delegation

Write down the work before making the first hire. Instead of saying “we need a marketing person,” define the expected posts, inbox replies, leads, follow-ups and reports. A new hire will not reduce the workload if the role is unclear.

Use written terms with a freelancer, intern or first employee. Clarify the deliverable, payment date, confidentiality and ownership of intellectual property at the beginning.

Read more: [First employee vs freelancer vs intern](phase-two/hiring-first-employee-vs-freelancer-vs-intern), [Freelancer contract and IP ownership](phase-two/freelancer-contract-and-ip-ownership-checklist)

## Retention and growth

Do not focus only on new customers. Learn why existing customers return or leave. Before buying more advertising, address support, delivery, refunds, product quality and the reason to buy again.

Keep a simple monthly report:

- New customers
- Repeat customers
- Acquisition source
- Returned orders
- Time needed to reconcile payments
- Most common customer complaint

## What you should have at the end of Phase 2

- [ ] Written steps for delivering the product or service
- [ ] Records for orders, payments, delivery and refunds
- [ ] A monthly income and expense report
- [ ] Written terms for the first team member or freelancer
- [ ] An understanding of applicable license, tax, VAT and contract needs
- [ ] Evidence showing which growth channels work
