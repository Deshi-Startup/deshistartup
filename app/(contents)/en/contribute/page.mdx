# How to Contribute

> **In short:** Anyone can improve any page of this site – just like Wikipedia. Reporting a mistake takes 2 minutes, a small fix takes 5, and a new guide takes an evening. Your language doesn't need to be perfect – reviewers will polish it.

Rules change, fees change, new questions appear. A knowledge base this size cannot stay accurate with just a handful of maintainers – it needs many eyes and many hands. What you already know (how you got a license, a bank's requirement, a typo you spotted) is someone else's next step.

## Path 1 – Report a mistake (2 minutes)

If you spot an error, an outdated fee, a broken link or a confusing explanation:

1. Click **“Report”** at the top or bottom of the page.
2. A GitHub form opens with the page name pre-filled.
3. Describe what's wrong (with a source, if you know one) and hit **Submit new issue**.

A free GitHub account is all you need.

## Path 2 – Fix it yourself (5–10 minutes)

For a typo, a link, a sentence or a number:

1. Click **“Edit”** on the page you want to fix.
2. GitHub opens the page's text (in Markdown). Click the pencil icon – GitHub automatically creates your own copy (a fork).
3. Make your fix. Don't worry about formatting – plain text is fine.
4. Click **Propose changes**, then **Create pull request**.
5. Once a reviewer approves, your change goes live.

## Path 3 – Write a new guide (the biggest help)

Hundreds of topics on this site are still marked **“to be written”** – you'll find them listed at the bottom of every section page. If you know a topic:

1. Open the topic's page and click **“Write this page”**.
2. Replace the placeholder with your writing. This skeleton works well:

```text
# Title

> **In short:** the essence in 3–4 lines.

## Who needs this, and when
## Step by step
## Costs and timelines (mention the year)
## Common mistakes
## Sources (official links first)
```

3. **Propose changes → Create pull request.**

First-hand experience is the most valuable kind of writing: “I got a trade license from Chattogram City Corporation in 2026 – these were the steps.”

## Writing rules (short version)

- **Think in Bangla, write in Bangla.** Never draft in English and translate — translated
  Bangla always shows. The full standard lives in
  [STYLE.md](https://github.com/Deshi-Startup/deshistartup/blob/main/STYLE.md).
- Simple, everyday language; address the reader directly. Short sentences, one idea each.
- Keep familiar English business terms as-is (trade license, VAT, bank account).
- Explain a new term on first use: “equity means a share of ownership”.
- Cite sources for every legal/fee/regulatory claim – official portals (RJSC, NBR, Bangladesh Bank) are best.
- Date your facts: “as of 2026, the fee is Tk 3,000”.
- Don't write what you're not sure of – or mark it as “needs verification”.

## How review works

Before anything goes live, a reviewer checks: do the facts match the sources, is the language simple, could this harm anyone. Your writing doesn't need to be perfect on the first try – **an incomplete but accurate page always beats an unwritten one.**

## If GitHub is not your thing

Write however you're comfortable – [open an issue](https://github.com/Deshi-Startup/deshistartup/issues/new) and paste your writing directly. Someone will put it in the right place.
