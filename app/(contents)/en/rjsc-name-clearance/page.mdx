# RJSC / Name Clearance / Company Registration

> **In short:** Before starting company registration, you need to handle name clearance properly. This is where many startups face their first procedural bottleneck.

## Why Is Name Clearance Important?

Name clearance at RJSC means your proposed company name has not already been taken by someone else, and the government has approved it.

- Identical or confusingly similar names are not acceptable.
- You should also check domain and trademark availability.
- Once the name is approved, you can prepare the required documents quickly.

## Steps for Name Clearance

1. Create an account on the [ROC / RJSC portal](https://roc.gov.bd).
2. Go to the Name Clearance section.
3. Submit three name preferences.
4. Pay the fee, usually around Tk 600 per name.
5. Wait for approval or rejection.
6. If rejected, submit the next preference.

## Tips for Choosing a Name

- Choose a short, simple, pronounceable name.
- The name must end with “Limited” or “Ltd.”
- Make sure the name is not offensive or misleading.
- Do not use names of government bodies or famous brands.
- Check domain availability, such as .com and .com.bd.

## Documents Needed for Registration

- Memorandum of Association (MoA)
- Articles of Association (AoA)
- Form IX (director consent)
- Form VI (registered office address)
- Directors’ NID copies and photos
- TIN certificates, if already available
- Bank draft or payment receipt

## Registration Process

1. Start registration after name clearance approval.
2. Fill out all forms carefully.
3. Upload the required documents.
4. Pay the registration fee.
5. RJSC will review the documents and approve them.
6. Download the Certificate of Incorporation.

## What to Do After Name Clearance

- Complete company registration.
- Get an e-TIN.
- Start the trade license process.
- Open a bank account.

## Common Problems

- If a name is rejected, you may need to change each word meaningfully.
- A name may look unused to you but still be considered too similar by RJSC.
- Try using clear transliteration or distinctive word combinations.

> Tip: Keep 3-4 strong name options ready before applying, so if the first one is rejected you can quickly move to the next.
