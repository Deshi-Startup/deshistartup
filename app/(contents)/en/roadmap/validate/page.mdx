# Phase 1 Roadmap – Idea, Validation and the First Formal Foundations

> **In short:** You do not need to build a large company in Phase 1. Drop weak assumptions quickly, talk to real customers, see whether a small test earns money or a meaningful commitment, and work out which documents and payment setup you need now.

Moving too fast at this stage increases the risk of building the wrong thing. First check whether the problem actually appears in people's daily lives. In Bangladesh, a small test might use a Facebook page, Messenger, Google Sheets, a simple landing page, a manually delivered service or a pilot with a few customers.

Finishing Phase 1 does not mean everything is settled. It means you should know whose problem you are solving, how people handle it today, whether anyone will spend money or time on your offer, and which documents and operating arrangements may come next.

---

## What you are trying to prove

| Question | A useful signal |
|---|---|
| Is the problem real? | The same problem appears in independent accounts from different people |
| What do people do today? | They already spend time, money or effort on a workaround |
| Will anyone pay? | Someone pays a deposit, places an order or booking, or agrees to a pilot |
| Can you deliver on a small scale? | You can deliver the product or service manually if necessary |
| Do you understand the risks? | Payment, delivery, refund and legal risks are written down |

---

## Recommended order of work

1. Read [Startup vs SME vs agency vs e-commerce](/en/start-here/startup-vs-sme) and decide which path fits.
2. Use [Idea Validation](/en/validation) to understand the problem, customer and existing alternatives.
3. Run a small MVP test and watch real use and payment behaviour.
4. Read [Finding Customers](/en/customers) and decide where the first customers are likely to come from.
5. Set up [Payments](/en/payments) so you can reconcile money with orders.
6. Use the [Legal Roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap) to separate documents you need now from those that may wait.
7. Check official sources to confirm whether you need a trade license, TIN, VAT/BIN registration or a company.

---

## Common Phase 1 mistakes

| Mistake | Better response |
|---|---|
| Building the product before looking for customers | Look for evidence of the problem and willingness to pay first |
| Treating praise from family or friends as validation | Watch the behaviour of independent, realistic customers |
| Trying to complete every document at once | Do the legal work that applies at the current stage |
| Starting without payment and refund rules | Keep records from the first order |
| Stopping at “the market is large” | Write down who the earliest customers could be |

## What you should have at the end of Phase 1

- [ ] One sentence naming the customer and problem
- [ ] Enough customer-interview notes to see whether the problem repeats
- [ ] A map of current alternatives and competitors
- [ ] Results from a small MVP test
- [ ] An initial payment, pilot, order or another real response
- [ ] A simple plan for payment, delivery, refunds and support
- [ ] A list of upcoming legal and tax tasks
