# Phase 3 Roadmap – Growth, Funding and a Repeatable Business

> **In short:** In Phase 3, it is not enough to know that the business is running. Measure where customers come from, what it costs to serve them, whether payments reconcile and how much pressure the team can handle. Then decide whether to grow from operating cash or seek outside investment.

You validated the problem in Phase 1 and organised the product, accounts and day-to-day work in Phase 2. The questions now change: Which channels bring customers? What does one new customer cost? Do customers buy again? Can delivery and support handle twice as many orders? How many months will the cash last? What evidence would support an investment case?

Sales growth can be encouraging, but costs can rise quickly too. Advertising without clear numbers, broad discounts, rushed hiring or a premature city launch can consume cash fast. Review the numbers before making a large spending decision.

---

## Start measuring growth

“Sales are growing” is not enough. Learn which sales leave money after direct costs, which acquisition channels bring repeat customers and where customers leave.

| Metric | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Customer acquisition cost (CAC) | What it costs to acquire one customer |
| Repeat purchase or retention | Whether customers return |
| Average order value | Average revenue per order |
| Gross margin | What remains after the direct cost of delivery |
| Refund or return rate | A signal about trust and operations |
| Cash runway | How many months the available cash may last |

Read more: [Startup Metrics 101](/en/metrics/metrics-101), [CAC, LTV, retention, churn and payback](/en/metrics/cac-ltv-churn)

## Prepare before fundraising

Outside investment may or may not suit the business. If you seek it, expect questions about the market, customer evidence, use of funds, team and accounts. Raising money is not itself a measure of success.

Prepare:

- A clear pitch deck
- Monthly income and expenses
- Customer metrics
- The cap table
- Company records
- Major contracts
- Product, technology and intellectual-property records
- A plan for using the money

Read more: [Pitch deck template](/en/funding/pitch-deck), [Fundraising data room](/en/funding/data-room), [Angel networks in Bangladesh](/en/funding/bangladesh-angels)

## Check operations before expanding

Before adding a new product, team, channel or location, ask: “Where would the pressure appear if today's work doubled?” Review support, delivery, cash-on-delivery reconciliation, bookkeeping and product quality one by one.

Write down:

- Order-to-delivery steps
- Complaint-handling rules
- Refund and return process
- Courier and payment reconciliation
- Team responsibilities
- Weekly reporting

Read more: [Manual operations dashboard](/en/metrics/ops-dashboard), [Working with courier partners](/en/operations/courier-partners)

---

## Test a new city or market

A new city involves more than changing an advertising location. Delivery time, language, local trust, prices, returns, agents, couriers and payment preferences may differ. Start with a small test in one or two cities or regions.

Before launch, write down:

- Why this city or region?
- Where could the earliest customers come from?
- Do you need a local partner?
- What are delivery cost and time?
- What happens to a returned order?
- Who provides support?

Read more: [Selling outside Dhaka](/en/growth/selling-outside-dhaka), [City launch playbook](/en/growth/city-launch-playbook)

## What you should have at the end of Phase 3

- [ ] Growth metrics by channel
- [ ] Monthly income, expenses and cash runway
- [ ] Customer-retention data
- [ ] A pitch deck or business one-pager
- [ ] Written operating procedures and responsibilities
- [ ] A plan for testing a new city or channel
- [ ] A reasoned decision about whether funding is needed
