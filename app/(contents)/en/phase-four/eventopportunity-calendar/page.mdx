# Event/Opportunity Calendar

> **Key Takeaway:** By understanding the Event/Opportunity Calendar, you can better grasp your sector-specific growth path, regulators, customer behavior, and operational bottlenecks.

## Why is this topic important?

By properly understanding the Event/Opportunity Calendar, you can plan in advance how to scale your business, what support you can access, and what mistakes to avoid. This topic can be used as part of your Phase 4 planning, functioning as a Directory / Calendar.

## How is this relevant in Bangladesh?

In Bangladesh, the ecosystem, policy, community, and sector-specific knowledge for startups or SMEs work together. Therefore, this topic is not just information; it acts as an effective tool for an execution roadmap.

## What should you consider first?

- What is the impact of this topic on your business model?
- Which partners, regulators, or support organizations will be useful to you?
- Which steps should be taken first to gain the most value?
- What mistakes might cost you more money later?

## Step-by-Step Action

1. Understand the real needs of customers, buyers, distributors, and regulators in your sector.
2. Create a small validation plan–where will the leads come from, how will payment happen, and how will delivery happen.
3. Incorporate logistics, compliance, supplier reliability, and customer service into your approach.
4. Conduct a pilot launch and track metrics–conversion, refund, repeat rate, margin.
5. Look into opportunities to utilize sector-specific subsidies or support.

## Common Mistakes

- If you don't understand the sector, the product won't reach the market correctly.
- It's not enough to just say "that sector is big"; assessing execution feasibility is crucial.
- Ignoring regulatory and operational complexity will lead to major problems later.

- [ ] Pilot launch plan has been created
- [ ] Compliance and logistics have been addressed upfront
- [ ] A simple KPI dashboard has been created
