# Applying for a BIN: Reconcile These Records Before Mushak-2.1

> **Key Takeaway:** A BIN is the 9-digit business identifier in the VAT system. Both full VAT registration and turnover-tax enlistment produce a BIN, so decide which route applies before starting. Create the VAT Online user for the correct person, complete the current Mushak-2.1 form, and then check the tax type, name, and effective date on the Mushak-2.3 certificate. Receiving a BIN starts invoice, return, and recordkeeping obligations.

Suppose an agency has BDT 4.5 million of turnover over 12 months. The proprietor hears that a BIN is needed and selects full VAT registration, even though the ordinary turnover rules might place the agency under turnover-tax enlistment. Choosing the wrong route changes both the invoice and return obligations.

---

## BIN, TIN, and the Old 13-Digit Number

| Number | System | Identifies |
|---|---|---|
| **TIN** | Income tax | An individual, firm, or company taxpayer |
| **BIN** | VAT or turnover tax | A registered or enlisted business |

NBR's current FAQ states that the BIN in the current VAT system has 9 digits. NBR also maintains a separate search for legacy 13-digit BINs. Do not copy a legacy number into a new application. Use the current BIN shown on the Mushak-2.3 certificate.

It is normal for the same business to have both a TIN and BIN. They are not interchangeable. See the [e-TIN Guide](/en/tax/e-tin) for the income-tax side.

---

## Decide Between Registration and Enlistment

**Full VAT registration:** Generally above BDT 8 million of turnover over 12 months, for a special mandatory class, or voluntarily. A fully registered person issues Mushak-6.3 tax invoices and files monthly returns.

**Turnover-tax enlistment:** Generally above BDT 3 million and up to BDT 8 million. The current rate is 3%. An enlisted person issues Mushak-6.9 invoices and has quarterly turnover-tax periods.

If the answer is unclear, first use [When Full VAT Registration or Turnover Tax Applies](/en/tax/when-vat-required) and [Turnover Tax and VAT Thresholds](/en/tax/vat-thresholds).

## Reconcile the Information Before Opening the Form

NBR's FAQ says that separate documents are not required to be submitted for registration or enlistment. Do not copy a long mandatory-upload list from an old blog. Keep these supporting records nearby so the form is accurate:

- NID or applicable identity document for an individual
- Certificate of Incorporation and RJSC particulars for a company
- Registration and partner information for a firm
- e-TIN, where applicable
- Legal and trading names
- Full principal and branch addresses
- Bank-account information
- H.S. or service codes for the supplies
- Preceding and succeeding 12-month turnover calculations
- Import, export, and withholding-entity status

The 2026 amended Mushak-2.1 form requests e-TIN “where applicable.” Give the information of the person obtaining the BIN. Do not substitute a director's personal TIN for the company's TIN.

## Apply Through VAT Online

1. Open [VAT Online](https://vat.gov.bd) and create the user in the name of the person to be registered or enlisted.
2. Keep the mobile number and email under the business's control, not an agent's personal account.
3. Open the current Mushak-2.1 under registration and select the correct ownership type.
4. Enter the legal name, trading name, TIN where applicable, addresses, bank details, activity, and codes.
5. Reconcile the requested full registration or turnover-tax enlistment with the turnover calculation.
6. If there are branches, understand central and separate registration. One central registration generally covers the same entity's economic activity, while a branch with independent records may seek separate registration.
7. Download the draft or preserve the screens and ask another person to check the names, figures, codes, and addresses.
8. Submit and retain the acknowledgment and application number. Respond to any query by following the comment.
9. After the certificate is created, download Mushak-2.3 and check the BIN, tax type, effective date, and address.

There is no government fee for the online application. Any adviser charge is for the service. Do not promise a certificate date. NBR's FAQ refers both to three working days after verification and to instantaneous online registration for a correct application. Validation, queries, and portal conditions can change the actual timing.

---

## Check Five Fields on the Certificate

- Legal name of the person
- Trading name, if any
- 9-digit BIN
- VAT registration or turnover-tax enlistment
- Effective date and business address

Do not issue invoices from an incorrect certificate. Use the portal's information-change procedure. Current Rules require many changes to be reported within 15 working days.

---

## Work That Starts After the BIN

| Status | Invoice | Tax period | Core work |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full VAT registration | Mushak-6.3 | Monthly | Output VAT, input-tax credits, withholding VAT, and return |
| Turnover-tax enlistment | Mushak-6.9 | Quarterly | 3% of turnover, sales, and invoice records |

Both statuses require the certificate to be displayed, the BIN to be used in specified records, and changes to be reported. A zero return may still have to be filed. Assign ownership of invoicing, bookkeeping, and returns before applying.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a legacy 13-digit BIN as a new e-BIN
- Entering the TIN and BIN interchangeably
- Applying without distinguishing full registration from enlistment
- Treating an old blog's document list as the current mandatory list
- Guessing the goods or service code
- Registering with an agent's mobile number and email
- Issuing Mushak-6.3 without checking the certificate's tax type
- Failing to assign return ownership after receiving the BIN

## Checklist

- [ ] Recorded the decision to register or enlist
- [ ] Applying in the correct individual's, firm's, or company's name
- [ ] Reconciled the legal name, TIN, and RJSC particulars
- [ ] Verified the activity and codes
- [ ] Kept the 12-month turnover calculation
- [ ] Business controls the mobile, email, and login
- [ ] Checked all five fields on the Mushak-2.3 certificate
- [ ] Assigned invoicing, bookkeeping, and return responsibilities

Rule of thumb: **Check the tax type on the certificate before the BIN number. Registration and enlistment use the same identification system but create different work.**
