# Step-by-Step Private Limited Company Registration

> **Key Takeaway:** Do not start uploading documents as soon as you receive name clearance. First write down who will own how many shares, who the directors will be, where the registered office will be, and what the company will do. Then prepare the MoA/AoA and RJSC Forms I, VI, IX, X, and XII. Submit them through the portal and pay the generated government charges. Registration ends when you receive the Certificate of Incorporation, but company TIN, banking, trade licensing, and ongoing records still remain.

Suppose Sumiya and her co-founder agree to own a software company 60:40, but the subscriber page says 50:50. One document spells a name as “Md.” and another as “Mohammad.” Small inconsistencies like these can trigger a query. The cheapest time to catch them is before the application starts.

This guide covers an ordinary private company with Bangladeshi shareholders. Foreign shareholders, regulated sectors, or special licences may add steps. In those cases, read [Basics of Starting a Foreign-Owned Company in Bangladesh](../phase-one/basics-of-starting-a-foreign-owned-company-in-bangladesh) and obtain professional advice.

---

## Step 1: Write Down the Ownership Before Registration

Put these decisions on one page before opening the portal:

- The cleared company name and exact spelling
- The registered-office address
- The shareholders and the number of shares each will take
- The directors
- The authorised capital and initial paid-up capital
- The activities the company will carry on
- The approvals needed for major decisions

A share is one small unit of company ownership. If Sumiya takes 600 of 1,000 shares, she owns 60%. That number must agree across the MoA, AoA, subscriber page, and forms.

Pause if the founders have not settled ownership. Shares can be changed later, but doing so creates more decisions, paperwork, and filings.

---

## Step 2: Check the Name-Clearance Expiry Date

Obtain RJSC name clearance before applying to register a private company. Check the approved spelling and expiry date on the clearance letter. If the 30-day validity period is almost over, finish the documents promptly or review the extension procedure.

The complete procedure is in [How to Get RJSC Name Clearance](../phase-one/rjsc-name-clearance). Name clearance does not create a company. It only reserves the approved name for a limited period.

---

## Step 3: Prepare the MoA and AoA

The Memorandum of Association, or MoA, states the company's name, objects, liability, and share-capital basics. The Articles of Association, or AoA, set the internal rules for shares, directors, meetings, and decisions.

Do not copy another company's documents and replace its name. The documents must match your actual business, ownership, and decision rules. Use [Memorandum and Articles of Association Basics](../phase-one/memorandum-and-articles-of-association-basics) to review the draft.

Check each subscriber's share count, identity details, and signature. Complete the witness details as well.

---

## Step 4: Reconcile the RJSC Forms

RJSC's [official FAQ](https://app1.roc.gov.bd/Guidlines/faq.html) lists these forms and documents for a private-company registration application:

- **Form I:** Declaration that the Companies Act requirements have been met
- **Form VI:** Notice of the registered-office address
- **Form IX:** Consent to act as a director
- **Form X:** List of people who have agreed to become directors
- **Form XII:** Particulars of directors and management
- **MoA and AoA:** The company's constitutional documents, with signatures and witnesses
- **Evidence of name clearance**
- **Evidence of the applicable stamps and fees**

Follow the portal's current instructions if it requests identity documents, photographs, addresses, or other supporting information. Keep names, dates of birth, addresses, and identification numbers consistent across every document.

---

## Step 5: Submit Through the Portal and Pay

Open an application through [RJSC online services](https://app1.roc.gov.bd/psp/rjschome). Enter the information, upload the prepared documents, and pay against the challan generated by the portal.

Charges vary with the documents and authorised capital. On 10 July 2026, [RJSC's current fee page](https://app1.roc.gov.bd/psp/RJSC_Fees) showed the following for a private company with authorised capital of up to BDT 1 million:

| Government charge | Amount checked on 10 July 2026 |
|---|---:|
| MoA stamp | BDT 1,000 |
| AoA stamp | BDT 2,000 |
| Filing six documents | BDT 1,200 |
| Share-capital fee up to BDT 1 million | BDT 0 |

Adding BDT 500 for one proposed name gives this example government total:

```text
1,000 + 2,000 + 1,200 + 500 = BDT 4,700
```

This example covers government fees and stamps only. It excludes payment charges, notarisation, professional fees, and other support costs. The figure changes above BDT 1 million in authorised capital. Treat the portal-generated challan and current fee calculator as final before paying.

---

## Step 6: Answer a Query by Finding the Mismatch

RJSC may ask a question or request a correction when documents do not agree. Match the comment to the relevant document instead of rebuilding the entire application. First check share counts, name spellings, signatures, addresses, and form data.

Do not promise an approval date. Section 23 of the Companies Act gives the Registrar 30 days when the submitted documents meet the requirements, but the actual duration of your application depends on queries, corrections, and portal conditions. Do not submit one week before an urgent contract and assume registration will be complete.

---

## Step 7: Download and Check the Certificate

After approval, download the Certificate of Incorporation. Check the company name, registration number, and registration date. This PDF is the principal evidence that the company has been incorporated.

Keep these records together:

- Certificate of Incorporation
- Approved MoA and AoA
- Submitted forms
- Evidence of fees and stamps
- Name Clearance Letter

Receiving the certificate does not finish every business formality. You still need to organise the company TIN, bank account, applicable trade licence and BIN, share register, and ongoing filings.

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Reconciled the shareholders, share counts, and directors
- [ ] Confirmed that the name clearance is still valid
- [ ] Matched the MoA and AoA to the real business and ownership
- [ ] Prepared Forms I, VI, IX, X, and XII
- [ ] Used the same names, addresses, and identification numbers throughout
- [ ] Paid against the portal-generated challan
- [ ] Saved the application number and payment evidence
- [ ] Corrected any query by following the comment
- [ ] Downloaded the certificate and approved documents
- [ ] Made a list of the post-registration work

Rule of thumb: **Write the ownership down before opening the portal. Five versions of the same fact across five documents invite a query.**
