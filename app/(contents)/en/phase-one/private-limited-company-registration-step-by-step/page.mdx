# Step-by-Step Private Limited Company Registration

> **Key Takeaway:** Once name clearance is approved, the rest of registering a private limited company comes down to preparing documents, submitting them online through the RJSC portal, paying the registration fee and stamp duty, and receiving the Certificate of Incorporation. A single wrong document or figure at any step can push the whole process back by a week or more, so it helps to know the full list upfront.

With clean paperwork, the process (after name clearance) usually takes 7-10 working days. If the company has foreign shareholders, add extra time for the bank encashment certificate.

## 1. You Need Name Clearance First

The step before registration is getting Name Clearance from RJSC. Without it, you can't start the registration process at all. See [How to Get RJSC Name Clearance](../phase-one/rjsc-name-clearance) for details.

## 2. Temporary Bank Account (If You Have Foreign Shareholders)

Local founders usually skip this step. But if the company has a foreign shareholder, you need to open a temporary bank account in the proposed company's name, remit the paid-up capital into it, and get an Encashment Certificate from the bank. See [Basics of Starting a Foreign-Owned Company in Bangladesh](../phase-one/basics-of-starting-a-foreign-owned-company-in-bangladesh) for details.

## 3. Prepare the Required Documents

You'll typically need:

- Signed and stamped Memorandum of Association (MoA) and Articles of Association (AoA)
- Form IX: consent of directors
- Form XII: particulars of directors
- Subscriber page (who's taking how many shares)
- NID/passport copies of directors and shareholders
- Proof of the registered office address
- Bank Encashment Certificate, if there's a foreign shareholder

Copy-pasting a template MoA/AoA is risky, since these documents define the company's objectives, share structure, and decision-making rules. See [Memorandum and Articles of Association Basics](../phase-one/memorandum-and-articles-of-association-basics) for details.

## 4. Submit the Application on RJSC's Portal and Pay Fees

Log in to RJSC's online portal, upload the signed MoA/AoA and all forms, then pay the registration fee and the stamp duty (which scales with authorized capital) online. Fees rise with the authorized capital amount, so calculate the exact cost with [RJSC's official Fee Calculator](https://app.roc.gov.bd/psp/fee_calculator) before submitting. For the full fee and timeline breakdown, see [RJSC Fees and Timeline Expectations](../phase-one/rjsc-fees-and-timeline-expectations).

## 5. RJSC Review and the Certificate of Incorporation

RJSC reviews the submitted documents, usually within a few working days. If everything checks out, it issues a digital Certificate of Incorporation with a registration number, the legal proof the company now exists. If anything is inconsistent, RJSC sends a query, and you'll need to respond and resubmit, which adds time.

## Common Mistakes

- Submitting an MoA/AoA copied word-for-word from another company's template without understanding it.
- Skipping RJSC's Fee Calculator and paying the wrong stamp duty or fee amount, which gets the application stuck.
- Leaving small inconsistencies in directors' or shareholders' NID details, which RJSC's query process catches.
- Treating the certificate as the finish line and delaying the next steps, like e-TIN, a bank account, and a trade license.

## Checklist

- [ ] Name clearance in hand and still within its validity window
- [ ] MoA/AoA prepared with professional help, signed, and stamped
- [ ] Form IX, Form XII, subscriber page, and all director/shareholder documents ready
- [ ] Registration fee and stamp duty calculated in advance with RJSC's Fee Calculator
- [ ] Post-certificate to-do list (e-TIN, bank account, trade license) written down
