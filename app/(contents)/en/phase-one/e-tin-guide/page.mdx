# e-TIN: Whose Name to Use and How to Apply

> **Key Takeaway:** An e-TIN is the 12-digit taxpayer identification number issued by NBR. A sole proprietor uses a personal TIN. An incorporated company needs a separate company TIN. Having a TIN does not by itself mean tax is payable, and a TIN certificate does not prove that a return was filed. Before applying, decide whether the taxpayer is the individual, a sole proprietorship, a firm, or a company.

Suppose Tanvir already has a personal e-TIN and later incorporates Tanvir Tech Limited. His number belongs to his personal income and return. The company's sales, expenses, and return belong under a separate company TIN. Reusing one number changes the apparent owner of the transaction.

---

## Separate Three Different Records

| Record | Plain meaning | What it does not prove |
|---|---|---|
| **TIN** | NBR's identifier for a taxpayer | That a return has been filed |
| **TIN certificate** | A record of the taxpayer's name, address, TIN, and tax jurisdiction | Tax payment or return submission |
| **Proof of return submission** | Acknowledgment for a specified assessment year | That every tax liability has been settled |

Section 261 of the Income Tax Act, 2023 identifies who must register as an assessee. It includes people required to file a return, people required to furnish proof of return submission, and people who voluntarily wish to file or pay tax. NBR issues a TIN to the registered assessee.

Section 264 separately requires **proof of return submission** for specified activities. Founder-relevant examples include:

- Becoming a director or sponsor shareholder of a company
- Obtaining or renewing a trade licence in a city corporation or municipality
- Obtaining or continuing an import or export registration certificate
- Submitting a tender to supply goods or services or execute a contract
- Selling goods or services to consumers in Bangladesh through a digital platform

For those activities, obtaining a new TIN is not the last step. The relevant assessment year's return must also have been submitted.

## Whose Name Should Be on the TIN?

### A Sole Proprietor or Professional

A sole proprietorship is not a company separate from its owner. Its business income goes into the individual's income-tax position, and the TIN belongs to that person. A trade licence may carry the establishment's trading name while the TIN belongs to the proprietor.

### A Partnership Firm

The tax positions of a partnership firm and its partners are not the same. Check NBR's current process for registering the firm and obtaining its TIN. Do not use one partner's personal TIN as the firm's number.

### A Private Limited Company

After incorporation, a company is a separate person under company law. Section 166 of the Income Tax Act requires a company to file a return, so the company needs its own TIN. A director's or shareholder's personal number does not become the company's number.

Use [Personal e-TIN vs Company TIN](../phase-one/personal-e-tin-vs-company-tin) for a quick decision guide.

## Prepare the Information Before Applying

Portal fields can change, but reconcile these records before starting.

**For an individual:**

- Name and date of birth from the NID or applicable identity document
- A mobile number and email address you control
- Present and permanent addresses
- An accurate description of profession or income source

**For a company:**

- Certificate of Incorporation
- RJSC registration number and date
- Company name and registered-office address
- A short description of the principal business
- Identity and contact details of the authorised representative

Keep spellings and dates consistent. A person may understand that “Md Rahim,” “Md. Rahim,” and “Mohammad Rahim” refer to the same person, while an online validation may not.

## Apply Through the NBR Portal

1. Open the [NBR e-TIN portal](https://secure.incometax.gov.bd/TINHome) and start new-user registration.
2. Complete the mobile or email verification requested by the portal.
3. Select the correct taxpayer type: individual, firm, or company. A wrong type may not be fixable by changing the name later.
4. Enter the identity, address, income-source, or business information. For a company, copy the RJSC particulars exactly.
5. Review the draft and reconcile the name, dates, address, and tax jurisdiction before submitting.
6. After successful registration, download and store the TIN certificate PDF.
7. Keep the login, registered mobile number, and email under the taxpayer's control. Do not leave them with an agent's personal account.

There is no government charge to register for a TIN through the NBR portal. Any amount paid to an adviser is a service fee, not a TIN fee. Do not promise a completion time. A mismatch or additional portal verification can delay the certificate.

## A TIN Can Create Ongoing Work

Section 166 may require a TIN holder to file returns. Companies, registered assessees, and shareholder directors are among the listed persons. Do not obtain a TIN and then skip the return because there was no income. A zero-income or loss year can still require a return.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating a TIN certificate as proof of return submission
- Using a personal TIN in the company's name
- Treating a sole proprietorship as a company and seeking a second company number
- Mistyping a name, date of birth, or RJSC number
- Letting an agent control the registered mobile number and email
- Failing to calendar the return obligation after receiving a TIN

## Checklist

- [ ] Chosen the correct taxpayer type: individual, sole proprietor, firm, or company
- [ ] Reconciled names and dates against identity and RJSC records
- [ ] Used a mobile number and email under the taxpayer's control
- [ ] Reviewed the address and tax jurisdiction before submission
- [ ] Stored the TIN certificate securely
- [ ] Recorded the return obligation and next deadline
- [ ] Checked whether a transaction requires the TIN or proof of return submission

Rule of thumb: **A TIN is an identity. Proof of return submission is the receipt for one assessment year's filing. Do not confuse them.**
