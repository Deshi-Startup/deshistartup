# e-TIN Guide

> **Key Takeaway:** e-TIN (Electronic Taxpayer Identification Number) is the 12-digit unique number NBR issues to identify you as a taxpayer. It's mandatory for a long list of things, from opening a bank account and getting a trade license to registering a company. Registration itself is free and online, and the certificate is usually generated within 15 to 20 minutes if everything checks out, but getting the details wrong or mixing up a personal e-TIN with a company's causes problems later.

This guide covers what e-TIN is, who needs one, and how to register on NBR's portal. For a detailed comparison of when a personal e-TIN is enough versus when the company needs its own separate TIN, see [Personal e-TIN vs Company TIN](../phase-one/personal-e-tin-vs-company-tin).

## 1. What e-TIN Is and Why It Matters

e-TIN is the unique identity number NBR assigns to every taxpayer, individual or company, under the income tax system. Since 2013, NBR has phased out the old manual/10-digit TIN and moved entirely to the online, 12-digit e-TIN system. While it's essential for filing income tax returns, in practice e-TIN also functions as a kind of "proof you exist officially" that banks, RJSC, and various licensing authorities check before taking you seriously.

It's worth remembering that having an e-TIN doesn't automatically mean you owe tax; it just registers you in the system. Exactly what income level triggers actual tax liability, or what turnover triggers VAT registration, is outside the scope of this guide. For the specific thresholds, see [Turnover Tax and VAT Threshold Explainer](../phase-one/turnover-tax-and-vat-threshold-explainer).

## 2. Who Needs an e-TIN

Under the income tax law, e-TIN has to be shown or is mandatory in a number of specific situations. The most common ones:

- Filing an income tax return once income crosses the taxable threshold.
- Opening a business bank account, or taking a loan or credit card in a company's name.
- Getting or renewing a trade license (city corporations and municipalities frequently require it).
- Registering a company with RJSC, and later getting an Import/Export Registration Certificate (IRC/ERC).
- Registering a vehicle or transferring ownership (BRTA).
- Participating in a government tender, or joining a trade body or chamber.
- Running as a candidate for city corporation or parliamentary elections.

For individual founders, the practical reality is that a personal e-TIN is usually enough to get started, but once the company is registered, the company needs its own separate e-TIN. When mixing the two up becomes dangerous is covered in detail in [Personal e-TIN vs Company TIN](../phase-one/personal-e-tin-vs-company-tin). VAT registration and the BIN (Business Identification Number) are an entirely separate system; for when VAT applies, see [VAT/BIN Guide](../phase-one/vatbin-guide) and [When VAT Registration is Required](../phase-one/when-vat-registration-is-required).

## 3. Steps to Register for e-TIN Online

The entire process runs through NBR's e-TIN registration portal ([secure.incometax.gov.bd/TINHome](https://secure.incometax.gov.bd/TINHome)):

1. Go to the portal and click "Registration" to create a new account. You'll need an active mobile number and email address.
2. Verify your mobile number with the OTP sent to it.
3. For an individual, fill in the form with your NID number, name, address, and source of income. For a company, you'll need RJSC's incorporation number and date, the company's registered address, and the authorized representative's (MD/Chairman) details.
4. Select the tax circle/zone you fall under (the system usually suggests one based on your address).
5. Once submitted, the system automatically generates the TIN certificate, which you can download immediately as a PDF; a copy is also sent to your registered email.

If your session times out or you don't download the certificate right away, you can log back in and retrieve it from "View TIN Certificate." Resident individuals and locally RJSC-registered companies can generally self-register through this online process. Foreign nationals, or entities without a local presence in Bangladesh, often need to apply directly through the relevant tax circle office or an authorized representative (a tax lawyer or firm); for the specific rules around foreign-owned companies, see [Basics of Starting a Foreign-Owned Company in Bangladesh](../phase-one/basics-of-starting-a-foreign-owned-company-in-bangladesh).

Keep in mind that the e-TIN registration portal ([secure.incometax.gov.bd](https://secure.incometax.gov.bd)) and the e-Return portal used for filing annual returns are separate systems with separate logins. For how and when to actually file returns after getting a TIN, see [How to File Tax and VAT Returns](../phase-one/how-to-file-tax-and-vat-returns) and the [Tax Calendar](../phase-one/tax-calendar-income-tax-vat-tds-vds-and-annual-returns).

## 4. Documents and Information You'll Need

**For individuals:**

- National ID (NID). If it isn't linked to your mobile number or the details don't match, registration can get stuck.
- An active mobile number and email address (for the OTP and the certificate).
- Current address and source of income (salary, business, profession, etc.).

**For companies:**

- RJSC's Certificate of Incorporation, registration number, and date.
- The company's registered office address.
- Certified copies of the MoA and AoA (worth having ready to upload).
- Trade license, if already obtained (often done around the same time).
- The authorized representative's (MD/Chairman) NID, mobile number, and email.

If you registered a personal e-TIN as a founder and later incorporate a company, the company needs its own separate e-TIN; one doesn't substitute for the other. See [Personal e-TIN vs Company TIN](../phase-one/personal-e-tin-vs-company-tin) for details.

## 5. Time, Cost, and Common Snags

- **Cost:** e-TIN registration and certificate download are completely free. If anyone charges you money, that's a consultant's or agency's service fee, not a government fee.
- **Time:** With accurate details and an NID-linked mobile number in hand, the whole process usually takes 15 to 20 minutes, and the certificate is generated instantly. Company registrations, or applications with mismatched details, can take longer.
- **Common snags:** A mobile number not linked to the NID, mismatches between the name/address on file and on the NID, and selecting the wrong tax circle.

Portal steps, form fields, and processing times change from time to time, so it's worth checking NBR's portal directly for the latest instructions before applying.

## Common Mistakes

- Sitting on an old manual/10-digit TIN certificate instead of re-registering it into the new 12-digit e-TIN system.
- Trying to run all of a company's banking or licensing on a personal e-TIN when the company needs its own.
- Starting registration without first checking whether the mobile number is linked to the NID.
- Closing the session without downloading the certificate right after it's generated.
- Confusing the e-TIN registration portal with the e-Return portal used for annual filing.

## Checklist

- [ ] Confirmed whether an e-TIN is needed, and whether it should be personal or in the company's name
- [ ] Verified the mobile number is linked to the NID
- [ ] For a company, RJSC incorporation number, MoA/AoA, and registered address are ready
- [ ] Registration completed on NBR's official portal (secure.incometax.gov.bd)
- [ ] Certificate PDF downloaded and stored somewhere safe
