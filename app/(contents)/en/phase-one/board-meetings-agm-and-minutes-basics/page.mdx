# Board Meetings, AGM, and Minutes Book Basics

> **Key Takeaway:** Whether your company has two shareholders or twenty, holding an Annual General Meeting (AGM), running board meetings, and keeping a minutes book is a legal requirement for every RJSC-registered private limited company in Bangladesh, not an optional formality. Getting this right from day one avoids fines, due-diligence red flags with investors, and shareholder disputes later.

Many founders assume board resolutions and AGMs only matter for large, listed companies. Under the Companies Act, 1994, they apply to every private limited company as well.

## 1. When You Must Hold the AGM

Under Section 81 of the Companies Act, 1994:
- The first AGM must be held within 18 months of incorporation.
- After that, at least one AGM must be held every calendar year, and the gap between two AGMs cannot exceed 15 months.
- Failing to hold the AGM on time can carry a fine of up to Tk 10,000, plus Tk 250 per day for continuing default.
- If you can't hold the AGM on time, you can apply to RJSC for an extension within 30 days of the deadline expiring (extensions are typically capped at 90 days).

**What happens at the AGM:** presenting and approving the audited balance sheet and profit & loss account, appointing or reappointing the auditor, declaring a dividend if applicable, and re-electing directors.

## 2. How Often to Hold Board Meetings

The Companies Act, 1994 doesn't mandate a fixed number of board meetings (this is usually set in the company's Articles of Association), but in practice, holding at least one board meeting per quarter is good discipline, especially once you have or plan to raise from investors.

- **AGM vs. board meeting:** Shareholders attend the AGM and approve major matters (accounts, auditor appointment). Only directors attend board meetings, which handle important operational decisions (approving large contracts, opening bank accounts, appointing officers).
- Quorum (how many directors must be present for a valid meeting) is usually set in the company's Articles of Association. Check the AoA filed at registration.

## 3. Keeping a Minutes Book Is Mandatory

Under Section 178 of the Companies Act, 1994, every company must record the minutes of every general meeting (AGM/EGM) and every board meeting in a dedicated book.

- Minutes signed by the chairman of the meeting are treated as prima facie evidence, meaning the meeting is presumed to have happened exactly as recorded.
- The minutes book must be kept at the company's registered office.
- Any shareholder can inspect it on written request and payment of a fee (the company must allow at least 2 hours of access per day).

## 4. Types of Resolutions

- **Ordinary resolution:** Passed by a simple majority (50%+), used for routine decisions.
- **Special resolution (Section 87):** Required for changing the company name, altering the Memorandum or Articles of Association, or reducing share capital. It needs at least 21 days' notice and support from at least three-fourths (75%) of members present and voting.
- **Board resolution:** Passed by directors in a board meeting, e.g., opening a bank account, authorizing signatories, approving a major contract. Banks and investors frequently ask for a copy of these.

For the full calendar of which forms (Schedule X, Form XII, Form 23B, and more) to file with RJSC after your AGM and board decisions, and by when, see [RJSC Annual Returns and Filing Calendar](../phase-one/rjsc-annual-returns-and-filing-calendar). Skipping these filings risks a show-cause notice, fines, and, if neglected long enough, the company being struck off.

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming that because the company is just family or friends, AGMs and minutes "aren't needed," and filing nothing for years.
- Making board decisions verbally with no written resolution or minutes. This becomes a problem when a bank or investor later asks for proof.
- Discovering right before a fundraise that three years of AGMs and minutes are missing, a major red flag in due diligence.

## Checklist

- [ ] First AGM held within 18 months of incorporation
- [ ] AGM held every calendar year, gap never exceeding 15 months
- [ ] Minutes of every AGM and board meeting written, signed by the chairman, and kept in the minutes book
- [ ] Written board/special resolutions kept for major decisions (bank accounts, large contracts, share changes)
- [ ] You know which forms are due at RJSC after each AGM, and file them on time
