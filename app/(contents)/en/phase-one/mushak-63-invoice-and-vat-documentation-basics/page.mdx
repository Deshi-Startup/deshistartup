# Mushak 6.3 Invoice and VAT Documentation Basics

> **Key Takeaway:** A VAT-registered business must issue a "Mushak 6.3" (VAT invoice/tax challan) on every taxable sale. It must clearly show the seller's and buyer's name, address, and BIN, an invoice serial number, a description of the goods or service, quantity, unit price, total value, and the VAT rate and amount. These invoices are the basis for the purchase/sales registers and the monthly VAT return, so an incorrect or incomplete invoice causes problems later during an audit, an input tax credit claim, or return filing.

This guide assumes your business is already VAT-registered (you have a BIN). If you haven't registered yet, start with [BIN and VAT Registration Guide](../phase-one/vatbin-guide), and check [When VAT Registration Is Required](../phase-one/when-vat-registration-is-required) and [Turnover Tax and VAT Threshold Explainer](../phase-one/turnover-tax-and-vat-threshold-explainer) to confirm whether registration applies to you or you fall under turnover tax instead. Here, we cover the day-to-day documentation you need once you're registered.

## 1. What "Mushak" Means, and Which Form 6.3 Is

"Mushak" is the Bangla name used for VAT-related official forms, prescribed under the Value Added Tax and Supplementary Duty Act, 2012 and the VAT and Supplementary Duty Rules, 2016 issued under it. These forms follow a numbering system (6.1, 6.2, 6.3, and so on), where each number serves a different purpose.

NBR's own VAT compliance guide states, as a taxpayer responsibility: "Ask for VAT Invoice (Mushak-6.3) during purchase and issue the same during sales." In other words, Mushak 6.3 is the core tax invoice/challan that must be issued on every taxable sale.

**Caution:** Many blogs and third-party sources online give conflicting numbers and names for Mushak forms (some sources call 6.1 the invoice, others call 6.3 a register). The details below are cross-checked against NBR's own page and multiple independent sources, but before relying on any specific detail, always confirm against the current official form (vat.gov.bd or your local VAT circle).

## 2. What a Mushak 6.3 Invoice Must Contain

A Mushak 6.3 invoice typically needs to show:

- **Seller information:** business name, address, and BIN (Business Identification Number).
- **Buyer information:** name, address, and BIN (if the buyer is also VAT-registered).
- **Invoice serial number:** sequential and unique, never reused.
- **Date and time of issue.**
- **Description of goods/services:** quantity and unit (pieces, kg, etc.), plus the HS code where applicable.
- **Unit price and total value (net value).**
- **VAT rate and amount:** the standard rate or the applicable specific/reduced rate.
- **Supplementary duty**, if the goods fall under it.
- **Signature of the issuing officer and the organization's seal/stamp.**

**Practical tip:** Serial number and date discipline causes the most trouble, since these serials can get checked for continuity during monthly return filing. If you're writing invoices manually, follow one consistent numbering system and never skip or repeat a number.

## 3. Who Issues It, and When

Any VAT-registered seller issues a Mushak 6.3 at the time of every taxable sale, cash or credit, whenever goods are delivered or a service is rendered. If the buyer is also VAT-registered and wants to claim input tax credit, it's their responsibility to ask for a correct Mushak 6.3 at the time of purchase, since a claim without a proper invoice can be disallowed.

For larger-turnover businesses, NBR is progressively making it mandatory to generate invoices through an Electronic Fiscal Device (EFD) or NBR-approved software. The specific turnover threshold for this requirement can change over time, so confirm with your local VAT circle or a VAT consultant whether your business currently falls under this mandate.

## 4. Other Mushak Forms Related to Mushak 6.3

Running a small business, you'll hear several other Mushak numbers besides 6.3. It's worth knowing roughly what each one does, even though the details of each are outside the scope of this guide:

- **Mushak 6.1 (Purchase Register/Book):** the month's purchase transactions, kept based on the Mushak 6.3 invoices received.
- **Mushak 6.2 (Sales Register/Book):** the month's consolidated sales transactions.
- **Mushak 6.5 (Invoice for transfer of goods):** used when goods move between branches or warehouses as an internal transfer rather than a sale.
- **Mushak 6.6 (VAT Deduction Certificate):** issued when VAT is deducted at source from a supplier.
- **Mushak 6.7 and 6.8 (Credit note and debit note):** for sales returns, discounts, or correcting errors on an invoice already issued.
- **Mushak 6.10:** a separate record for large-value single-challan purchases/sales.
- **Mushak 9.1:** the monthly VAT return, where the totals from the Mushak 6.1/6.2 registers get reported. For the full filing process, see [Monthly Return Workflow After BIN/VAT Registration](../phase-one/monthly-return-workflow-after-binvat-registration).

The core idea: Mushak 6.3 invoices feed into the 6.1 and 6.2 registers, and the totals from those registers get reported on the Mushak 9.1 return. If your invoices aren't kept in order from day one, reconciling the return at month-end gets hard.

## Common Mistakes

- Selling on a verbal basis or with just a plain cash memo instead of issuing a proper Mushak 6.3 format invoice.
- Writing the buyer's or seller's BIN incorrectly or leaving it out, which causes problems with input tax credit or an audit later.
- Not keeping invoice serial numbers sequential, or reusing the same number.
- Not updating the Mushak 6.1/6.2 registers regularly and trying to reconstruct them all at month-end, which introduces errors.
- Not preserving invoice copies and registers for long enough; VAT records generally need to be kept for a number of years after the relevant tax period ends, so confirm the exact retention period with a VAT consultant.

## Checklist

- [ ] A Mushak 6.3 format invoice is issued on every taxable sale
- [ ] The invoice correctly shows the seller's and buyer's name, address, and BIN
- [ ] Invoice serial numbers and dates are sequential and unique
- [ ] The VAT rate and amount are calculated correctly
- [ ] The Mushak 6.1 (purchase) and 6.2 (sales) registers are updated regularly
- [ ] Invoice and register copies are stored safely for the required retention period
