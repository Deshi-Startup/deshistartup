# Mushak-6.3 Tax Invoice: What Goes in Each Field

> **Key Takeaway:** A fully VAT-registered person issues a Mushak-6.3 tax invoice for each supply. It shows the actual issue date and time, supplier's name, address and BIN, description, value excluding VAT, rate, VAT amount, and total including VAT. For a supply above BDT 25,000, include the buyer's name, address, and BIN where applicable. Number invoices sequentially by fiscal year, make at least two copies, and keep the records for five years.

Suppose a software company provides BDT 100,000 of setup services. Its ordinary invoice says only “Total BDT 115,000 including VAT.” The reader cannot see the base value, VAT rate, or VAT amount. Mushak-6.3 must separate those figures.

This guide is for fully VAT-registered businesses. If Mushak-2.3 says “turnover-tax enlistment,” issue Mushak-6.9 rather than Mushak-6.3. See the [BIN Application Guide](../phase-one/vatbin-guide) to check the status.

---

## When to Issue the Invoice

Rule 40 of the VAT Rules requires a registered person to issue Mushak-6.3 **against each supply** and show the actual date and time of issue. Do not deliver goods today and create the invoice at month-end. For services supplied in stages, reconcile the contract and billing milestones with the VAT time-of-supply rules.

Whether payment arrives in cash, on credit, through a bank, or online is not the sole invoice trigger. The central event is the supply of goods or services.

---

## Required Information

Include these current-rule fields:

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| Form name | Mushak-6.3 tax invoice |
| Invoice number | A separate sequential number by fiscal year |
| Issue time | Actual date and time |
| Supplier | Legal name, address, and BIN |
| Buyer | Name, address, and BIN where applicable for a supply above BDT 25,000 |
| Supply | Clear description of the good or service |
| Goods details | Quantity, supply date and time, transport type and number where applicable |
| Value excluding VAT | Value before VAT |
| VAT rate | Current rate applicable to the supply |
| VAT | Amount in money |
| Total | Value excluding VAT plus VAT |

A company stamp or signature may form part of internal control, but it is not presented here as a universal statutory field because it is absent from this current-rule list. Follow any separate sector order or buyer-document requirement that applies.

## A Calculation Example

Assume that 15% really applies to a service priced at BDT 100,000:

```text
Value excluding VAT = BDT 100,000
VAT = 100,000 × 15% = BDT 15,000
Total including VAT = 100,000 + 15,000 = BDT 115,000
```

Show all three figures. Do not stop at “BDT 115,000 including VAT.” Do not assume every service carries 15% either. Check the exact service code, exemption, and applicable rate.

If the contract price of BDT 115,000 is VAT-inclusive, adding VAT again would bill BDT 130,000. State whether the contract price is VAT-exclusive or VAT-inclusive before signing.

## Serial and Copy Rules

- Use a sequential number for each fiscal year, such as `2026-27/0001`.
- Keep separate serials for each place of supply and show that place's name and address.
- Make at least two copies. Give the original to the buyer and keep a copy.
- When goods are transported, the original invoice accompanies the goods.
- If a number is cancelled, preserve the record and reason instead of deleting it.

You may use your own software or invoice design if it includes the Mushak-6.3 form name, all prescribed information, and the minimum copies. A polished PDF cannot omit the legal fields.

---

## The BDT 25,000 Buyer-Information Rule

The current Rules request the buyer's name, address, and BIN, where applicable, for a supply above BDT 25,000. In practice:

- Obtain the correct BIN for a BDT 80,000 B2B service to a registered buyer
- Do not invent a BIN for every BDT 5,000 retail customer
- Do not omit the supplier's own BIN merely because the buyer is unregistered

Before copying the buyer's BIN, match the name against NBR's search. One wrong digit can prevent the buyer's input-tax record from reconciling with your sale.

## The Record Path from Mushak-6.3 to the Return

| Form | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Mushak-6.1 | Registered person's purchase account |
| Mushak-6.2 | Registered person's sales account |
| Mushak-6.2.1 | Combined purchase-sales account for goods resold without processing |
| Mushak-6.3 | Fully registered person's VAT invoice |
| Mushak-6.5 | Transfer invoice for goods moved between branches or warehouses |
| Mushak-6.6 | VAT withholding certificate |
| Mushak-6.7 / 6.8 | Credit note / debit note |
| Mushak-6.9 | Enlisted person's turnover-tax invoice |
| Mushak-6.10 | Purchase-sales information for invoices above BDT 200,000 |
| Mushak-9.1 | Monthly VAT return |

The invoice, sales account, bank receipt, and Mushak-9.1 should tell the same story. Reconcile weekly instead of trying to identify every mismatch at month-end.

---

## Correct an Issued Invoice Without Deleting It

If price, quantity, or tax changes after issue, do not delete the old PDF and reuse a new number. Depending on the event, use Mushak-6.7 credit note or Mushak-6.8 debit note. Reconcile the correct tax period with the bookkeeper.

For an information error such as a wrong BIN or description, obtain the relevant VAT circle's current correction procedure. Merely emailing a replacement PDF leaves two versions without a correction trail.

---

## Keep the Records for Five Years

Section 107 of the VAT Act requires accounts, invoices, returns, and other economic-activity records to be kept for five years. Keep serial registers, cancelled invoices, credit and debit notes, Mushak-6.6 certificates, and payment evidence alongside the PDFs.

Example folder structure:

```text
2026-27/
  sales-mushak-6.3/
  purchase-invoices/
  withholding-mushak-6.6/
  credit-debit-notes/
  mushak-9.1-and-payments/
```

## Common Mistakes

- Issuing Mushak-6.3 while enlisted for turnover tax
- Failing to separate value excluding VAT, VAT, and total
- Requiring a buyer BIN on every sale or missing the BDT 25,000 rule
- Failing to number by fiscal year
- Using one serial across two places of supply
- Deleting an incorrect invoice without a credit or debit note trail
- Omitting Mushak-6.10 information for an invoice above BDT 200,000
- Destroying records before five years

## Checklist

- [ ] Mushak-2.3 confirms full VAT registration
- [ ] Issue an invoice at the actual time of each supply
- [ ] Supplier name, address, and BIN are correct
- [ ] Applicable buyer information appears above BDT 25,000
- [ ] Value excluding VAT, rate, VAT, and total are separate
- [ ] Serials are correct by fiscal year and place
- [ ] Buyer original and supplier copy are retained
- [ ] Reconcile with 6.1, 6.2, and 9.1 regularly
- [ ] Five-year record retention is in place

Rule of thumb: **An invoice should answer four questions in one minute: what was supplied, what was the price before VAT, what was the rate, and what was the total?**
