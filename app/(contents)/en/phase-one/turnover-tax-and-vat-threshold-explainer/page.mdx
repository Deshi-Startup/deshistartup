# BDT 3 Million or 8 Million: Turnover Tax and VAT Thresholds

> **Key Takeaway:** As checked against NBR's current information on 10 July 2026, turnover-tax enlistment generally starts when 12-month turnover exceeds BDT 3 million. Full VAT registration generally starts above BDT 8 million. Turnover tax in the middle band is 3%. That is not the complete test. NBR also directs some classes of goods, services, and businesses to register regardless of turnover. Check the sales figure first, then the exact business classification.

Suppose monthly sales are BDT 400,000. Saying “annual sales are BDT 4.8 million, so there is no VAT” is only half an answer. The business is below the BDT 8 million full-registration threshold, but it is above the BDT 3 million enlistment threshold. It may therefore owe turnover tax. If it is in a special mandatory class, full registration may apply instead.

---

## The Three Current Bands

| Turnover over 12 months | General rule | Tax treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Up to BDT 3 million | No enlistment or registration merely because of turnover | Check special classes and voluntary registration separately |
| Above BDT 3 million and up to BDT 8 million | Enlist for turnover tax | 3% of turnover |
| Above BDT 8 million | Full VAT registration | Applicable VAT rate for the supply |

NBR's current FAQ shows the BDT 3 million and BDT 8 million thresholds. Section 63 of the VAT Act sets turnover tax at 3%. The standard VAT rate is 15%, but do not put 15% on every invoice without classifying the supply. Exemption, zero-rating, a specific rate, or a special method may apply.

These figures were checked on 10 July 2026. A budget, Act, SRO, or general order can change them. If this page's `verified` date is old, re-check NBR.

---

## Twelve Months Does Not Mean One Fiscal Year

The threshold uses a **rolling 12-month period**. At each month-end, calculate the preceding 12 months. For full registration, a reasonable estimate of the succeeding 12 months also matters. Section 4 of the VAT Act refers to both past turnover and estimated future turnover.

Suppose the 12-month total at the end of July 2026 is:

```text
Current rolling sales = BDT 7.6 million
Old month leaving the window in August = BDT 300,000
Confirmed August orders = BDT 1 million
New rolling total = 7.6m - 0.3m + 1m = BDT 8.3 million
```

The business is likely to cross BDT 8 million in August. Verify the registration decision and prepare the records now instead of waiting for month-end.

## What Goes into Turnover?

Start with all goods and services supplied to customers, recorded by month. Cash, bank transfers, bKash, COD, and credit invoices are different payment channels for the same business. Do not calculate turnover from bank deposits alone.

The registration-threshold definition excludes certain amounts, including:

- Exempt supplies
- Sale of a capital asset used by the business
- Sale of the whole economic activity or part of it
- A supply caused by permanently closing the activity

Keep the reason and evidence before excluding an amount. Selling an old company laptop is not the same as selling laptops as stock.

## A 3% Turnover-Tax Example

Suppose an enlisted business has BDT 1.2 million of turnover in a tax quarter. A simple calculation is:

```text
BDT 1,200,000 × 3% = BDT 36,000
```

This is 3% of turnover, not 3% of profit. Even if the business makes only BDT 100,000 of profit on BDT 1.2 million in sales, the simple turnover-tax example remains BDT 36,000.

The turnover-tax period is quarterly. After enlistment, reconcile the current return and record requirements against the VAT Rules and portal.

---

## Above BDT 8 Million, 15% Is Not Necessarily the Cash Cost

The standard VAT rate is 15%, but the amount payable may not be simply `sales × 15%`. Eligible input-tax credits, VAT withheld at source, exemptions, and sector-specific rates can change the calculation.

For example, if output VAT is BDT 150,000 and eligible input VAT is BDT 90,000, the initial difference is:

```text
BDT 150,000 - BDT 90,000 = BDT 60,000
```

Do not finalise a return from this example. Input-tax credits have invoice, timing, and eligibility conditions. The example only shows the structural difference between turnover tax and full VAT.

---

## A Special Class Can Override the Threshold

NBR General Order 17/VAT/2019, amended by 02/VAT/2023, directs specified businesses to register regardless of turnover. The list uses H.S. codes, service classifications, location, and type of activity. “I build software” or “I import” is not enough to classify the supply. Match the actual code and activity.

Section 4 of the VAT Act also creates a separate requirement for manufacturing goods or supplying services subject to supplementary duty. Use [When VAT Registration Is Required](../phase-one/when-vat-registration-is-required) for the decision process.

## Make This Decision Every Month

1. Combine sales from every channel and calculate the preceding 12 months.
2. Make a reasonable succeeding-12-month estimate from confirmed contracts and orders.
3. Above BDT 3 million, check turnover-tax enlistment.
4. Above BDT 8 million, or with evidence that it will soon be exceeded, check full registration.
5. Even below the thresholds, compare the goods, service, and code with NBR's special list.
6. If the threshold or classification is uncertain, obtain a written view from a VAT professional or the relevant commissionerate.

## Common Mistakes

- Using the fiscal year instead of a rolling 12-month period
- Ignoring COD, cash, or credit sales because they are absent from one bank account
- Doing nothing above BDT 3 million merely because BDT 8 million has not been reached
- Calculating turnover tax on profit
- Confusing turnover-tax provisions in income-tax law with turnover tax under the VAT Act
- Relying on an old BDT 5 million threshold or another stale figure
- Ignoring the special mandatory-registration list

## Checklist

- [ ] Maintain monthly sales records
- [ ] Calculated the preceding and succeeding 12-month positions
- [ ] Know where the business sits against BDT 3 million and BDT 8 million
- [ ] Kept evidence for every claimed exclusion
- [ ] Matched the goods or service to the correct classification and code
- [ ] Checked NBR's current FAQ, Act, and general orders
- [ ] Scheduled the next monthly review

Rule of thumb: **Review VAT thresholds every month on a rolling 12-month basis, not once a year.**
