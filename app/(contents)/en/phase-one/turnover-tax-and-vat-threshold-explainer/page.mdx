# Turnover Tax and VAT Threshold Explainer

> **Key Takeaway:** Bangladesh's VAT system splits businesses into three tiers by annual turnover: below a lower threshold there is historically no obligation (though this is narrowing), a middle band requires enlisting for a simplified turnover tax instead of full VAT, and crossing the top threshold makes full VAT (BIN) registration mandatory. The figures in this guide reflect the latest information available under the FY2026-27 budget and the Finance Act, 2026, but these thresholds and rates change with every year's budget. Always verify the current figures on NBR's website, or with your accountant or tax advisor, before making a registration decision.

Many founders treat "VAT" and "turnover tax" as the same thing, or assume that low turnover means no relationship with NBR at all. Under the VAT and Supplementary Duty Act, 2012, turnover actually places a business into one of three distinct positions, each with its own rules, rate, and filing obligations.

## 1. The Three-Tier Structure

Under the VAT and Supplementary Duty Act, 2012, there are three possible positions based on annual turnover:

1. **The lower threshold:** Below this, businesses have historically had no VAT-related obligation at all (full exemption).
2. **The middle band (enlistment / turnover tax):** Within this range, a business must enlist for a simplified "turnover tax" scheme instead of registering for full VAT (no input tax credit, but simpler bookkeeping and filing).
3. **The upper threshold (full VAT registration):** Above this, full VAT registration (a Business Identification Number, or BIN) is mandatory, and VAT is payable at the standard rate.

For the step-by-step BIN registration process, see [VAT/BIN Guide](../phase-one/vatbin-guide). This guide covers only the Tk figures and rates, not the registration paperwork or steps.

## 2. Current Thresholds (as of FY2026-27)

According to a professional analysis of Finance Bill 2026 published by KPMG Bangladesh (Rahman Rahman Huq), which cites Section 2(48) of the VAT and Supplementary Duty Act, the current turnover-based thresholds (unchanged by the Finance Act, 2026) are:

| Annual Turnover | Obligation |
| --- | --- |
| Up to Tk 30 lakh | Before the Finance Act, 2026 there was no enlistment obligation; under the Finance Act, 2026, enlistment (turnover tax registration) is now mandatory even below this level |
| Above Tk 30 lakh, up to Tk 50 lakh | Enlistment mandatory, turnover tax applies (not full VAT) |
| Above Tk 50 lakh | Full VAT registration (BIN) mandatory, VAT applies at the standard rate |

**Important caution:** At the time of writing (July 2026), some online sources, particularly personal blogs and older articles, state the mandatory VAT registration threshold as Tk 80 lakh and the turnover-tax band as Tk 30 lakh to Tk 80 lakh. These figures appear to be confused with an earlier 2025 proposal or stale data, and they do not match the recent (June 2026) analysis from a professional accounting firm. Because sources genuinely conflict on the exact current figure, verify the current threshold on NBR's official website, recent SROs/circulars, or with an accountant or tax advisor before making a registration decision for your business.

## 3. The Standard VAT Rate

Under the VAT and Supplementary Duty Act, 2012, the standard VAT rate in Bangladesh is **15%**. This rate applies to most imports and supplies, except goods and services exempted under the First Schedule and various sector-specific reduced rates (for example 5%, 7.5%, or 10% in some cases). This rate has held steady for several years and remains unchanged under the Finance Act, 2026, though sector-specific exemptions and reduced rates can change with every budget. Always check the relevant SRO to confirm whether a reduced rate or exemption applies to your specific sector or product.

## 4. The Turnover Tax Rate: From a Flat Percentage to a Fixed Amount

For the past several years, businesses in the turnover-tax band (the middle row of the table above) generally paid turnover tax at a flat rate of **4%** of turnover.

The Finance Act, 2026 changes this structure. Turnover tax is no longer a fixed percentage of turnover; instead, it is a fixed Taka amount set by NBR based on the nature of the business, sector, and region, capped at a maximum of **Tk 2 lakh**. This means two different types of businesses in the same turnover band could owe different fixed amounts, to be specified through NBR notifications or SROs.

In practice, this means assuming "turnover tax means 4%" will likely be wrong from FY2026-27 onward. Confirm the exact fixed amount for your specific sector through NBR's latest notifications or with your accountant.

## 5. Mandatory Registration Regardless of Turnover

The thresholds above are the general rule based on turnover alone. Certain business categories (for example, importers/exporters, e-commerce platforms, telecom companies, banks, and certain large service providers) are legally required to register for VAT regardless of how low their turnover is. The detailed list and reasoning behind this category-based obligation is outside the scope of this guide; see [When VAT Registration is Required](../phase-one/when-vat-registration-is-required) for the full picture.

Separately, "startup sandbox" entities as defined under the Income Tax Act, 2023 (businesses built around innovation or technology-driven new products, services, or processes, and registered under the VAT Act, 2012) may qualify for special benefits from 1 July 2026 to 30 June 2035, including full exemption from turnover tax and VAT exemption on local supplies. Confirm eligibility through NBR's latest notifications or with a tax advisor.

## 6. Why These Thresholds Change Every Year

Under the VAT and Supplementary Duty Act framework, these thresholds, rates, and bands are revised through the national budget and Finance Act every year. As this guide was being written, the Finance Act, 2026 (passed July 2026) overhauled the entire turnover tax rate structure and extended the enlistment obligation to lower turnover levels than before. No figure in this guide should be treated as permanent.

To track this kind of change after each year's budget, see [Regulatory Changelog for Budget, NBR Circulars, and RJSC Notices](../phase-one/regulatory-changelog-for-budget-nbr-circulars-rjsc-notices) and [How to Update the Budget Changelog Every June](../phase-one/how-to-update-the-budget-changelog-every-june).

## Common Mistakes

- Relying on an old blog post or a years-old Facebook post for the turnover tax and VAT registration thresholds, when the numbers are no longer valid after a budget change.
- Assuming that being in the turnover-tax band means "no VAT obligation at all," and skipping both VAT registration and enlistment; enlistment itself is a mandatory obligation, not optional.
- Still calculating turnover tax as a flat 4%, when the Finance Act, 2026 replaced this with a business-specific fixed Taka amount.
- Waiting until turnover has already crossed a threshold before registering, which risks fines or interest for late registration.
