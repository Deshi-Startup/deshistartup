# When Full VAT Registration or Turnover Tax Applies

> **Key Takeaway:** Full VAT registration generally applies when turnover over 12 months exceeds BDT 8 million. Turnover-tax enlistment generally applies above BDT 3 million and up to BDT 8 million. NBR's special list can require full registration even at lower turnover. Import, export, and tender rules require the use of a BIN, but that does not make full VAT registration the only possible route in every case. Match the activity, code, and turnover first.

Suppose two businesses each sell BDT 4 million a year. One provides an ordinary service. The other manufactures goods included in NBR's registration-regardless-of-turnover list. The first may fall under turnover-tax enlistment. The second may require full registration. Equal sales do not produce equal answers.

---

## The Five Decision Gates

### Gate 1: Do You Make Only Exempt Supplies?

If every good or service is exempt under the VAT Act and current SROs, do not impose tax merely because turnover is high. Exemption depends on the H.S. or service code, stage of supply, and conditions. One supply can be exempt while another supply by the same business is taxable.

Document the position:

| Supply | Code | Exemption source | Conditions met? |
|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |

Do not rely on an exemption without a source.

### Gate 2: Are You in a Special Mandatory Class?

Section 4 of the VAT Act requires registration regardless of turnover for manufacturing goods or supplying services subject to supplementary duty. NBR General Order 17/VAT/2019, amended by 02/VAT/2023, directs additional classes to register regardless of turnover.

The order does not use casual industry names. It uses H.S. codes, service classifications, location, and stage of supply. Match four facts:

1. Good or service
2. Exact code
3. Manufacturing, importing, wholesaling, retailing, or service provision
4. Business location

If the class clearly applies, prepare for full VAT registration. If it is uncertain, obtain a written view from the relevant VAT commissionerate or a VAT professional.

### Gate 3: Has Turnover Exceeded BDT 8 Million?

Check full registration when the preceding rolling 12 months, or a reasonable succeeding-12-month estimate, exceeds BDT 8 million. This is not merely the July-to-June fiscal year. Review the rolling total monthly.

See [Turnover Tax and VAT Thresholds](../phase-one/turnover-tax-and-vat-threshold-explainer) for a calculation template.

### Gate 4: Has Turnover Exceeded BDT 3 Million?

If no special full-registration class applies and 12-month turnover exceeds BDT 3 million but does not exceed BDT 8 million, check turnover-tax enlistment. The current rate is 3%.

A fully registered person issues a Mushak-6.3 tax invoice. An enlisted turnover taxpayer issues a Mushak-6.9 turnover-tax invoice. Tell the client which document you can issue.

### Gate 5: Must a BIN Be Used for the Transaction?

Rule 11 of the VAT Rules requires use of a BIN for specified activities, including:

- Import and export other than baggage imports
- Obtaining an IRC or ERC
- Supplies to a withholding entity
- Participation in a tender
- Bond licensing
- Approval of a bank loan in favour of a registered or enlisted person

The key word is **BIN**. Both a full VAT-registration certificate and a turnover-tax enlistment certificate contain a BIN. Do not conclude that a tender automatically requires full VAT registration. Read the tender conditions, classify the supply, calculate turnover, and ask which invoice the buyer needs.

## Should You Register Voluntarily?

Section 8 of the VAT Act allows a person making taxable supplies to seek voluntary registration. Write down both the benefit and the work before applying.

**Possible reasons:**

- A major buyer requires a Mushak-6.3 tax invoice
- Eligible input-tax credits are commercially important
- Strong evidence shows turnover will soon exceed BDT 8 million

**Obligations that follow:**

- Monthly VAT returns
- Correct tax invoice for every supply
- Purchase, sales, and input records
- Reconciliation of VAT withheld and input-tax credits
- A requirement to remain registered for at least one year after voluntary registration

Do not register merely because a buyer said “send the BIN.” Ask whether it needs Mushak-6.3, any BIN, or a generic vendor-onboarding document.

## Three Examples

### Small Software Agency

Preceding-12-month sales are BDT 2.4 million, and the service code does not match a special mandatory class. A corporate buyer asks for a BIN only as an identifier. Obtain the requirement in writing. Voluntary full registration creates monthly return work.

### Facebook-First Brand

COD, bKash, and bank sales total BDT 4.2 million over 12 months. Looking only at BDT 2.5 million in one bank account understates turnover. Check turnover-tax enlistment and prepare for Mushak-6.9 invoices and quarterly turnover tax if enlisted.

### Fast-Growing SaaS Company

Preceding-12-month sales are BDT 6.5 million, while signed annual contracts support a succeeding-12-month estimate of BDT 9.2 million. The Act considers estimated turnover. Review full registration now and build VAT into pricing before crossing the threshold.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating every import or tender as requiring full VAT registration
- Comparing an industry name instead of the exact code with the special list
- Confusing turnover-tax enlistment with full registration
- Promising Mushak-6.3 when the business can issue only Mushak-6.9
- Calculating turnover from one bank account
- Registering voluntarily without assigning the monthly compliance work
- Claiming an exemption without keeping the SRO and conditions

## Checklist

- [ ] Listed every good and service code
- [ ] Kept the source and conditions for any exemption
- [ ] Checked the special registration order and amendments
- [ ] Calculated preceding and succeeding 12-month turnover
- [ ] Understand the difference between BDT 3 million enlistment and BDT 8 million registration
- [ ] Asked why the counterparty needs a BIN
- [ ] Assigned monthly work before voluntary registration
- [ ] Recorded the decision and next review date

Rule of thumb: **Check the code, then turnover, then the buyer's invoice requirement. Do not decide VAT status without all three.**
