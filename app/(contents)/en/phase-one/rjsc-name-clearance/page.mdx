# How to Get RJSC Name Clearance

> **Key Takeaway:** The first formal step in registering a private limited company is getting Name Clearance from RJSC (Registrar of Joint Stock Companies and Firms). Getting this wrong, a name that gets stuck or has to be changed later, delays everything that follows: the MoA/AoA, bank account, trade license, and branding.

Name clearance means RJSC has confirmed your proposed name doesn't already clash with a registered entity and is usable. It is not final approval of company registration, just clearance to use that name.

## 1. Naming Rules

RJSC rejects applications outright for several common reasons:

- The name is identical, or too close in spelling or pronunciation, to an already-registered company.
- The name resembles a well-known international company or a long-running local social, cultural, or business organization.
- The name is misleading, politically sensitive, or offensive.
- The name uses words like "Bangladesh," "Bank," "Insurance," or "Government." These require separate permission from the relevant authority.
- The name doesn't carry the correct suffix for the company type. A private limited company's name must end in "Private Limited" or its Bangla equivalent.

**Practical tip:** Clearing RJSC isn't enough on its own. Check the name is also available as a domain (.com, .com.bd) and social media handle, and won't create a future trademark conflict. See [Trademark Search and Filing Guide](../phase-one/trademark-search-and-filing-guide) if relevant.

## 2. The Application Process

1. Create an account on RJSC's online portal and go to the Name Clearance system.
2. Search RJSC's database to check your proposed name isn't already taken.
3. Submit the application and pay the required fee online.
4. Approval or rejection is usually communicated within 1-2 working days.
5. If rejected, you'll need to reapply with an alternative, so have 3-5 strong backup names ready from the start.

Fees and processing timelines change from time to time, so check the current fee and instructions on the [RJSC portal](https://roc.gov.bd) right before applying.

## 3. Validity After Approval

Once your name is cleared, you must complete the next steps of registration (submitting the MoA/AoA, paying the registration fee) within a set window. If that window lapses, the clearance can expire and you may need to reapply. This window has changed over time, so read the validity period stated on your approval notice carefully and put the deadline on your calendar.

## Common Mistakes

- Applying with a name you simply like, without searching RJSC's database first.
- Applying with only one name option. If it's rejected, you start the whole process over.
- Finalizing a name without checking domain or social handle availability, then running into branding problems later.
- Forgetting the clearance's validity window and missing the deadline to register.

## Checklist

- [ ] 3-5 backup names checked against RJSC's database
- [ ] Proposed name avoids restricted/permission-only words (Bangladesh, Bank, Insurance, etc.)
- [ ] Correct suffix added for the company type (Private Limited, etc.)
- [ ] Name checked for domain and social media handle availability
- [ ] Approval validity deadline noted on the calendar
