# How to Get RJSC Name Clearance for a Company

> **In short:** Before registering a private company, apply for “Name Clearance” through the RJSC portal. One application may propose 1 to 10 names, but RJSC clears only one. On 10 July 2026, the official fee page listed BDT 500 for each proposed company name. Clearance remains valid for 30 days from issue. Search company names, domains, and trademarks before applying, then put the expiry date in your calendar as soon as the letter arrives.

RJSC is the Office of the Registrar of Joint Stock Companies and Firms, the government office responsible for registering companies and several other entity types. The portal calls this service “Name Clearance,” often shortened to “NC.”

Clearance does not create the company. RJSC is confirming that the proposed name does not closely resemble a name already registered or booked for the same entity type. The company exists after registration and issue of the Certificate of Incorporation.

---

## Search in Three Places Before Choosing

Suppose Sumiya wants to name her software company “Nirapod Hisab.” The Facebook page name is available, so she applies immediately. RJSC may still reject it if a close entity name is already on record. RJSC may clear it while an existing trademark creates a later brand dispute.

Run three separate searches:

1. **RJSC entity-name search:** look for registered, booked, or pending names under the same entity type
2. **Domain and social handles:** check the places where customers will find you
3. **Preliminary trademark search:** look for similar brands in the same field

RJSC clearance does not grant trademark ownership. Buying a domain does not reserve the company name. These are separate doors.

---

## Build a Short List You Would Actually Use

RJSC's [name-clearance process](https://app1.roc.gov.bd/help/rjsc_bus_pro_bri_nameclearance.htm) allows a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 10 names in one application. Only one is cleared. Each proposed name carries a separate fee, so do not submit ten merely to fill the form.

Prepare three to five names you would genuinely accept. Check whether each one:

- is easy to pronounce and spell
- closely resembles a company of the same type
- wrongly suggests a government, bank, insurer, or regulated institution
- becomes misleading if the business later changes
- has settled Bangla and English spellings

Use the same legal suffix in the portal and prepared documents. Do not assume that the words “Private Limited” are automatically the required suffix. Have the professional preparing the documents align the name with the current Companies Act and RJSC instructions.

## Application Steps

Button labels may change, but the work remains:

1. Sign in to the [RJSC online service](https://app1.roc.gov.bd/psp/rjschome)
2. Use “Search Entity Names” to find close names
3. Open the “Name Clearance” application and choose the entity type
4. Enter only names you would accept, in preferred order
5. Submit and pay the fee shown by the portal
6. Watch the status and answer any query
7. Download the Name Clearance Letter after approval

Keep the application number and proof of payment in a separate folder. A screenshot is not enough. The downloaded clearance letter is the evidence that this step is complete.

---

## Fee and Validity

RJSC's [current fee page](https://roc.gov.bd/site/page/f257a30a-dec7-438f-a790-80f1e4f00fa5/) lists BDT 500 for each proposed company name. Three names therefore cost:

```text
3 × BDT 500 = BDT 1,500
```

Fees can change. Before paying, match this amount against the challan generated by the portal. If RJSC issues a new instruction after the `verified` date above, follow the portal.

RJSC's process page says clearance is valid for 30 days from issue. Upon application, validity may first extend to 60 days and then to a maximum of 90 days from the original issue. The current fee page lists BDT 200 for each extension application.

The practical meaning is simple: begin preparing registration documents as soon as the name is cleared. Do not wait until the final week. After expiry, do not assume the name remains booked for you.

---

## What to Check When the Application Stops

RJSC's core test is whether the proposed name closely matches a registered, booked, or pending name of the same entity type. A small spelling change does not necessarily create a distinct name. Consider both pronunciation and appearance.

Read the query before taking another action. If similarity is the issue, use another genuine option. If written approval is requested for a special word, do not force the application without it. Contact RJSC before paying repeatedly when the reason remains unclear.
