# Share Capital, Directors, and Shareholders: Reconcile the Numbers

> **Key Takeaway:** Authorised capital is the ceiling for share issues. Issued capital is the shares the company has allotted. Paid-up capital is what the company has actually received against those shares. Directors manage the company, while shareholders own its shares. One person can hold both roles, but the votes and records are different. A new allotment increases total shares. A transfer changes the owner of existing shares.

Suppose Sumiya has 600 shares and Rafi has 400. The company later issues 250 new shares to an investor. Sumiya still has 600 shares, but her ownership falls from 60% to:

```text
600 ÷ 1,250 = 48%
```

No shares were taken from her. Her percentage fell because the total increased. This is dilution.

---

## Four Capital Boxes

| Term | Plain meaning | What it is not |
|---|---|---|
| **Authorised capital** | Maximum nominal value of shares permitted by the MoA | Cash in the bank |
| **Issued capital** | Nominal value of shares actually allotted | Necessarily equal to the authorised amount |
| **Called-up capital** | Amount requested so far against issued shares | An uncalled balance |
| **Paid-up capital** | Amount actually received from shareholders | Every deposit made by a founder |

Suppose authorised capital is BDT 1 million and each share has a BDT 10 face value. The ceiling is:

```text
BDT 1,000,000 ÷ 10 = 100,000 shares
```

If the company initially issues 20,000 shares and collects the full BDT 10 per share:

```text
Issued capital = 20,000 × 10 = BDT 200,000
Paid-up capital = BDT 200,000
Unused authorised capacity = BDT 800,000
```

Writing BDT 1 million of authorised capital does not require depositing BDT 1 million. Conversely, a BDT 200,000 founder deposit is not automatically paid-up capital. The allotment, member register, receipt, and filing must classify it consistently. Otherwise, it may be a founder loan.

## How Much Authorised Capital?

Leave room for foreseeable share issues, but do not choose a very large figure merely for appearance. Some RJSC registration fees and stamps vary with authorised capital. Increasing it later can require a resolution, MoA change, and RJSC filing.

The Companies Act does not state one general minimum paid-up amount for every ordinary company. A bank, regulated sector, licence, foreign investment, or foreign-employee permission may impose a separate condition. Do not present such a condition as the Companies Act's universal minimum. Check the current policy of the authority that applies.

## Directors and Shareholders Have Different Jobs

| Role | Function | Typical vote |
|---|---|---|
| **Director** | Manages the company and makes board decisions | Board meeting |
| **Shareholder** | Holds ownership and makes member decisions | General meeting or written member resolution |

A founder can be both a director and shareholder. Yet “I own 60%, so I decide everything at the board” is not necessarily true. Read the AoA rules for board quorum, voting, reserved matters, and appointments.

A private company needs at least two directors. RJSC's official FAQ says Form XII for a new director or changed particulars is filed within 14 days. Form IX, the director's consent, is filed within 30 days of appointment. Keep the decision date, consent, board or member resolution, and forms together.

---

## The 2-to-50 Member Limit

At least two people form a private company. The Companies Act definition caps members at 50, with exclusions for certain employee members and former employees who remained members. Joint holders of one or more shares count as one member for this purpose.

An ESOP gives employees an option to acquire shares in future. Granting an option is not the same as allotting the shares. Recalculate the member count after an allotment places a name in the register of members.

---

## What Changes in a New Allotment?

In an allotment, the company creates and gives new shares. This can:

- Increase total shares
- Dilute existing percentages
- Bring money into the company
- Use authorised capacity
- Require approvals under the AoA and earlier agreements
- Require Form XV at RJSC

RJSC's official FAQ says Form XV is filed within 60 days of the allotment. Do not treat day 60 as the day to begin. Prepare the approval, evidence of funds, share count, member register, and certificates from the allotment date.

### Dilution Table

| Owner | Before | After new issue | New percentage |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Sumiya | 600 | 600 | 48% |
| Rafi | 400 | 400 | 32% |
| Investor | 0 | 250 | 20% |
| **Total** | **1,000** | **1,250** | **100%** |

Do not derive 250 shares merely from “20%” in a term sheet. The number changes depending on whether the percentage is pre-money or post-money and whether an option pool is created before or after the investment.

---

## What Changes in a Transfer?

A transfer does not create shares. One holder transfers existing shares to another. If Rafi sells 100 shares to Sumiya, the total remains 1,000. The company does not receive the sale price unless there is a separate transaction.

Before a transfer, check:

1. Whether the AoA requires a first offer to other members
2. Whether board approval is required
3. Whether Form 117, the instrument of transfer, is complete
4. Whether current stamp duty has been paid
5. Whether the member register and share certificates have been updated
6. Whether the agreement and payment evidence are retained

Saying “I gave away the shares” does not change the register of members.

---

## Maintain a Cap Table

A capitalisation table records who owns how many shares. Keep at least:

| Name | Share class | Shares | Face value | Paid-up | Ownership % | Certificate |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
|  |  |  |  |  |  |  |

After every allotment or transfer, reconcile the cap table, member register, certificates, bank records, and RJSC form on the same day. A 60:40 split in one record and 50:50 in another will surface during the next investment.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating authorised capital as cash in the bank
- Recording a founder deposit as paid-up capital without an allotment decision
- Treating director and shareholder votes as the same
- Failing to calculate dilution before a new issue
- Missing the 60-day Form XV filing period
- Increasing total shares during a transfer
- Omitting Form 117, stamp, member register, or certificate work
- Treating an ESOP option as an allotted share

## Checklist

- [ ] Know the authorised, issued, and paid-up figures separately
- [ ] Share money reconciles with bank and company records
- [ ] Separated director and shareholder decision rules
- [ ] Checked the two-director and 2-to-50-member rules
- [ ] Prepared a dilution table before a new issue
- [ ] Calendar Form XV within 60 days of allotment
- [ ] Reconciled the AoA, Form 117, stamp, and register for a transfer
- [ ] Cap table and RJSC records show the same figures

Rule of thumb: **Keep the share numbers identical in four places: cap table, member register, bank records, and RJSC filings.**
