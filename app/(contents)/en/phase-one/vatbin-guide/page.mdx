# VAT/BIN Guide

> **Key Takeaway:** A BIN (Business Identification Number) is your business's identity card in the VAT system, issued through VAT registration under NBR's VAT and Supplementary Duty Act, 2012. The whole process now runs online on the vat.gov.bd portal, free of charge, but missing or inaccurate documents get the application stuck in NBR's query process. This guide only covers "how to register"; whether registration is mandatory for you, or the exact taka threshold, is covered in separate guides.

BIN and e-TIN are two completely different registrations, so it's normal for the same company to hold two separate identity numbers. e-TIN is issued by NBR's income tax wing for income tax purposes, while BIN is issued by NBR's VAT wing for VAT purposes. Most businesses need both, and you typically need an e-TIN before you can apply for a BIN. How to get an e-TIN itself isn't covered here, see the [e-TIN Guide](../phase-one/e-tin-guide) and [Personal e-TIN vs Company TIN](../phase-one/personal-e-tin-vs-company-tin).

## 1. What VAT and BIN Actually Are

Under the VAT and Supplementary Duty Act, 2012, VAT is charged at every stage of supplying goods or services, and every registered business is given a unique identity number for collecting and remitting VAT, called a BIN. It's now a 13-digit e-BIN, issued only through NBR's online VAT system (iVAS, the Integrated VAT Administration System). Holding a BIN lets a business bid on tenders, carry out certain banking and import/export activities, and legally collect VAT and remit it to NBR.

Whether your business actually needs to register for VAT (the turnover-based obligation, or whether you fall under turnover tax instead) isn't decided here. See [When VAT Registration Is Required](../phase-one/when-vat-registration-is-required) for that decision logic, and [Turnover Tax and VAT Threshold Explainer](../phase-one/turnover-tax-and-vat-threshold-explainer) for the actual taka figures.

## 2. What to Have Ready Before You Apply

Before applying for a BIN, have this documentation and information ready:

- A valid e-TIN certificate
- Trade license (current for the running fiscal year)
- NID or passport of the business owner/directors
- Business type, address, and details of the main activities
- An estimate of annual turnover
- Bank account details
- Copy of the IRC/ERC if you import or export
- A list of all branches or sales outlets with their addresses, if you have more than one (needed for central registration)

If you're a company, you'll also need the RJSC Certificate of Incorporation. If you haven't registered the company yet, see [Step-by-Step Private Limited Company Registration](../phase-one/private-limited-company-registration-step-by-step) first.

## 3. Creating an Account on vat.gov.bd

Go to NBR's VAT Online portal (vat.gov.bd) and create a new taxpayer account with your mobile number and email, verified via OTP. Once sign-up succeeds, a username and password are sent by email, which you then use to log in and start the application.

## 4. Filling Out Mushak 2.1 and Uploading Documents

Log in and go to "BIN Registration" to fill out the electronic application, Mushak-2.1, entering your business type, address, activities, and estimated turnover. Then upload all the scanned documents listed in Step 2 and submit the application. The whole process runs online and carries no government fee.

## 5. NBR Review and Getting the BIN Certificate

The submitted application is reviewed by the relevant VAT circle at NBR. If the documents and information check out, approval usually comes within a few working days, but it can take longer if NBR needs clarification or a site inspection. Once approved, NBR electronically issues the VAT Registration Certificate (Mushak 2.3), which carries the 13-digit BIN, and it can be downloaded from the portal.

## 6. What Changes Once You Have a BIN

Once you have a BIN, the business is legally required to collect VAT and issue a Mushak invoice on every sale. The detailed rules for Mushak invoices aren't covered here, see [Mushak 6.3 Invoice and VAT Documentation Basics](../phase-one/mushak-63-invoice-and-vat-documentation-basics). You also become obligated to file a VAT return every month; the full process for that is in [Monthly Return Workflow After BIN/VAT Registration](../phase-one/monthly-return-workflow-after-binvat-registration).

## Common Mistakes

- Writing vague or inaccurate estimated turnover or business activity details, which triggers NBR's query process.
- Submitting the application with an expired trade license.
- Applying for central registration without listing the addresses of all branches.
- Treating the BIN certificate as the finish line and not preparing for monthly returns or issuing Mushak invoices.
- Confusing BIN and e-TIN as the same number and using the wrong one on an invoice or return.

## Checklist

- [ ] e-TIN certificate in hand
- [ ] Trade license current and scanned
- [ ] NID or passport of owner/directors scanned and ready
- [ ] Estimated annual turnover and business activity details written down
- [ ] Address list for all branches prepared, if you have more than one
- [ ] Account created on vat.gov.bd with OTP verification complete
- [ ] A plan in place for monthly returns and Mushak invoices after getting the BIN
