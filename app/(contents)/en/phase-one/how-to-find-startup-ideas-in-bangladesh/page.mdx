# Practical Ways to Find Startup Ideas in Bangladesh

> **Key Takeaway:** A great startup idea doesn't just appear out of nowhere. The key to building a successful startup in Bangladesh is deeply observing real-life problems and using technology to organize unorganized sectors.

If you want to start a startup but don't have any ideas, you can find an effective idea by following the methods below:

## 1. Focus on Unorganized Sectors
The Bangladeshi economy is still largely unorganized or informal. Wherever you see a lack of specific structure for a job, excessive reliance on middlemen, or too much manual labor–that is where a startup opportunity is hidden.
- **Example:** Hiring a truck used to be extremely complicated. 'Truck Lagbe' organized this unorganized sector through an app.
- **Look for:** How technology can bring discipline to areas like agricultural supply chains, local transport, real estate brokerage, or household services (plumbers, cleaners).

## 2. 'Local' Adaptation of Global Models (Copying with Localization)
Many startup models that have succeeded in developed countries have not yet arrived in Bangladesh, or if they have, they haven't been localized appropriately. Instead of simply copying a successful foreign model, adapting it (Localizing) to fit the psychology and infrastructure of the Bangladeshi people can be a brilliant idea.
- **Example:** While Pathao started with the Uber model in Bangladesh, they quickly realized that motorcycles were more effective than cars in Dhaka's traffic. This was their local innovation.
- **Look for:** Which startups are succeeding in developing countries like India, Indonesia, or Latin America that could also work in Bangladesh.

## 3. Identify Inefficiencies in the B2B Sector
Most new entrepreneurs only want to solve problems for general consumers (B2C). However, there are also many problems within large companies or small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs) in Bangladesh, solving which they will happily pay for.
- **Example:** 'ShopUp' became one of the country's largest startups by solving supply chain and logistics problems for small Facebook sellers or grocery store owners.
- **Look for:** What kind of problems are businesses facing when keeping accounts, managing employees, or sourcing raw materials?

## 4. Solve Your Own Problem (Scratch Your Own Itch)
What problem do you face in your daily life or workplace that you are actively trying to solve? If you face a problem, there is a high probability that thousands of other people are suffering from the same issue.
- **Example:** The founders of 'Chaldal' were inspired to create an online grocery delivery platform from the frustration of navigating Dhaka's traffic to buy groceries.
- **Look for:** What was the most annoying task in your day today? Could this be automated with software or a service?

## 5. Capitalize on Changes (Riding Regulatory or Tech Waves)
New business opportunities often arise due to new government regulations or technological shifts.
- **Example:** When the government started encouraging digital payments, it created huge opportunities for fintech companies. Similarly, the advent of AI has created new opportunities for edtech or customer support startups.

## Idea Filtering Checklist
After having a few ideas, ask yourself the following questions to validate them:
- [ ] Does this problem affect many people (at least hundreds of thousands)?
- [ ] Does this problem happen repeatedly (daily/weekly/monthly)?
- [ ] Are people currently spending money or willing to spend money to solve this problem?
- [ ] Do I or my team have the skills to implement this idea?
