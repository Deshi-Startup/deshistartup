# Finding Customers

> **In short:** In Bangladesh, finding customers is not only digital marketing. It means posting in Facebook Groups, having Messenger conversations, talking to people in markets, and building trust. In Bangladesh, selling means earning trust.

---

## How Is Customer Acquisition Different in Bangladesh?

Getting customers in Bangladesh works differently from Silicon Valley:

| Topic | Bangladesh | Rest of the world |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| **Main platform** | Facebook | Google, Instagram, TikTok |
| **Communication** | Messenger, WhatsApp, phone call | Email, chat widget |
| **Trust** | People know you first, then buy | People see the brand, then buy |
| **Payment** | COD, bKash | Credit card |
| **Decision** | Family opinion matters for expensive items | Individual decision |
| **Language** | Bangla | English |

## Facebook-First Commerce

For many people in Bangladesh, Facebook is the internet. So Facebook should come first in your marketing strategy.

### Facebook Page Setup

1. **Create a professional page** in the Business/Brand category.
2. **Fill in complete information** such as phone, address, website, and hours.
3. **Use profile and cover photos** with clear, professional branding.
4. **Write the About section**: what you do, who it is for, and why you are different.
5. **Add a call-to-action button** such as “Send Message,” “Shop Now,” or “Call Now.”

### Facebook Group Strategy

Facebook Groups are extremely powerful in Bangladesh.

**What to do:**

- Join relevant groups in your niche.
- Add value in the group; do not only promote.
- Follow group rules.
- Engage authentically by answering questions.

**What not to do:**

- Do not spam or dump links.
- Do not promote in irrelevant groups.
- Do not use fake reviews or testimonials.

### Facebook Ads

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Minimum budget** | You can start from Tk 100-500 per day |
| **Best format** | Image + caption, video, carousel |
| **Targeting** | Age, location, interest, behavior |
| **Bangla content** | Write in Bangla; it often gets more engagement than English |
| **CTA** | “Send Message” is often the most effective in Bangladesh |

**Ad steps:**

1. **Test phase** – Run 3-5 different ads with a small budget, around Tk 200-500/day.
2. **Analyze** – See which ad gets the most engagement.
3. **Scale** – Increase the budget on the ad that works.
4. **Optimize** – Try to reduce cost per result.

**Tips:**

- **“Send Message” CTA** is highly effective in Bangladesh because people like to message directly.
- **Video content** of 15-30 seconds works well.
- **Customer testimonials** should use real customer reviews or videos.
- **Bangla copy** works well, and you can mix in English where natural.
- **Local context** matters: connect with Eid, Pohela Boishakh, and local events.

## Messenger and WhatsApp as Sales Channels

Many businesses in Bangladesh sell only through Messenger or WhatsApp.

**How to do it:**

1. **Set auto-replies** with basic information.
2. **Create quick replies** for frequently asked questions.
3. **Set up a product catalog** in Messenger.
4. **Confirm orders** by phone.
5. **Follow up** after delivery.

**Tips:**

- The faster your response time, the higher your conversion.
- Use voice messages, because many customers do not want to type.
- Keep the tone professional but friendly.

| Situation | Do you need a website? |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Starting with only Facebook sales | Not at the beginning |
| Want to build a brand | Yes |

## Trust Building

Trust is the biggest barrier in Bangladesh. People are often afraid to buy from new brands.

### Ways to Build Trust

**1. Social Proof**

- Show customer reviews and testimonials.
- Use real photos, not stock photos.
- Show numbers such as “1,000+ satisfied customers.”
- Video testimonials are especially effective.

**2. Transparency**

- Use clear pricing with no hidden charges.
- Clearly write your return/refund policy.
- Share a physical address if you have one.
- Publicly share a phone number.

**3. Professional Presence**

- Keep branding consistent: logo, colors, fonts.
- Use a professional email such as info@yourbrand.com, not only Gmail.
- Keep social media active.
- Post content regularly.

**4. Personal Connection**

- Address customers by name.
- Make phone calls after messages when needed.
- Respond quickly when there is a problem.
- Provide after-sales service.

## Distribution Outside Dhaka

Distribution outside Dhaka is both a major opportunity and a major challenge.

### Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Logistics and delivery take longer | Use reliable courier partners such as RedX, Pathao, or Steadfast |
| Customers want to see the product | Show the product on video call and use detailed photos |
| Trust is lower, including outside Dhaka | Offer COD and build local presence |
| Payment relies heavily on bKash/Nagad | Focus on MFS |
| Language or dialect may differ | Communicate in local language where possible |

### Opportunities

- **Less competition** – Many markets outside Dhaka have less competition.
- **Loyal customers** – Once you earn trust, customers can become loyal.
- **Lower marketing cost** – Word of mouth is more effective.
- **Untapped market** – Many products and services are still limited to Dhaka.

### Distribution Strategy

1. **Courier partners** – RedX, Pathao Courier, Steadfast, SA Paribahan
2. **Local agents/distributors** – Appoint agents in different districts
3. **Facebook Groups** – Post in district-based buy/sell groups
4. **Bikroy.com / OLX** – List on classified platforms
5. **Local events/mela** – Set up stalls in markets and fairs

## Customer Retention

Keeping existing customers is cheaper and more effective than acquiring new ones.

**What to do:**

- [ ] Send a follow-up message or call after delivery.
- [ ] Ask for feedback: what did they like, what can be improved?
- [ ] Create a loyalty program with discounts for repeat customers.
- [ ] Send regular updates about new products or offers.
- [ ] Send birthday or occasion greetings for a personal touch.
- [ ] Solve problems quickly when they happen.

## Pricing Strategy in the Bangladesh Context

Bangladesh is a price-sensitive market. Some useful strategies:

| Strategy | When to use it |
|----------|----------------|
| **Competitive pricing** | When the market has many competitors |
| **Value-based pricing** | When you offer unique value |
| **Freemium** | For SaaS or digital products |
| **Bundle pricing** | When selling multiple products together |
| **Introductory offer** | To attract customers at the beginning |

**Tips:**

- “Tk 999” psychological pricing works.
- Offer free delivery with a minimum order.
- Offer EMI options for large amounts.
- Offer student discounts if students are a major market.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| Content only in English | Write in Bangla and mix English naturally |
| Only posting products | Add value and educate customers |
| Slow message response | Reply as quickly as possible |
| Ignoring customer complaints | Solve quickly and respond publicly |
| Focusing only on Dhaka | There is opportunity across Bangladesh |
| Thinking price is everything | Trust, quality, and service all create value |
