# Bangladesh Startup Ecosystem: Who Does What

> **TL;DR:** A startup depends on customers, payments, logistics, law, tax, talent, funding, government services, communities and trust. Do not approach every door at once. Start with the part that can remove the current constraint.

Some Bangladesh businesses begin through a Facebook page; some technology products first run as a manual service. Different customers may need MFS, bank transfer, cards or cash on delivery. Document mismatches can also delay bank or payment-provider checks. Online product work, field operations and records all matter.

## Main Parts

| Part | Role |
|---|---|
| Founders | Identify a problem, test, take risk and build a team |
| Customers | Provide evidence through use, payment and feedback |
| Government and regulators | Registration, tax, VAT, licenses and policy |
| Investors | Capital, network, governance and growth expectations |
| Incubators and accelerators | Mentoring, training, networks and sometimes capital |
| Communities | Peers, co-founders, hiring leads and practical experience |
| Payments and logistics | Collection, settlement, delivery, returns and records |
| Talent | Engineering, design, operations, sales and finance |
| Media and content | Public information, stories and market context |

At the idea stage, focus on the customer and problem. Once sales start, payments, delivery and documents move forward. Funding preparation adds accounts, company structure, a data room and investor relationships.

## Government and Regulators

| Organisation or service | What it covers |
|---|---|
| RJSC | Company and firm registration and name clearance |
| NBR | TIN, income tax, VAT and returns |
| VAT Online | VAT registration, BIN and VAT services |
| BIDA and OSS | Investment services and selected government processes |
| Bangladesh Bank | Banking, payments, foreign exchange and circulars |
| Local authority | Trade licenses and local permissions |

Use a guide for orientation, then verify the action on the official portal or with a qualified professional.

## Investors and Funding

Funding is one part of a startup, not the business itself. Revenue, grants, debt, angels and venture capital all impose different costs and duties. Write what the money will change before choosing the source.

Investors commonly examine the problem, customer use or payment, team, growth potential, accounts, legal records and use of funds. A deck does not replace that evidence.

## Market, Payments and Delivery

“People are online” is not a market analysis. Learn where the customer spends time, how they pay, where delivery must go, what a return costs and who influences the decision.

Further reading: [Payments](payments), [Finding Customers](customers) and [Cash on Delivery and Delivery Risk](phase-three/cod-and-delivery-risk)

## Communities, Mentors and Talent

Advice depends on context. A mentor experienced in corporate software may not know Facebook commerce or a regulated health product. Give more weight to experience that matches the stage and market.

For a co-founder or early hire, check available time, risk tolerance, customer contact and willingness to put terms in writing, not skill alone.

Further reading: [Finding Co-founders](phase-two/how-to-find-co-founders), [Hiring Early Employees](phase-two/hiring-first-employees) and [Founder Life](founder-life)

## Where to Look for Problems

Do not choose a sector from a trend list. Look for a specific group that repeatedly faces a problem and already spends time or money on it. Areas to investigate include:

- small-business accounts, orders, inventory and payments
- agriculture, supply and food processing
- education, training and skills
- healthcare and supply chains
- logistics, returns and warehousing
- safer participation and work for women
- distribution outside Dhaka
