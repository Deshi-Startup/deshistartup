# Bangladesh Startup Ecosystem Overview

> **In short:** A startup needs more than an idea. It needs an ecosystem: people and institutions that advise you, fund you, understand law and markets, and help you reach customers.

## Main Parts of the Ecosystem

1. **Founders / Entrepreneurs** — Ideas, execution, and risk-taking
2. **Investors** — Angels, VCs, accelerators, grants
3. **Government and regulators** — RJSC, NBR, VAT, city corporations, BEZA
4. **Support organizations** — Incubators, accelerators, coworking spaces
5. **Market** — Customers, suppliers, competitors
6. **Talent** — Developers, designers, marketers
7. **Infrastructure** — Internet, payments, logistics

## Where Can You Get Help?

### Government Programs

- **Startup Bangladesh** — Prototype and development grants
- **BD Venture** — Private equity and investment
- **BASIS** — Training, mentorship, networking
- **BEZA** — IT Park and special economic zone benefits

### Mass Market and Customers

- Facebook Groups, Messenger commerce, online marketplaces
- Corporate procurement or B2B chains
- Local kirana shops and small traders

### Logistics and Payments

- **Payments:** bKash, Nagad, bank transfer, PayPal depending on service type
- **Logistics:** Pathao, RedX, SA Paribahan, Sundarban Courier
- **Infrastructure:** Fiber internet, mobile coverage, electricity

## Advantages for Startups in Bangladesh

- Lower labor cost
- A large Bangla-speaking market
- Growing mobile internet usage
- Government initiatives that provide grants and incentives
- A market with room for fast growth within South Asia

## Biggest Challenges

- **Trust** — Customers are often cautious with new brands.
- **Payments** — Credit card penetration is low, while COD and MFS are common.
- **Regulation** — Registration and compliance can be complex.
- **Logistics** — Last-mile delivery outside Dhaka can be expensive.
- **Talent** — Finding a skilled technical team can be difficult.

## Focus Areas

1. **Consumer internet** — E-commerce, delivery, fintech
2. **Agritech** — Agricultural supply, marketplaces, input access
3. **Healthtech** — Telemedicine, medical supplier networks
4. **Edtech** — Online tuition, training, certification
5. **Small business tools** — Accounting, inventory, order management
