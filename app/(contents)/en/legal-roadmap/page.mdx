# Legal Roadmap – Start to Legal Launch

> **In short:** Once your idea is validated, the next major task is building the legal foundation. This is what makes your business safer and more durable.

## Main Steps

1. **Choose the business type** – Sole proprietorship, partnership, private limited company
2. **RJSC / name clearance** – Register the company or partnership name
3. **Company registration** – Incorporation certificate or partnership registration
4. **TIN / VAT / BIN** – NBR tax ID and VAT registration
5. **Trade License** – From the local city corporation or municipality
6. **Bank account** – Bank account in the company or business name
7. **Contracts / Terms** – Supplier, customer, employee agreements
8. **Annual compliance** – Audit, tax return, license renewal

## Step-by-Step Legal Guide

### Step 1: Choose an Entity

- Start by looking at your growth plan, liability tolerance, and investment goals.
- `Sole proprietorship` is fast to start, but personal liability is higher.
- `Private limited company` is investor-friendly and has limited liability, but compliance is heavier.

### Step 2: Name and Registration

- Get name clearance from RJSC.
- In the next step, register the company or partnership.
- If the name and legal validity are not clear, it can create problems later.

### Step 3: Tax Identity

- Get an e-TIN.
- If you may cross the VAT threshold, get a BIN.
- Start bookkeeping from the beginning.

### Step 4: Trade License

- Choose the city corporation or municipality based on your business address.
- Clarify which license category you need.
- Renewal will be required every year.

### Step 5: Contracts and Proper Documents

- Prepare supplier agreements, customer terms, and employment contracts.
- Note whether you need IP documents, trademark filings, or a privacy policy.
- If needed, work with a local lawyer or consultant.

## Quick Compliance Checklist

- [ ] I have chosen my business entity.
- [ ] I have completed name clearance.
- [ ] I have registered the company or partnership.
- [ ] I have obtained an e-TIN.
- [ ] I have registered for VAT/BIN if applicable.
- [ ] I have obtained a trade license.
- [ ] I have opened a bank account.
- [ ] I have started an accounting system.
- [ ] I have planned yearly renewal and audit work.

## What Should You Do First?

Normal order:

1. Name clearance
2. Registration
3. TIN
4. Trade license
5. Bank account
6. Operational launch

> In some cases in Bangladesh, you can work on multiple steps at the same time. For example, even while name clearance is pending, you may prepare for TIN or trade license work if your documents are ready.

## Important Tips

- If official numbers or processes are unclear, check the relevant authority’s website.
- You may be able to get legal support through NGOs or accelerators.
- Choose the correct trade license category. A wrong category can create renewal or audit problems later.
- Start bookkeeping now. Even small teams should keep accounts updated regularly.
