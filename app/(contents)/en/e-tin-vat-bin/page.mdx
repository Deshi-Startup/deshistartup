# e-TIN, VAT and BIN – A Simple Guide to Tax and VAT Identity

> **In short:** e-TIN is your tax identity; BIN/VAT is your business identity in the VAT system. These are not just certificates. They connect to bank accounts, invoices, corporate customers, tenders, payment gateways, accounting and compliance. VAT thresholds, rates, exemptions, return dates and processes can change, so confirm with NBR, VAT Online and, where needed, a chartered accountant before acting.

Tax and VAT can feel intimidating for a new founder. You do not need to memorize the whole law at the start. First, understand which identity is used for what, how to keep business money separate and when professional help is needed.

---

## What Is e-TIN?

e-TIN, or electronic Taxpayer Identification Number, is the NBR tax identity. Individuals, sole proprietors, directors, shareholders and companies may have separate tax identities. Having a personal TIN does not automatically complete all company tax duties.

TIN may be needed to:

- submit personal income tax returns
- open business bank or merchant accounts
- support trade license, RJSC or other registration processes
- show tax identity to suppliers or customers
- document directors/shareholders
- onboard with large customers, marketplaces, tenders or payment gateways

Official portal: [NBR e-TIN Portal](https://secure.incometax.gov.bd/TINHome)

## Personal TIN vs Business Tax Identity

Many founders start with personal accounts. For small tests this can be practical. Once regular income starts, personal income, business income, customer money, courier settlements, ad spend and supplier payments must be separated.

| Topic | Personal TIN | Business/company tax identity |
|---|---|---|
| For | Individual, founder, director, freelancer | Registered company or business |
| Use | Personal tax return, director documentation | Business income, banking, invoices, compliance |
| Caution | Do not mix personal and business income | Companies need separate accounts and filings |

If you incorporate a company, keeping income, costs, bank accounts and records under the company becomes even more important.

## What Is BIN/VAT?

BIN, or Business Identification Number, is the business identity in the VAT system. Once VAT registered, a business must handle VAT invoices, returns, sales/purchase records and required documentation.

Think about VAT/BIN when:

- taxable sales are happening regularly
- corporate customers ask for VAT invoices
- marketplace, supplier, distributor or tender paperwork requires it
- you are entering import, manufacturing, trading or regulated supply chains
- the business has reached a stage where avoiding tax/VAT registration becomes risky

Official portal: [VAT Online](https://vat.gov.bd)

## Organize Documents Before Starting

| Information/document | Why it may be needed |
|---|---|
| National ID, mobile, email | Identity verification |
| Trade license | Local business permission |
| Company documents | Certificate, MoA/AoA, director details if incorporated |
| Office/shop address proof | VAT, trade license and bank verification |
| Bank account | Payment and tax connection |
| Business activity | Tax/VAT classification |
| Estimated sales | Registration and filing responsibility |
| Accountant/chartered accountant | Explanation and filing support |

Check that names and addresses match across documents. Small spelling mismatches can become large problems later.

## General Path To Getting e-TIN

Use the NBR e-TIN portal to create or log in to an account, enter identity and taxpayer information, choose the right taxpayer class and download the certificate. Before submitting, check name, date of birth, NID number, address and mobile number.

Getting a TIN certificate is not the end. You still need to understand whether you must submit returns, what income sources to report and which records to keep.

## General Path To VAT/BIN Registration

First, understand whether your business activity falls under VAT and what the current rules require. Then check VAT Online or the relevant VAT office instructions. Keep trade license, TIN, company documents, address proof, bank information and business activity details ready.

After receiving BIN, the most important work is returns and records. VAT registration without returns creates risk. Build a system to reconcile sales, purchases, discounts, refunds, delivery charges, cash on delivery, bKash/Nagad, payment gateways and bank deposits.

## Minimum Record Keeping

Even a small business should reconcile accounts monthly. Google Sheets can work at first, but the habit must be consistent.

| Record | What to keep |
|---|---|
| Sales log | Date, customer, order/invoice, amount, payment method |
| Purchase log | Supplier, invoice, VAT challan reference if available |
| Payment reconciliation | Courier, bKash/Nagad, gateway and bank deposits |
| Returns/refunds | Reason, amount, replacement or refund |
| Expenses | Rent, salary, ads, software, delivery, packaging |
| Tax/VAT calendar | Returns, payments, renewals, adviser notes |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better approach |
|---|---|
| Mixing personal and business money | Keep separate bank/mobile finance channels and an accounting sheet |
| Taking VAT registration and forgetting returns | Maintain a calendar and accountant support |
| Taking large payments without invoices | Clarify purchase order, invoice, receipt and tax/VAT treatment first |
| Deciding from an old threshold someone told you | Check the latest NBR/VAT source |
| Calling a chartered accountant at the last minute | Set the accounting format early |

## Decision Checklist

- [ ] Personal e-TIN exists and the information is correct
- [ ] Business income is being separated from personal income
- [ ] Trade license/company names and addresses match
- [ ] Current VAT/BIN rules have been checked from official sources
- [ ] Sales, purchases, refunds and payment reconciliation are tracked
- [ ] Return dates are on the calendar
- [ ] A chartered accountant or tax adviser has been consulted where needed
