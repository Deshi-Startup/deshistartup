# e-TIN, VAT and BIN: What You Need and When

> **TL;DR:** An e-TIN identifies a taxpayer. Proof of return submission shows that a return was filed for a particular tax year. A BIN identifies a business in the VAT system. They are not interchangeable. Start with the transaction, business category and twelve-month turnover rather than assuming one certificate settles every obligation.

This page is a short route map. The current procedures live in separate guides so that the same legal rule does not become stale in several places.

## A TIN Is Not Proof of Filing a Return

Suppose Munira has an e-TIN certificate and a bank asks for proof that she filed the current tax-year return. The TIN certificate is not enough. One document gives her taxpayer number; the other proves a return was submitted.

Section 166 of the Income Tax Act, 2023 sets out who must file. Section 264 requires proof of return submission for specified transactions. Use the decision list in the [e-TIN Guide](phase-one/e-tin-guide) for your role, income and transaction.

## A BIN Does Not Put Everyone in the Same VAT Regime

Based on NBR information checked on 10 July 2026, a business generally needs to consider turnover-tax enlistment once turnover exceeds BDT 3 million in twelve months and full VAT registration once it exceeds BDT 8 million. The turnover-tax rate in the middle band is 3%. Some goods, services or activities may require registration regardless of turnover.

The test is not limited to a July-to-June year. It considers the previous rolling twelve months and a reasonable estimate of the next twelve months. See the worked examples in [Turnover Tax and VAT Thresholds](phase-one/turnover-tax-and-vat-threshold-explainer).

## Prepare the Records Before the Certificate

A BIN brings return, invoice and recordkeeping duties. Separate at least:

- sales, refunds and discounts
- supplier invoices and payments
- courier cash-on-delivery settlements
- mobile financial services, gateway and bank deposits
- personal money, loans and business revenue

Rule of thumb: **Understand the duty before taking the certificate, and set up the records before the duty begins.**
