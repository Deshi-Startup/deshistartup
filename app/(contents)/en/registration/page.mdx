# Business Registration: When, How and Which Structure

> **TL;DR:** Business registration is not synonymous with incorporating at RJSC. Your stage, liability, customers, co-founders, funding plan and sector determine whether a sole proprietorship, partnership, private limited company or another structure fits. Start with the reason you need formal documents now.

Some founders incorporate before testing the idea and take on filing work too early. Others begin regular sales without clean records. The right timing depends on risk, transactions, customers, team and sector rules.

## Ask What Is Blocked

| What you need | What to consider |
|---|---|
| Business bank account | Trade license, TIN and entity documents |
| Payment provider | Business documents, bank, tax and website requirements |
| Corporate customer | Invoice, entity or trade license, contract |
| Co-founder | Ownership, roles, equity and written terms |
| Equity funding | Private limited company, clean cap table and accounts |
| Regulated activity | Sector licenses and approvals |

Customer interviews and free tests usually do not require an incorporated company. Once you charge, hire, rent premises, enter a regulated field or sign corporate contracts, check which documents are immediately required.

## Main Structures

| Structure | Plain description | Common fit | Main caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sole proprietorship | A business operated by one person | Small shop, solo service or early commerce | Business liability can reach the owner personally |
| Partnership | Several people carry on a business together | Service firm, trading or a small operation | Partners generally have unlimited personal liability |
| Private limited company | A separate legal entity limited by shares | Scalable startup, corporate work or equity funding | More accounts, audit and filings |
| One person company | A company form with one member | A solo founder seeking a company structure | Confirm current eligibility and procedure with a professional |

For the decision in detail, read [Company Types](/en/company-types).

## Sole Proprietorship

A sole proprietorship generally begins with the relevant trade license, the owner's tax records, business banking or payments, and VAT registration or enlistment where applicable. There is no separate incorporation that shields the owner from business liabilities.

Even at a small scale, keep business income, refunds, costs and personal spending separate.

## Partnership

A partnership deed should state capital contributions, profit and loss sharing, decision rights, bank authority, responsibilities, exits and dispute resolution. Under the Partnership Act, partners may be jointly and separately liable without limit for acts of the firm. A private agreement does not necessarily remove that exposure to outsiders.

## Private Limited Company

RJSC handles incorporation. The process starts with name clearance, followed by the MoA, AoA, prescribed forms, director and shareholder details, registered office and government fees.

This structure commonly fits when you need clear co-founder equity, corporate contracts, company-owned IP, a board or equity investment. It also brings recurring accounts, audit and filing duties.

Use the current [Private Limited Company Registration](/en/registration/private-limited) guide for the documents, fee example and application steps.

## The Usual Company Sequence

1. Check the proposed name with RJSC and consider domain and brand risk.
2. Prepare the MoA, AoA, prescribed forms, director and shareholder details and registered office.
3. Submit through the RJSC portal and pay the government fees.
4. Respond to any review and obtain the Certificate of Incorporation if approved.
5. Complete the post-incorporation work: banking, trade license, tax, VAT analysis, share records, accounts and compliance calendar.

Do not copy an MoA, AoA or share structure without understanding it. These documents affect the company's permitted activities, control and later investment.

## Budget Beyond Incorporation

Separate one-time costs from annual costs. Government registration fees are only one part. Budget for accounts, audit, tax and VAT work where applicable, license renewal, RJSC filings and professional advice.

## Checklist

- [ ] Identified the task that requires formal documents
- [ ] Recorded why the chosen structure fits
