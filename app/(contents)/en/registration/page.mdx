# Business Registration

> **In short:** To start a business in Bangladesh, you need official registration. This guide covers which business entity fits you, how to register with RJSC, and what it may cost.

---

## Entity Types

In Bangladesh, you can mainly register four types of business entities:

### 1. Sole Proprietorship

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Owner** | 1 person |
| **Liability** | Unlimited, including personal assets |
| **Registration** | Trade License only |
| **Cost** | Tk 5,000-Tk 15,000 for trade license |
| **Best for** | Small shops, freelancers, early-stage testing |

**Advantages:**

- Easiest and cheapest option
- Less paperwork
- All profit belongs to you

**Disadvantages:**

- Business and personal liability are not separate.
- Raising larger investment is difficult.
- Investors usually do not prefer this structure.

### 2. Partnership

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Owners** | 2-20 people |
| **Liability** | Unlimited for all partners |
| **Registration** | Partnership registration with RJSC |
| **Cost** | Tk 5,000-Tk 20,000 |
| **Required document** | Partnership Deed |

**Best for:** Small businesses run by 2-3 people that do not yet need a company structure.

### 3. Private Limited Company

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Owners (shareholders)** | 2-50 people |
| **Directors** | At least 2 |
| **Liability** | Limited to share value |
| **Registration** | Company registration with RJSC |
| **Cost** | Tk 25,000-Tk 60,000+, depending on authorized capital |
| **Timeline** | 7-15 working days online |
| **Best for** | Startups and businesses planning to raise investment |

**Advantages:**

- Limited liability protects personal assets.
- Investors prefer this structure.
- Higher credibility.
- Easier to win larger contracts.

**Disadvantages:**

- More compliance, such as annual returns and audit.
- Higher cost.
- More paperwork.

### 4. Public Limited Company

| Topic | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Shareholders** | 7+ people |
| **Stock market** | Can be listed |
| **Best for** | Large corporations or IPO-track companies |

> **For most startups, a private limited company is the right choice.**

## Private Limited Company Registration: Step by Step

### Step 1: Choose a Company Name

You need to apply for name clearance at RJSC.

**How to do it:**

1. Go to the [RJSC website](https://roc.gov.bd).
2. Open the Name Clearance section.
3. Submit 3 name preferences in case the first is rejected.
4. Pay the fee: Tk 200 + 15% VAT, total Tk 230 per application.

**Name rules:**

- The name must end with “Limited” or “Ltd.”
- It cannot match an already registered name.
- You cannot use the name of a government body.
- Offensive or misleading names are not acceptable.

**Tips:**

- Search RJSC online to see whether the name already exists.
- Choose a short, memorable name.
- Check whether the domain name is available, such as .com or .com.bd.

### Step 2: Prepare Documents

Required documents:

| Document | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Memorandum of Association (MoA)** | Company objectives, authorized capital, shareholder information |
| **Articles of Association (AoA)** | Internal rules of the company |
| **Form IX** | Directors’ information and consent |
| **Form VI** | Registered office address |
| **Directors’ NID** | Copies of national ID cards |
| **Directors’ photos** | Passport-size photos |
| **Tax Identification Number (TIN)** | All directors should have TINs |
| **Bank draft** | Registration fee |

**Authorized capital and registration fee:**

| Authorized capital | Approximate registration fee |
|--------------------|------------------------------|
| Tk 100,000 | Tk 4,000-Tk 5,000 |
| Tk 500,000 | Tk 9,000-Tk 12,000 |
| Tk 1,000,000 | Tk 14,000-Tk 18,000 |
| Tk 2,500,000 | Tk 25,000-Tk 35,000 |
| Tk 5,000,000 | Tk 40,000-Tk 55,000 |

> **Tip:** Most startups begin with Tk 1,000,000 authorized capital. It can be increased later.

### Step 3: Apply Online to RJSC

1. Create an account on the [RJSC Portal](https://roc.gov.bd).
2. After name clearance approval, apply for company registration.
3. Upload all documents.
4. Pay the fee by bank draft or online payment.
5. RJSC will review the application and approve it.

### Step 4: Receive Certificates

After approval, you will receive:

- **Certificate of Incorporation**, which is the birth certificate of your company
- **Company Registration Number**
- Registered company details

### Step 5: Post-Registration Tasks

After the company is registered, you still need to complete a few tasks:

- [ ] **TIN (Tax Identification Number)** through NBR e-TIN registration
- [ ] **BIN (Business Identification Number)** for VAT registration
- [ ] **Trade License** from city corporation or municipality
- [ ] **Bank Account** in the company name
- [ ] **Share Certificates** issued to shareholders
- [ ] **Stamp Duty** for MoA and AoA where required

## e-TIN Registration

All companies and directors should have TINs.

**How to do it:**

1. Go to the [NBR e-TIN Portal](https://nbr.gov.bd).
2. Click “Register for TIN.”
3. Provide NID or company registration number.
4. Fill in the information.
5. Download the TIN certificate.

**Cost:** Free  
**Time:** Immediate online

---

## VAT/BIN Registration

VAT or turnover tax depends on your annual turnover:

- **Annual turnover below Tk 50 lakh:** VAT or turnover tax exempt.
- **Annual turnover between Tk 50 lakh and Tk 3 crore:** 4% turnover tax applies.
- **Annual turnover above Tk 3 crore:** 15% standard VAT applies, and BIN is mandatory.

> **Note:** IT services, e-commerce, or software businesses may have special VAT rates or exemptions.

**How to do it:**

1. Go to the [VAT Online Portal](https://vat.gov.bd).
2. Apply for BIN registration.
3. Upload the required documents.
4. After approval, you will receive a BIN number.

## Cost Summary

| Item | Approximate cost |
|------|------------------|
| Name clearance | Tk 230 including VAT |
| Company registration with Tk 10 lakh authorized capital | Tk 14,000-Tk 18,000 |
| MoA/AoA preparation by lawyer/consultant | Tk 5,000-Tk 15,000 |
| TIN registration | Free |
| BIN/VAT registration | Free |
| Trade License | Tk 5,000-Tk 15,000 |
| Stamp Duty | Tk 1,000-Tk 5,000 |
| **Total approximate cost** | **Tk 30,000-Tk 60,000** |

## Do You Need a Consultant?

| Situation | Consultant needed? |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Sole proprietorship | Usually no |
| Simple partnership | Maybe |
| Private limited company | **Yes, recommended** |
| Foreign shareholder | **Definitely** |

**Why a consultant helps:**

- Preparing MoA/AoA correctly
- Navigating the RJSC portal process
- Avoiding costly mistakes

**Where to find consultants:**

- Registered company secretaries at RJSC
- Local law firms
- Online service platforms and business service partners

## Annual Compliance

After a company is registered, some work must be done every year:

| Task | Deadline | Where |
|------|----------|-------|
| Submit Annual Return | Within 30 days of AGM | RJSC |
| Submit Income Tax Return | By November 30 each year | NBR |
| Submit VAT Return | Monthly | VAT Online |
| Audit Report | Before AGM | Chartered Accountant |
| Renew Trade License | Every year | City corporation |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| Starting business without registration | Register first, then operate |
| Setting authorized capital too high | Tk 10 lakh is usually enough at the beginning |
| Not getting TIN/BIN | These are required and penalties may apply |
| Not submitting annual returns | Can lead to fines and company strike-off |
| Mixing personal and business money | Keep a separate bank account |
