# Founder Life

> **In short:** Founder life is not just about being an owner. It is a life of working 7 days a week, 10-12 hours a day, and making new decisions every day.

## What Is Founder Life?

Founder life is a way of working where your own hands need to be involved in almost every part of the business.

A founder often works on many things at the same time:

- Thinking through the idea
- Understanding customer problems
- Selling
- Providing support
- Managing finance
- Working with the team
- Planning for the future

In Bangladesh, many founders start alone. So being a founder does not only mean being the CEO. You also need to be a:

- Problem solver
- Salesperson
- Operator
- Recruiter
- Storyteller
- Financial planner
- Customer advocate

## The Reality of Founder Life

### 1. You Will Do Many Jobs Every Day

A new founder’s day often looks like this:

- Checking plans or follow-ups early in the morning
- Following up with leads from Facebook, WhatsApp, or Messenger
- Talking to customers
- Monitoring orders or deliveries
- Discussing work with the team or suppliers
- Handling sales, content, or promotion
- Planning the next day at night

This life can feel very busy, but not every busy task is important. As a founder, your job is to focus more on priorities than on the number of tasks.

### 2. You Need to Get Used to Uncertainty

Many things in founder life are uncertain:

- Who will buy?
- Who should you sell to?
- When will cash flow become difficult?
- When do you need to make team decisions?
- When should the product change?

Do not treat uncertainty only as danger. Treat it as part of the system.

Your job is to:

- Create hypotheses
- Run small experiments
- Watch the results
- Learn quickly

### 3. You Need to Build the Right Mindset

It is normal to feel low as a founder.

Many founders think:

- “Am I doing this wrong?”
- “Why are people not buying?”
- “Am I capable enough?”

These thoughts are normal. But as a founder, you need to stay honest with yourself.

A good founder:

- Understands their limits
- Works in small, steady steps
- Maintains routines to avoid emotional crashes
- Protects their mental health

## The 5 Main Responsibilities of Founder Life

### 1. Holding the Vision

You need to keep sight of why the business exists.

You will do many small tasks every day, but your mind should keep asking:

- Why did I start this business?
- What do we want to become in the future?
- What big customer problem are we solving?

### 2. Customer Obsession

Your first priority is customer understanding.

As a founder, your work is to:

- Understand the customer’s language
- Listen to their problems
- Collect their feedback
- Turn feedback into product or service improvements

### 3. Execution

Most of the real work happens in execution.

A large part of your work is:

- Follow-up
- Follow-through
- Fixing small issues
- Coordination
- Delivery quality

### 4. Financial Discipline

Money management is very important in founder life.

You need to regularly ask:

- Where is our revenue coming from?
- Which channel is profitable?
- Which customers take the most time?
- Which costs can be reduced?

### 5. Building People

In the end, the strength of your business will be its people.

As a founder, your work is to:

- Build the team
- Define roles
- Build trust
- Create ownership

## A Daily Rhythm for Founder Life

A simple routine can look like this:

1. **Morning:** Set priorities
2. **Before lunch:** Customer follow-up or sales
3. **Afternoon:** Operations, support, suppliers, finance
4. **Evening:** Tomorrow’s plan and learning
5. **Night:** Reflection, notes, next step

If you follow the same rhythm regularly, your brain gets less chaotic.

## 7 Founder Life Habits

- Set 3 priorities every day.
- Always write down customer feedback.
- Ask once a day, “Which task matters most?”
- Focus on major decisions instead of only small tasks.
- Review cash flow and revenue regularly.
- Keep time for yourself.
- Do not try to do everything alone.

## A Practical Founder Life Checklist

- [ ] I wrote down today’s 3 most important tasks.
- [ ] I collected feedback from at least one customer.
- [ ] I checked revenue or order status.
- [ ] I reviewed cash flow or expenses once.
- [ ] I wrote tomorrow’s priorities.
- [ ] I kept 30 minutes for myself.

## The Biggest Founder Life Lesson

The most important thing in founder life is to keep going.

You will not always be able to do everything.
You will not always make the right decision.
Still, you need to continue in small, consistent steps.

> **Being a founder means trying to understand a little more every day.**
