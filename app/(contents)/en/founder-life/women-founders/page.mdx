# Women Founders: Safety, Meetings, Travel and Ownership

> **TL;DR:** A woman founder may have to manage safety, family concern, online harassment and ownership records alongside the business itself. Write the rules in advance: where meetings happen, who knows the plan, where evidence is stored and whose name belongs on each business document.

Suppose Sharmin runs a homemade-food business in Chattogram and receives a proposal to supply lunches to an office. Alongside margin, she must consider the contact's identity, the meeting venue and whom to tell. Those are operating questions, not distractions from the business.

## Discuss the Risk with Family

Family members may worry about safety, study, income or social pressure. Make the plan concrete:

- how long the test will run
- how much money is at risk
- how study, work or household time will be protected
- which evidence means continue
- which condition means stop or change

If a family member wants to help, agree on the task, time and money. Do not assume permanent unpaid labour.

## Before a Client Meeting

Adapt a safety list to the situation:

- Confirm the person's identity and agenda by phone or video first.
- Hold a first in-person meeting in daylight at a familiar public venue.
- Tell a trusted person who, where, when and the expected return time.
- Take a colleague, partner or trusted person when useful.
- Control your own route home rather than relying on the other party.
- Leave if the situation feels unsafe. No deal is worth remaining in danger.

No checklist guarantees safety. Adjust it for the location, work and personal risk.

## Travel and Delivery

Compare courier pickup, local delivery or a designated worker instead of assuming the founder must deliver every order. Before choosing a provider, read the current service area, charges, compensation, return and complaint terms.

For a first large supplier transaction, verify identity, address, sample, invoice and payment terms. Share a market or warehouse visit plan with someone you trust.

## Privacy and Online Harassment

Using the founder's face is not a brand requirement. Genuine product images, work samples, clear terms and timely support can build trust without exposing more personal information than necessary.

- Separate personal and business phone numbers.
- Think before publishing a home address; use an appropriate business or pickup address where possible.
- Save screenshots, links, profile details and timestamps of harassment, then report and block through the platform.
- For threats, blackmail or persistent harassment, tell someone you trust and contact Bangladesh Police's [Police Cyber Support for Women](https://www.police.gov.bd/en/police_cyber_support_for_women). Its published hotline is **01320-000888**.
- For immediate danger in Bangladesh, call the national emergency service at **999**.

Store evidence securely and avoid spreading harmful content while documenting it.

## Keep Ownership Records Correct

“Put everything in your own name” is not correct for every structure. First identify whether the business is a sole proprietorship, partnership or company.

- For a sole proprietorship, keep the trade license, bank and merchant records in the actual proprietor's name.
- For a company, licenses, bank accounts and contracts belong in the company's name. Record shares, directors and authorised signatories correctly.
- A personal e-TIN and company TIN are different. See [Personal vs Company TIN](/en/tax/personal-vs-company-tin).
- Do not leave passwords, OTPs, bank authority and original documents under another person's sole control. Keep secure backups.
- If a partner or family member contributes money, record whether it is debt, a gift or equity and sign the terms.

Incorrect ownership records create correction work during banking, lending, fundraising or a sale. Read [Company Types](/en/company-types) and [Trade License](/en/trade-license), then confirm uncertain cases with a qualified professional.

## Choose Communities Carefully

Founder communities can provide operating experience about suppliers, couriers and safety. A large group does not make every answer reliable. Do not publish identity documents or personal numbers. Verify legal, tax and large-payment advice against an official source or professional.

## Checklist

- [ ] Written the test period, budget and stop condition
- [ ] Chosen the meeting venue, time and person to inform
- [ ] Separated personal and business communication
- [ ] Set a secure process for harassment evidence
- [ ] Saved the emergency and PCSW numbers
- [ ] Matched ownership and banking records to the entity
- [ ] Retained control of passwords, OTPs and original documents
