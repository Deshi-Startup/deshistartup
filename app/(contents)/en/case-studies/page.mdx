# Case Studies – Real Stories from Bangladeshi Startups

> **TL;DR:** International examples can teach useful ideas, but the same tactic may not transfer unchanged to Bangladesh. These case studies use public sources to examine the problem, first test, customer acquisition, operations and limits. Founder interviews remain interviews, not independent market evidence.

## Why Local Case Studies Matter

Payments, delivery, addresses, language, trust and regulation affect sectors differently in Bangladesh. Before copying an international tactic, examine whom a local company served, which manual work it did and where it struggled.

Every case study follows the fifteen questions in [`plan/case-study-format.md`](https://github.com/Deshi-Startup/deshistartup/blob/main/plan/case-study-format.md), including the problem, local context, first wedge, first customers, business model, limits and unknowns. A claim that cannot be fully verified is labelled as a company claim, founder account or unknown.

The plan includes both success and failure cases, because looking only at surviving companies creates selection bias. A page carrying a **“to be written”** notice is a planned topic, not a completed case study.
