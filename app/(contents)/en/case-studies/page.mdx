# Case Studies – Real Stories from Bangladeshi Startups

> **In short:** Generic startup advice (most of it from Silicon Valley) misses Bangladesh's reality – a cash-heavy economy, low initial trust in new brands, weak card penetration, Dhaka-centric infrastructure, and regulatory uncertainty. This section tells you what actually happened here, with sources, without the hype.

## Why case studies – what generic advice misses

Most startup content is written with the US or India's large markets in mind, where card payments are common, investors are plentiful, and regulatory paths are clearer. Bangladesh is different: most transactions still run through cash or Mobile Financial Services (bKash, Nagad), cash-on-delivery still drives e-commerce, and building trust with a new brand takes time. The startups that survived and grew here did not sidestep these realities – they built their models inside them.

Every case study follows the 15-question structure defined in [`plan/case-study-format.md`](https://github.com/Deshi-Startup/deshistartup/blob/main/plan/case-study-format.md): what the problem was, why it was Bangladesh-specific, what the founders saw before others did, what the first wedge was, how trust was built, what the business model is, and what a new founder should and should not copy. Every factual claim is sourced, and facts are kept separate from anecdotes – no case study here is overclaimed.

This section holds two kinds of writing: Success Stories and Failure & Lessons – because looking only at winners creates a distorted picture. What did not work, and why, is an equally important lesson.
