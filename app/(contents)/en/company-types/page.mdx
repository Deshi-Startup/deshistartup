# Company Types – Sole Proprietorship, Partnership or Private Limited?

> **In short:** Your legal structure affects liability, tax, bank accounts, ability to raise investment, credibility with customers and compliance cost. At a very early validation stage you may not need a company, but if you have regular sales, co-founders, corporate customers, employees or funding plans, the structure matters.

New founders often ask, “Should I open a company?” Break the question down first: are you working alone or with partners? Will customers ask for company papers? Is money coming in regularly? Does the product or service create liability risk? Might you need investment or large contracts later? These answers guide the structure.

This page is not legal advice. It gives you the language to speak more clearly with a chartered accountant, company secretary or lawyer.

---

## The Three Most Common Paths

| Path | Plain meaning | Good use | Main tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sole proprietorship | Business under one person | Solo services, small shop, early commerce | Higher personal liability, harder to raise investment |
| Partnership | Two or more people running a business | Service firm, trading, small operations | Partner-dispute risk |
| Private limited company | Separate legal entity with shareholders/directors | Startups, corporate customers, funding, larger teams | More cost, filing and governance |

Bangladesh has other structures too, such as one person company, branch office, liaison office, NGO or social enterprise paths. Most new founders first decide among these three.

---

## When Sole Proprietorship May Be Enough

If you are starting alone, testing the market on a small budget, have low product/service risk and do not need outside investment yet, sole proprietorship can be a realistic starting point. Many freelancers, small shops, local services and early online sellers start this way.

The limits are real. Business liability can mix with personal risk. Larger corporate customers may not always be comfortable. If you later need co-founder equity, investment or large contracts, you may need to change structure.

If you choose this path, keep accounts separate from the beginning. Mixing personal expenses, business income, courier settlements and ad spend in one account makes tax and accounting harder later.

## When To Consider Partnership

A partnership can work when two or more people start a service firm, trading business, local operation or small manufacturing activity. Do not skip written terms just because you are friends or family.

At minimum, write down:

- who contributes capital, labor or assets
- how profit and loss will be shared
- who can make which decisions
- who operates the bank account
- what happens if someone leaves
- how a new partner can join
- how disputes will be handled

Partnership mixes relationships and money. Written terms feel uncomfortable early, but reduce risk later.

## When A Private Limited Company May Be Needed

A private limited company is the more formal structure. It may be needed when you must define co-founder ownership, prepare for investors or grants, sign contracts with corporate customers, keep intellectual property under the company, hire a team or plan for scale.

But a company is not just a registration certificate. It brings accounting, tax, audit, filings, board decisions, shareholder records, bank paperwork, trade license, VAT, payment gateways and governance discipline. The structure gives credibility, but it also demands order.

## Fast Decision Map

| Your situation | Possible starting point |
|---|---|
| Only customer interviews and landing-page tests | Research without a formal entity, but keep notes and expenses |
| Small Facebook commerce order tests | Consider sole proprietorship + trade license path |
| Two co-founders building a software product | Write co-founder terms first, then plan company timing |
| Corporate pilot or B2B contract | Private limited may be more credible |
| Imported product sales | Check trade, import, tax and VAT requirements early |
| Fintech, health, food or education certification | Get sector-specific legal advice first |
| Active investor conversations | Private limited company and clean cap table |

## If You Have Co-founders, Write Terms Before The Company

Many founders think co-founder terms can wait until incorporation. In reality, the opposite is safer. Before company registration, write down roles, time commitment, future ownership expectations, what happens if someone leaves and who owns the work already created.

Write down:

- founder roles
- expected time commitment
- future equity or ownership expectations
- vesting logic
- intellectual property ownership
- exit terms
- who is paying expenses
- what happens if decisions deadlock

This may not be a lawyer-reviewed final agreement, but it creates clarity early.

## Ten Questions Before Choosing

- Will you have regular income in the next 6 months?
- Will customers ask for invoices or company documents?
- Do you have co-founders or partners?
- Will you apply for funding, grants or accelerators?
- Can the product or service create liability?
- Will personal bank/mobile finance usage create tax and accounting risk?
- Could sector-specific licenses be required?
- Do you have a stable business address?
- Who will keep accounts?
- Can you afford annual compliance cost?
