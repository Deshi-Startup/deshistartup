# Should You Trade Alone, Take Partners, or Form a Company?

> **In short:** Still interviewing customers and testing an idea? There is no need to rush into company registration. A sole proprietorship may fit a small business with one owner. If two people will share profit, put the partnership terms in writing. Planning to issue shares, bring in an investor, or sign larger contracts? Consider a private limited company. Before deciding, write down the owners, who carries business debts, and what you expect to do in the next 12 months.

“Should I open a company?” sounds like a yes-or-no question. Start with three smaller questions: who owns the business, who carries its debts, and where do you want it to be in one year? Once those answers are on paper, the right path becomes much clearer.

This guide is not legal advice. It will help you ask a chartered accountant, company secretary, or lawyer better questions. Have a professional review any partnership deed or company document before you sign it.

## See the Difference at a Glance

New businesses in Bangladesh commonly begin through one of these three paths. The table is a starting point, not a final verdict. Find the row closest to your situation, then read the explanation below it.

| Path | Plain meaning | When it may fit | Main caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sole proprietorship | You and the business are not separate legal persons | You are starting a small sales or service business alone | Business debts can become your personal responsibility |
| Partnership | Two or more people agree to run a business and share its profit | People who know each other are starting a service, trading, or small manufacturing business | One partner's business act can expose the others to liability |
| Private limited company | Registration creates a separate body corporate | Share ownership, an investor, or larger contracts are on the horizon | Accounts, returns, and company records need ongoing attention |

Bangladesh also has one person companies, public companies, foreign-company branches, and other structures. Those serve more specific situations. Most first-time founders should understand these three choices first.

## The Core Difference: Who Is Responsible When Something Goes Wrong?

Liability means the burden of a business debt, compensation claim, or legal demand. The practical question is simple: if the business runs short of money, whom can a creditor pursue?

Suppose Rahim runs a clothing business in Chattogram. Before Eid, he buys BDT 200,000 of inventory from a supplier on credit. Sales disappoint and he cannot pay the full balance.

- **As a sole proprietor:** Rahim and the business are not separate legal persons. Rahim remains responsible for the unpaid business debt.
- **In a partnership:** both partners may face a claim even if Rahim's partner placed the order in the firm's name. Section 25 of the [Partnership Act, 1932](https://bdlaws.minlaw.gov.bd/act-print-157.html) makes partners jointly and severally liable for firm acts. In practical terms, a creditor may demand the full amount from one partner.
- **Through a private limited company:** a contract made in the company's name is generally the company's debt. Section 24 of the [Companies Act, 1994](https://bdlaws.minlaw.gov.bd/act-print-788.html) makes the registered company a body corporate. Personal guarantees or breaches of law can still create personal liability. “Limited” is not a magic shield.

This distinction can change the entire decision. If the business will take large advances, buy on credit, borrow money, or carry a meaningful risk of harm, do not compare structures only by registration cost. Write down the plausible debt as well.

## When Sole Proprietorship May Fit

Sole proprietorship may be the simplest place to start when most of these statements are true:

- you are the only owner
- you do not plan to issue ownership to anyone yet
- outside equity investment is not an immediate goal
- the product or service carries relatively modest risk
- customers will accept documents and invoices in your business name

Suppose Tanvir works alone from Bogura, providing design services. His goal for the first six months is to find ten repeat clients. Opening a private limited company merely to use the word “startup” would add paperwork without helping him learn faster.

Sole proprietorship does not mean mixing every payment in one personal wallet or bank account. Track business income, personal withdrawals, advertising, and courier settlements separately. Otherwise tax and accounting become painful later.

Company registration is also not the only legal question. Trade-license, tax, and sector-specific rules may still apply. The documents depend on what you do and where you operate.

## If You Choose Partnership, Put Paper Under the Relationship

A partnership is a contractual relationship in which people run a business and share its profit. Under the [Partnership Act, 1932](https://bdlaws.minlaw.gov.bd/act-print-157.html), one partner acting in the usual course of business can bind the firm. “But we are friends” is not legal protection.

Suppose Nasrin and her cousin open a coaching centre in Sylhet. They agree to split profit equally. Two years later, the centre is doing well. New questions appear: who owns the brand? Who paid the rent deposit? If one leaves, who keeps the Facebook page and student list?

A partnership deed records those answers before the dispute begins. At minimum, decide:

- how much money each person contributes and how much time each will work
- how profit and loss will be shared
- the largest commitment one partner may approve alone
- who controls the bank account
- how an outgoing partner's entitlement will be calculated
- whose consent is required for a new partner
- how you will settle a deadlock or dispute

If two founders are building a software product intended to grow quickly, ask whether partnership is enough. A private limited company usually provides a clearer way to record ownership when future investors will receive shares.

Rule of thumb: **the closer the relationship, the more important the written agreement. Write it to protect the relationship.**

## When to Consider a Private Limited Company

Ownership in a private limited company is divided into shares. People who own those shares are shareholders. Directors oversee the company's major decisions and legal responsibilities.

Seek professional advice about this path when two or three of these conditions apply:

- co-founders will divide ownership through shares
- an investor will receive part of the company
- software, a brand, or other intellectual property should belong to the company
- a larger organisation expects to contract with a company
- you are building a business intended to grow over several years

Registration is not the finish line. Filing means submitting required forms and records to RJSC on time. The company must also maintain accounts, tax records, applicable audits, director decisions, and share records. Decide who will do that work and how the business will pay for it before registering.

Having a private limited company does not cause investors to appear. It provides a clearer structure for ownership and contracts. You still have to build the customers, revenue, and business.

## Which Path Should You Examine First?

Do not make the final decision from one matching row. Use the row to form your next questions, then speak to an accountant or lawyer if the stakes justify it.

| What you are doing now | Examine this path first | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Interviewing customers or testing an MVP | No company yet | Learning notes and expense records are the first job |
| Selling regularly through a Facebook page by yourself | Sole proprietorship and trade license | There is one owner and no outside share investment |
| Building software with a co-founder | Written founder terms, then consider private limited | Ownership and previously created work need clear homes |
| Running a service or trading business with family or friends | Compare partnership with private limited | Liability and future ownership may point to different answers |
| Discussing ownership with an investor | Private limited | Share ownership is easier to record and change |
| Working in fintech, health, food, or education | Check sector licensing first | A business structure does not replace a sector approval |

## Make a 20-Minute Decision Draft Today

Take one sheet of paper and complete this checklist. It will also make a professional consultation more useful.

- [ ] I have written down every owner's name and role
- [ ] I have recorded how much money each person will contribute
- [ ] I have decided whether outside investment may be needed in the next 12 months
- [ ] I have estimated the largest debt or customer advance the business may carry
- [ ] I have checked which documents customers may request
- [ ] I have recorded who keeps the name, customer data, and created work if someone leaves
- [ ] I have named the person responsible for annual accounts and paperwork

If every answer points to you alone and no outside share investment is planned, examine sole proprietorship first. If there are several owners but the business will remain small and stable, compare partnership liability with a private company's obligations. If shares, investors, or larger contracts are approaching, discuss private limited registration.

Rule of thumb: **use the simplest path while one person is testing small. Settle the structure before ownership, major debt, or outside money arrives.**
