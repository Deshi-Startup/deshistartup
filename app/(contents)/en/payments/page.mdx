# Payments: MFS, Bank, Cards and Cash on Delivery

> **TL;DR:** Payments in Bangladesh are not limited to cards. One customer may prefer bKash or Nagad, another may pay on delivery, while a B2B customer may ask for a bank transfer and invoice. Compare customer convenience, settlement time, charges and reconciliation work before choosing a method.

Founders often leave payment design until the end. The product and Facebook page are ready, but there is no clean way to connect an order with its payment, refund and accounting record. Set up that connection before volume grows.

## Start with the Customer and the Record

Bangladesh businesses may handle mobile financial services (MFS), bank transfers, cash on delivery, payment gateways, courier collections and corporate invoices at the same time.

| Customer or business | Common options to evaluate |
|---|---|
| Small Facebook-led sales | MFS, cash on delivery and written order confirmation |
| Own e-commerce site | Gateway, MFS, cards and cash on delivery |
| B2B service | Bank transfer, invoice, purchase order and payment terms |
| SaaS or digital product | Gateway, bank and foreign-payment rules |
| Physical products outside Dhaka | Courier, cash on delivery, returns and reconciliation |

Ask two questions: how does the customer want to pay, and how will you identify and reconcile that payment safely?

## Mobile Financial Services

bKash, Nagad, Rocket and Upay are familiar MFS options. They may suit small orders, advances, booking money, delivery charges, online classes or service fees.

Using a personal account for regular business receipts mixes personal and business money. Provider terms may also distinguish personal and merchant use. Once sales become regular, check the current merchant conditions and apply for a business merchant account if eligible.

For each payment, record the order ID, payer, amount, transaction reference, date and refund status.

Official sites:

- [bKash](https://www.bkash.com)
- [Nagad](https://nagad.com.bd)

## Bank Transfers

Bank transfers are useful for B2B sales, large orders, salaries, suppliers, corporate pilots and recurring invoices. A corporate customer may request a TIN, trade license, company document or bank details alongside the invoice.

Do not stop at seeing a deposit. Record which invoice and customer it belongs to, whether it is partial or full, any tax or VAT withheld and bank charges.

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Invoice or order number | Connect the deposit to the sale |
| Customer | Record and follow-up |
| Expected amount | Identify a short or excess payment |
| Received amount | Reconcile with the bank statement |
| Payment date | Track cash flow |
| Tax, VAT and charges | Calculate the net receipt |

## Cash on Delivery

Some online buyers prefer cash on delivery. It lowers the barrier to paying in advance, but puts failed-delivery and return costs on the seller.

Common risks include fake orders, unreachable phones, wrong addresses, returns, courier charges, delayed settlements and unmatched collections.

Consider Rahim, who buys a shirt for BDT 800 and lists it for BDT 1,200. He receives ten orders, but two return. Eight delivered sales produce BDT 9,600 in revenue. The sold inventory costs BDT 6,400. Advertising for ten orders costs BDT 800 and packaging BDT 200. Ten outbound and two return courier trips at BDT 120 cost BDT 1,440. Only BDT 760 remains, or BDT 76 per order received. The apparent BDT 400 gross margin was not the final profit.

Confirm the name, phone, address, product, price, delivery charge and expected time in writing. Where appropriate and accepted by the customer, consider a small advance for a high-risk order. Reconcile the courier report with your own order sheet regularly.

Further reading: [Cash on Delivery and Delivery Risk](/en/operations/cod-risk)

## Payment Gateways

A payment gateway can combine cards, MFS and bank options for a website or app. Onboarding may require documents, fees, technical integration and a settlement wait.

Examples currently operating in Bangladesh include:

- [SSLCOMMERZ](https://sslcommerz.com)
- [aamarPay](https://aamarpay.com)
- [shurjoPay](https://shurjopay.com)

These are examples, not endorsements. Before signing, verify the provider's current Bangladesh Bank authorisation, your eligibility, settlement terms, charges and complaint process. Ask:

- Which payment methods are supported?
- How long does settlement take?
- Which setup and transaction charges apply?
- How do refunds and chargebacks work?
- Which plugins or APIs are maintained?
- What documents are required?
- What happens if a settlement is delayed?

## Foreign Customer Payments

Foreign payments for SaaS, software or services involve banking, foreign-exchange, invoice, tax and export-document rules. Availability of international products changes and a product that lets someone pay may not provide a lawful route for a Bangladesh business to receive export proceeds.

Talk to the receiving bank before the first invoice. Confirm the payment route, account, purpose description, invoice, declaration or form, conversion and recordkeeping requirements.

Further reading: [SaaS Exports from Bangladesh](/en/ideas/export-saas) and [Foreign Exchange, Remittance and Foreign Shareholders](/en/trade/foreign-exchange)

## Trust and Refunds

The buyer needs to know what happens after payment and after a problem. Publish the return and refund policy, use order numbers, send confirmation, provide courier tracking and keep a working support channel. Show genuine customer reviews only with permission; do not invent or rewrite them.

## Minimum Reconciliation Sheet

Give every order one row:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Order ID | “DS-001” |
| Customer | Name and phone |
| Product or service | Item sold |
| Total | Product plus delivery |
| Method | MFS, bank, gateway or COD |
| Payment status | Paid, due, partial or refunded |
| Delivery status | Sent, delivered or returned |
| Receipt or transaction ID | Payment evidence |
| Notes | Problem or follow-up |

Reconcile the sheet with bank, MFS, gateway and courier reports at least weekly. Unmatched money is not a reliable business record.

## Checklist

- [ ] Identified the methods customers are comfortable using
- [ ] Created order IDs and a reconciliation sheet
- [ ] Planned how to separate personal and business money
- [ ] Set a courier-report process for cash on delivery
- [ ] Published return and refund terms
- [ ] Checked gateway or merchant-account requirements
- [ ] Kept bank, tax and VAT records
