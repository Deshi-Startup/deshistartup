# Startup, SME, Agency, and E-commerce: What Is the Difference?

> **In short:** Startup, SME, agency, and e-commerce are not four exclusive boxes. A business can be both an SME and an e-commerce operation. An agency can later create a startup. Write three answers first: what do you sell, where do you sell it, and what happens to the work when customers grow? The useful labels will then become clearer.

Suppose Rahim sells clothing through Facebook from Chattogram. Selling online makes the activity e-commerce. Its size may also place it in an SME category for a government programme. Because every order still requires inventory, packing, and courier work, the operation does not yet scale like a startup.

One business can carry several labels because the labels answer different questions.

---

## What Each Label Actually Describes

| Label | The question it answers | Simple example |
|---|---|---|
| Startup | How can the business grow quickly? | Giving the same software to many shops |
| SME | How large is the enterprise and which official size category applies? | A small factory, restaurant, or service firm |
| Agency | Do you perform separate work for each client? | Design, software, or marketing services |
| E-commerce | Through which channel does the sale happen? | Selling through a website, marketplace, or Facebook page |

This distinction removes a common false choice. “E-commerce or startup?” is not always a valid question. An e-commerce marketplace may be a startup, while a small Facebook clothing store may operate as an SME.

---

## Startup Describes the Growth Equation

A startup is still searching for a workable model. Its goal is to take the same solution to many customers quickly. If ten times as many customers require ten times as many people, branches, and costs, scaling is difficult.

Suppose Sumiya is building bookkeeping software for small shops. She visits the first 20 shops and sets up each account herself. Serving 200 shops the same way would require ten more people. At this point, it is still a labour-dependent service.

Sumiya later builds self-service setup. Shop owners learn through videos, pay online, and contact support when needed. The same team can now serve many more shops. The business has found a more startup-like growth path.

Technology alone is not enough. The operating model must work repeatedly. Read [What is a startup?](../start-here/what-is-a-startup) for the full explanation.

## SME Describes Size, Not Quality or Modernity

SME means small and medium enterprise. A government policy or financing programme may classify a business using assets, employees, turnover, and sector. The exact criteria can change by programme. Do not assume eligibility from an old table. Check the current circular for the facility you want.

SME does not mean old-fashioned. A small factory may use new machinery. A restaurant may accept online orders. A software agency may also be an SME by size.

These businesses often begin with familiar demand. Expansion may require another branch, more equipment, additional inventory, or more people. Gradual growth is not a weakness. Managing cash and profit from the beginning is usually the sensible priority.

---

## Agency Describes Selling People's Work

An agency or service firm performs defined work for clients. Design, software, accounting, marketing, legal work, and consulting can all follow this model.

Suppose Nasrin's three-person design agency completes four projects a month at BDT 50,000 each. Monthly sales are BDT 200,000. To take eight projects, the team must work unreasonable hours or hire more designers. Revenue and human time rise together.

That is not a bad model. An agency can earn revenue with little starting capital. Repeated client work may also expose the same problem again and again. Building one product to solve that problem can create a startup beside the agency.

Keep the two sets of accounts separate. Record who spends time on the product, how much it costs, and whether product customers actually exist. Otherwise a busy agency can be mistaken for a successful product.

---

## E-commerce Describes the Sales Route

E-commerce means selling goods or services online. The route may be your own website, a marketplace such as Daraz, or a Facebook page. Facebook-based commerce is often called f-commerce.

The label says nothing about the underlying economics. You may buy and resell clothing, run a marketplace for other merchants, or sell a digital subscription. All three sell online, but their inventory, margins, and growth mechanics differ.

If you buy and resell goods, track four numbers first: purchase price, advertising, courier cost, and returned orders. More orders do not automatically create more profit. If inventory remains unsold, sales may look healthy while cash is trapped.

---

## Funding Sources Are Not Permanent Rules

“A startup always uses VC money” and “an SME always uses a bank loan” are both too simple. Many startups begin with founders' money and customer revenue. SMEs may use family money, bank finance, or a new partner's capital.

What you give in return is different:

- debt must be repaid with interest
- equity gives away part of company ownership
- a client advance creates an obligation to deliver work
- founder money keeps the financial risk in the founder's pocket

Bangladesh Bank has separate policies for startup and SME financing. When seeking a public facility, the programme's eligibility conditions matter more than the label you choose for yourself.

---

## How the Wrong Label Creates the Wrong Work

Suppose Tanvir sells homemade pickles in Bogura. Every new order requires ingredients, bottles, packing, and courier service. He spends months approaching investors as a “food-tech startup.” His real first jobs were fixing contribution margin, bringing old customers back, and organising production. The label sent him towards the wrong work.

The opposite happens too. An agency manually prepares the same report for ten clients. Software could allow thousands of businesses to produce it themselves. Looking only at project invoices may hide the product opportunity.

There is no status in a label. Its job is to reveal the right arithmetic and next action.

## Describe Your Business in Three Lines

Write this today:

```text
We sell: [goods / a defined service / use of the same product]
We reach customers through: [shop / direct sales / Facebook / website / client outreach]
We grow by: [adding people and branches / giving the same system to many customers]
```

- [ ] I have described what we sell in one line
- [ ] I have separated the sales channel from the product
- [ ] I have listed which costs double when customers double
- [ ] I will check current programme definitions before claiming public eligibility
- [ ] I have checked whether today's work matches the label

Rule of thumb: **what you sell, where you sell it, and how you grow are three different answers.**
