# Startup, SME, Agency, and E-commerce: What Is the Difference?

> **In short:** You need to be clear from day one about what kind of business you are starting. A startup and an agency have completely different operating models, funding paths, and scaling strategies.

New entrepreneurs in Bangladesh are often confused about what they are actually building. Let’s compare 4 common categories:

## 1. Startup
A startup is a business designed to scale very quickly using technology or an innovative model.
- **Examples:** Pathao (ride sharing), Shikho (edtech), ShopUp (B2B commerce).
- **Goal:** Solve a large, specific problem and capture market share quickly.
- **Growth:** Exponential. The cost of going from 1 customer to 10,000 customers does not rise proportionally.
- **Funding:** Usually raised from venture capitalists or angel investors.
- **Risk:** Very high.

## 2. SME or Regular Business
An SME is a traditional business built around proven demand in a local market.
- **Examples:** Restaurant, grocery store, boutique shop, pharmacy, or small manufacturing factory.
- **Goal:** Become profitable from the beginning and serve demand in a specific area.
- **Growth:** Linear. To increase revenue, costs often rise as well, such as opening a new branch.
- **Funding:** Personal savings, loans from family or friends, or bank loans.
- **Risk:** Moderate, because the business model is already tested.

## 3. Agency or Service Business
An agency is a business where you provide specific services to clients.
- **Examples:** Digital marketing agency, software development firm, law firm, consultancy.
- **Goal:** Earn project-based or retainer fees by serving specific clients.
- **Growth:** Headcount-dependent. The more projects you take, the more people you usually need to hire, which makes rapid scaling difficult.
- **Funding:** Can be started with very little capital. Work often begins with client advance payments.
- **Risk:** Comparatively low.

## 4. E-commerce or Online Shop
This means selling products online or through social media such as Facebook or Instagram. It is often a modern or digital form of an SME.
- **Examples:** A platform like Daraz is a startup, but a Facebook clothing page or gadget shop is usually regular e-commerce or f-commerce.
- **Goal:** Buy or produce products, sell them online, and make a profit.
- **Growth:** Linear. If you move from 10 orders a day to 100, packaging and logistics costs also rise.
- **Funding:** Personal capital or bank loans.
- **Risk:** Inventory or stock may remain unsold.

## Which One Is Right for You?

- If you want to **grow fast, raise large investment, and accept extreme risk**, build a **startup**.
- If you want to **make profit from day one and grow steadily with independence**, start an **SME or e-commerce business**.
- If you want to **build a business by selling a specific skill, such as coding, design, or marketing**, start an **agency**.

Remember, none of these is inherently smaller or greater than the others. The right choice depends on your business goal, available capital, and risk appetite.
