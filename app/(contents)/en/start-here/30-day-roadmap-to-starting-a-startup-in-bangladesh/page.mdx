# 30-Day Roadmap to Starting a Startup in Bangladesh

> **In short:** Turning an idea into reality doesn't have to take years. Done right, 30 days is enough to get an idea to the point where the market can test it. This roadmap tells you, week by week, what to do on which day — all built for the realities of Bangladesh.

Let's fix the goal first. The aim of the first 30 days is not to build a big product. It's one thing only — to get into the market as fast as possible and learn how customers actually react. Whether people really want it, whether they'll pay for it — 30 days tells you a lot.

## The four weeks at a glance

| Week | Focus | Days | What you'll have |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Idea and problem validation | 1–7 | A clear problem, conversations with 10–15 people |
| 2 | Solution and legal basics | 8–14 | An MVP plan, a name, trade licence started |
| 3 | MVP and market prep | 15–21 | A small product, a plan for the first 10 customers |
| 4 | Launch and first customers | 22–30 | Soft launch, 5–10 paying customers, feedback |

## Week 1: Idea and problem validation

In the first week, don't build a product. Work only on the problem.

- **Days 1-2: Identify the problem.** What exactly are you trying to solve? Is it truly a serious problem, or does it only feel important to you?
- **Days 3-4: Talk to customers.** Talk to at least 10–15 people who face this problem. Ask how they solve it today. (See: [how to talk to customers](/en/phase-one/customer-discovery-interviews-in-bangladesh))
- **Days 5-6: Look at competitors.** Is anyone in Bangladesh or abroad working on this? How, and where are they weak?
- **Day 7: Decide.** Move forward only if the conversations show the problem is real and people are willing to pay for a solution.

## Week 2: Solution design and legal basics

This week, give the idea a structure.

- **Days 8-9: Plan the MVP.** An MVP is the smallest launchable version. Instead of a full app or website, think about the cheapest way to get your solution in front of customers — even a Facebook page or WhatsApp group.
- **Days 10-11: Name and branding.** Pick a simple name. If you need one, make a free logo with Canva.
- **Days 12-14: Basic legal and payments.** You don't need company registration at the start. But begin the [trade licence](/en/trade-license) process, and prepare to take money through a merchant bKash/Nagad or bank account.

## Week 3: Build the MVP and prepare for market

Now get the basic product ready to launch.

- **Days 15-17: Build the MVP.** If you need a landing page, make one with WordPress, Wix, or Framer. If it's a service, set your packages and pricing.
- **Days 18-19: Marketing plan.** Where will the first 10 customers come from — your network, Facebook groups, or a small-budget Facebook boost?
- **Days 20-21: Content and copy.** Write short posts or messages that explain your value proposition (exactly what the customer gains).

## Week 4: Launch and first customers

In the final week, take the MVP to market.

- **Days 22-24: Soft launch.** First share the product with people close to you, friends, or specific Facebook groups. Watch how they respond.
- **Days 25-27: First 5-10 paying customers.** Don't rely on free usage — get people to pay. The first sales prove there's real demand.
- **Days 28-29: Collect feedback.** Talk to the people who used it. Write down in detail what worked and what didn't.
- **Day 30: Plan the next step.** Decide what to change based on the feedback. If people clearly don't want it, move on fast to a new idea — failing fast to learn fast is the smart move here.

## 30-day checklist

- [ ] Written the problem and the customer in one line
- [ ] Talked to 10–15 potential customers
- [ ] Checked what competitors or alternatives exist
- [ ] Decided on the smallest MVP
- [ ] Started the trade licence and payment setup
- [ ] Done a soft launch
- [ ] Got at least 5 paying customers or firm commitments
- [ ] Made the next decision based on feedback
