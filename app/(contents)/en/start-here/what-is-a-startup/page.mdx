# What Is a Startup?

> **In short:** A startup is a new business built to solve a specific problem, with the goal of growing very quickly by using technology or a new business model.

## What Is the Difference Between a Startup and a Regular Business?

Many people think every new business is a startup. But opening a restaurant and building a platform like Pathao or Chaldal are very different business models.

### 1. Rapid Growth vs Linear Growth
A regular business, such as a clothing shop or grocery store, usually grows linearly. If one shop earns Tk 1 lakh per month, doubling revenue may require another shop and more staff.
By contrast, a startup is designed to grow exponentially. Because of software or technology, thousands of new customers can be added without costs rising at the same rate.

### 2. Solution and Innovation
Startups usually solve a problem that has not been solved before, or they bring a better, faster, or cheaper solution to an old problem.
For example, bKash addressed the long-standing challenge of sending money across Bangladesh by using mobile phones.

### 3. High Uncertainty
In a startup, it is often unclear at the beginning whether customers will actually buy the product. Startups work in unknown or uncharted markets, so the chance of failure is high alongside the chance of success. Regular businesses usually follow proven models, such as selling food through a restaurant.

### 4. Funding
Because startups aim to grow quickly, they often need significant capital early. This capital usually comes from venture capitalists or angel investors. They invest in exchange for company shares, hoping the company will become many times larger within a few years.

## Why Are Startups Important in Bangladesh?
Bangladesh has many foundational problems across transport, education, healthcare, logistics, agriculture, and more. Traditional businesses cannot always solve these problems efficiently. Startups use technology to solve them faster while also creating meaningful employment.

## Is Your Venture a Startup?
Ask yourself 3 questions:
1. Can my solution be distributed quickly across the country or beyond using technology?
2. Will my revenue grow much faster than my costs?
3. Am I solving a problem that others have not seriously addressed before?

If the answer is yes, welcome: you are on the path to building a startup.
