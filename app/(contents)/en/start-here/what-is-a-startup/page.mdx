# What Is a Startup?

> **In short:** A startup isn't just any new business. It's a venture that finds a new solution to a specific problem and is built to grow very fast using technology or a new model. That potential to scale quickly is what sets a startup apart from an ordinary business.

Say two people start a business. One opens a restaurant in the neighbourhood. The other builds an app that lets people all over the country order food from home. Both are businesses, but the maths inside them is different — the first is a regular business, the second is a startup. Here's where the difference lies.

## What a startup really means

A startup rests on two things: a new solution, and a design for growing fast. It goes after a problem that doesn't have a good solution yet, or brings something much faster or cheaper than the old way. And it's built so that ten times the customers doesn't cost ten times as much to serve. That's where technology comes in.

Take bKash. It solved the old headache of sending money to a remote village using just a mobile phone — a new solution — and rode the mobile network to spread across the country fast. When those two traits show up together, you have a startup.

## How it differs from a regular business

| Dimension | Regular business (SME) | Startup |
|---|---|---|
| Growth | Step by step, in a straight line | In leaps, fast |
| Market | Known, proven demand | New or still unproven |
| Risk | Moderate | Much higher |
| Capital | Own money or a bank | VCs / angels, in exchange for equity |
| Goal | Profit from the start | Grow fast first |

Three of those differences, unpacked:

- **How it grows.** To double a restaurant's income you need another branch and more cooks. A software product can take on a thousand new users at almost no extra cost. A startup is built in that second mould.
- **Uncertainty.** In a startup you don't know at the start whether people will actually buy — the market itself is unknown. Nobody needs fresh proof that a restaurant can sell food.
- **Funding.** Growing fast takes more capital up front, so a VC (venture capital) firm or an angel investor puts in money in exchange for equity (a share of ownership).

## How the government defines a startup

Bangladesh has an official definition too. Under Bangladesh Bank's startup finance policy (as of 2025), a startup is a company with annual turnover under BDT 100 crore, less than 12 years old, that uses technology or a new idea with the potential to grow fast. You have to fit this definition to apply for the government startup fund or low-interest startup loans.

## Why startups matter in Bangladesh

Bangladesh has no shortage of fundamental problems — transport, education, healthcare, logistics, agriculture. Traditional businesses can't solve all of them. Startups use technology to tackle these fast, and create a lot of jobs along the way. A founder's good solution, then, isn't just a business — it's a chance to ease a daily struggle for many people.

## Is your venture a startup?

Ask yourself three questions:

1. Can my solution spread fast across the country (or beyond) through technology?
2. As revenue grows, will costs grow much more slowly?
3. Have I picked a problem that doesn't have a good solution yet?

Three yeses? You're on the startup path. One or two nos? No need to worry — yours might make a great SME or service business that turns a profit from day one. To work out which fits you, see [which type of business is right for you](/en/start-here/startup-vs-sme-vs-agency-vs-e-commerce-business-in-bangladesh).

## A few common misconceptions

- **"A startup means an app."** An app is just a channel. bKash and Pathao run on apps, sure — but what made them startups is the model, not the app.
- **"Any new business is a startup."** A new tea stall is a new business too, but it isn't a startup.
- **"A startup means raising funding."** Funding is one means, not the goal. The real goal is building a model that grows fast.
