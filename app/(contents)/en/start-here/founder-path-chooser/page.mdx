# Founder Path Chooser

> **TL;DR:** This site isn't a novel you read from page one. Below are six familiar situations. Find yours, read its three or four pages in order, and forget the rest of the site for now. When your situation changes, come back here and pick up the next path.

Imagine someone sends you this site's link on Messenger at eleven at night. You open it and see licences, VAT, equity, couriers, hiring, funding. Hundreds of pages. The natural reaction: if I have to learn all this before starting, I'll never start. Good news: you don't. Think of the site as a pharmacy shelf. The shelf holds a thousand medicines; you need two. This page tells you which two.

Two questions are enough to find your spot. One: has anyone paid you yet, even a single taka? Two: what do you actually want in the next six months? Keep those answers in mind and pick your situation from the six below. If you fit more than one, take the earlier one. The paths connect to each other, so there's no way to get lost.

## 1. "I have an idea in my head, nothing more"

Class or office by day, a notebook full of ideas by night. No sales yet, no product either. You have exactly one job right now: test whether the idea stands up, as cheaply as possible.

Your path, in order:

1. [Should you build a startup or a regular business?](/en/start-here/startup-or-business) – settle which game you're playing first
2. [I have an idea but no product](/en/journeys/idea-but-no-product) – the first test, without building anything
3. [Idea validation](/en/validation) – the full section, when you want to go deeper

Not worth thinking about yet: company registration, a logo, an office, funding.

## 2. "I want to start alongside my job or studies"

You don't have the courage or the cushion to quit yet. You don't need to. Starting on the side isn't weakness, it's the cheapest laboratory there is. Your salary is your funding right now.

1. [Founder readiness checklist](/en/start-here/founder-readiness-checklist) – do the honest time-and-money maths first
2. [I want to test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand) – landing page to pre-orders, doable on weekends
3. [The 30-day roadmap](/en/start-here/30-day-roadmap) – a month's plan that runs on evenings and Fridays

Not worth thinking about yet: the resignation letter. The condition that triggers going full-time is on the checklist page.

## 3. "I'm selling on a Facebook page, time to get organised"

Say you are like Rahim, running a clothing page from Chattogram. Sixty or seventy orders a month, couriers coming and going, money landing in a personal bKash. A bank account, merchant payment service, or larger supplier now asks for business documents. It is time to organise them.

1. [I want to start an online business – licence to launch](/en/journeys/start-an-online-business) – the whole clean-up path at a glance
2. [Trade license](/en/trade-license) – how this document works for your area and activity
3. [I want to accept digital payments](/en/journeys/accept-digital-payments) – from personal bKash to a merchant account
4. [e-TIN, VAT and BIN](/en/e-tin-vat-bin) – which one you need when, and when you don't

Not worth thinking about yet: a private limited company or VC funding. Are you the only owner with no plan to issue shares? Sole proprietorship may fit. Compare the options in [Company types](/en/company-types).

## 4. "I'm building a tech product, team and funding ahead"

You are building software or an app, perhaps with a co-founder. Your head is full of investors, equity, and large markets. This path has one trap: thinking about funding before customers. A good presentation matters, but it is not enough. You also need evidence of a painful problem, usage, or willingness to pay.

1. [Company types](/en/company-types) – why structure comes first if you want investors
2. [I want to register a private limited company](/en/journeys/register-a-private-limited-company) – the full process, step by step
3. [Funding roadmap](/en/funding) – where startup money in Bangladesh actually comes from
4. [I want to raise angel or VC funding](/en/journeys/raise-angel-vc-funding) – from preparation to term sheet

Not worth thinking about yet: an offshore holding company, a big office, press coverage. And without hard customer evidence, shelve pages 3 and 4 – do [idea validation](/en/validation) first.

## 5. "I run a service or coaching business, now I want a product"

Take Nasrin's situation: a coaching centre running in Sylhet, with real income. But the income comes from selling hours. Teach a class and get paid. Do not teach, and the income stops. She is therefore considering an online course. Many students could use the same content, although sales and support work remain. The agency-to-software-product story is similar.

1. [Startup vs SME vs agency vs e-commerce](/en/start-here/startup-vs-sme) – where the service game and the product game differ
2. [I want to test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand) – your old clients are the first test bed
3. [Finding customers](/en/customers) – reaching buyers beyond your existing circle

Building software? Also see: [I want to launch a SaaS from Bangladesh](/en/journeys/launch-a-saas)

Not worth thinking about yet: shutting down the service overnight. That income is your runway. Before reducing it, calculate how many months you can operate while the product is still proving revenue.

## 6. "I live abroad and want to build something back home"

You're in London, Dubai or Kuala Lumpur, watching the market back home. You have some money and an idea. The real problem is distance: who runs the paperwork, how the banking gets done, and who your trusted person on the ground is.

1. [Building in Bangladesh as a diaspora founder](/en/journeys/diaspora-founder-building-in-bangladesh) – the full remote-start path
2. [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem) – who's doing what at home, and where to plug in
3. [Legal roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap) – which paperwork comes at which stage, so you know in advance

Not worth thinking about yet: a return date. Run a small pilot from where you are first; the big decisions come after.

## None of these fit?

Use a two-line rule. If you have not sold anything yet, examine situation 1 or 2 first. If sales are running, compare situation 3. Your goal may already be specific, such as accepting payments, making a first hire, or importing products. In that case, go straight to the [journeys section](/en/journeys), where paths are organised by goal.

Let's end back at the pharmacy shelf. Nobody buys medicine in advance; you buy it the day you need it. Same with this site. Rule of thumb: **don't read everything and then start. Start, and read each page the day you need it.**
