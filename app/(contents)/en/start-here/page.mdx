# Start Here – A Guide to Starting a Startup or Small Business in Bangladesh

> **In short:** Business does not start with opening a company on day one. First, understand whose problem you are solving, whether they care enough to pay, and what you can test in a small, low-risk way. Then handle registration, payments, accounting, team, funding and scale in the right order.

If your goal is already clear – like starting an online business, registering a company, taking payments, or raising funding – the [Journeys](/en/journeys) section gives you step-by-step paths by goal, telling you which guide to read at each step.

## Why Bangladesh Needs Local Business Guidance

- Bangladesh has a population of around 170 million, and digital adoption is growing quickly.
- Many businesses start through Facebook, Messenger, WhatsApp, mobile wallets, bank transfer or cash on delivery before they have formal systems.
- Government programs, ecosystem players and startup-support platforms are becoming more active, but rules and paperwork still need care.
- Competition is uneven in many categories, so a focused niche product, service or local business can gain traction quickly.

## Your First Jobs

1. **Choose a problem** – What specific problem do you want to solve?
2. **Understand the customer** – Who experiences this problem?
3. **Understand the Bangladesh context** – How do payments, trust, distribution, and regulation work here?
4. **Validate the idea** – Do not only think about it; test it with real people.
5. **Review the legal and registration roadmap** – Once the idea is validated, prepare for entity setup, licenses, tax and payments.
