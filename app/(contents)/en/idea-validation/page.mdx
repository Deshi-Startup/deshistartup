# Idea Validation

> **In short:** Before starting a startup, make sure people truly want your solution and are willing to pay for it. In Bangladesh, the biggest mistake is building the product first and only later checking whether customers exist.

---

## Where Do Ideas Come From?

Good startup ideas do not usually come from thinking, “Let’s build an app.” They come from:

### 1. Your Own Problems

Problems you personally face can be a strong source of ideas.

**Examples:**

- Having to call home every day to order a gas cylinder → delivery solutions like Pathao or RedX
- Struggling to find rental housing in Dhaka → Bashabo/BashaBari-type solutions

### 2. Problems You See Around You

What do people around you complain about?

**What to do:**

- Ask friends, family, and classmates: “What is the most annoying task in your daily life?”
- Watch what people complain about in Facebook groups.
- Talk to shopkeepers when you visit local markets.

### 3. Data

Look at what is happening in a sector:

- **BBS (Bangladesh Bureau of Statistics)** — Population, income, urbanization data
- **DataReportal** — Internet, social media, mobile usage data
- **Bangladesh Bank** — Payment, remittance, banking trends
- **LightCastle Partners** — Sector reports and market research

## 5 Steps to Validate an Idea

### Step 1: Clarify the Problem

What **problem** does your idea solve? Write it in one line:

```text
My idea solves [this problem] for [these people].
```

**Example:**

```text
My idea solves the problem of ordering wholesale products online
for small shopkeepers outside Dhaka.
```

**Check:**

- [ ] Does the problem really exist? Are people complaining about it?
- [ ] How big is the problem? How many people face it?
- [ ] Are people solving this problem somehow today? Are there competitors or alternatives?

### Step 2: Talk to People (Customer Discovery)

This is the most important step. Talk to at least **20-30 potential customers**.

**What to ask:**

| Ask | Do not ask |
|-----|------------|
| “Did you do this task last week?” | “Would you buy this product?” |
| “How do you solve this problem now?” | “How do you like my idea?” |
| “How much do you spend on this problem?” | “How much would you pay?” |
| “What is the most annoying part?” | “Do you need this feature?” |

> **Golden rule:** Trust behavior, not words. If someone says “Great idea!” but never uses it, the idea still has a problem.

**Where to find people:**

- Facebook Groups for relevant communities
- Local markets or bazaars, where you can talk directly to shopkeepers
- University campuses if you are a student
- LinkedIn for professionals
- Messenger/WhatsApp for people you already know

### Step 3: Study Competitors

Does something like your idea already exist?

**How to search:**

1. Search on **Google** using problem keywords.
2. Search **Facebook**, because many Bangladesh businesses operate only through Facebook pages.
3. Check the **Play Store / App Store** for apps.
4. Ask friends: “Do you use any service like this?”

**Do not be afraid if competitors exist.** Instead, study:

- What are they doing wrong?
- What complaints do customers have about them?
- What can you do **differently**?

### Step 4: Test Small (MVP Test)

Before building the full product, test with the **smallest possible version**.

**MVP examples in Bangladesh:**

| Product idea | MVP (smallest test) |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Food delivery app | Deliver 10 orders using a Facebook page + WhatsApp |
| Online tutoring platform | Teach 5 students yourself on Zoom |
| SaaS for shops | Manage accounts for 5 shops using an Excel sheet |
| E-commerce niche | Post 10 products in a Facebook Group and see whether anyone buys |

**What to learn from the MVP:**

- [ ] Do people really want this?
- [ ] Are they willing to pay?
- [ ] How much are they willing to pay?
- [ ] Which features are actually needed?

### Step 5: Validate with Numbers

Work out a few basic numbers:

```text
Total Addressable Market (TAM) = How many people might have this problem?
Serviceable Market = How many people can you realistically serve?
Revenue per customer = How much money will you earn from each customer on average?
```

**Think in the Bangladesh context:**

- Population is 170 million+, but internet users are around 130 million.
- Smartphone users are growing, but many people still use feature phones.
- Card penetration is low, around 4%, so payment methods matter.
- Distribution outside Dhaka is different.

## Bangladesh-Specific Validation Checklist

- [ ] **Language:** Do customers speak Bangla or English? What language will your product use?
- [ ] **Payment:** How will customers pay: bKash, Nagad, COD, bank transfer?
- [ ] **Trust:** Trust in new brands is low in Bangladesh. How will you build trust?
- [ ] **Distribution:** Where are your customers: Facebook, markets, another channel? How will you reach them?
- [ ] **Competition:** Are Chinese products or India-based services already in this market?
- [ ] **Regulation:** Does this business require any government license?
- [ ] **Seasonality:** Will Eid, monsoon, exams, or other seasons affect the business?

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Better approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| “My idea is unique; no one has done it.” | Almost every idea has competitors. Think about how you will be different. |
| “Let’s build it and see if anyone buys.” | Validate first, then build. |
| “Everyone likes my idea.” | Family and friends may be polite. Listen to unknown customers. |
| “I will build an app.” | Validate the problem first. An app may not be necessary. |
| “The market is huge; everyone is my customer.” | Choose a specific audience. “Everyone” usually means “no one.” |

## Key Takeaways from This Chapter

1. **Ideas are cheap; execution is expensive** — validate first.
2. **Talk to people** — not only online surveys, but direct conversations.
3. **Start with an MVP** — a Facebook page, WhatsApp group, or simple landing page.
4. **Look at the numbers** — customers, money, market size.
5. **Do not ignore the Bangladesh context** — payment, trust, and distribution are different here.
