# I Want to Build an Agritech Startup – From Field to Market

> **Summary:** Agritech doesn't happen from an office. Farmers, middlemen, wholesale arots, dealers – until you get inside that chain, you won't even see the real problem. And everything runs on seasons: boro, aman, winter vegetables. This path takes you from field research through a one-season pilot and the agent model to funding.

Say you want to help farmers get fair prices for their crops. Noble idea – but the questions pile up. Why would a farmer trust you? The middleman pays cash on the spot; can you? How does someone without a smartphone use your app? The answers aren't in a Dhaka café. They're in the field.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the sector.** Map the roles around inputs, credit, crop sales, storage and advice before choosing a problem. → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem)
2. **Go to the field and listen in the farmer's language.** Talk to at least 20–30 farmers and 5 middlemen or arot traders. Who gets paid when, who gets cheated, who carries the risk – draw the chain. → [Idea validation](/en/validation)
3. **Pilot one season in one area.** Not the whole country – one crop in one upazila. Deliver the service by hand; the app comes later. → [Test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand)
4. **Test an agent model.** Check whether a local young person, dealer or cooperative member can help reach farmers and what their incentives would be. → [Finding customers](/en/customers)
5. **Map the money.** Ask farmers and businesses whether they use cash, MFS or banks. State who is responsible if money is delayed. → [Payments](/en/payments)
6. **Do the paperwork in stages.** Regular buying and selling means a trade license and TIN. Selling agricultural inputs (seed, fertilizer, pesticide) needs separate approvals – verify before you start. → [Legal roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap)
7. **Go deeper into the sector.** There's a dedicated agritech playbook (the guide is still to be written – its page lists sources). → [Agritech startup playbook](/en/industries/agritech)
8. **Think about growth money.** Compare the terms of grants, debt, impact funds and equity. → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Sat down with at least 20 farmers in the field
- [ ] Mapped the full chain – who buys, who sells, where money gets stuck
- [ ] Have results from a one-season, one-area pilot
- [ ] Lined up agents or local partners
- [ ] Keeping cash and mobile-banking accounts separate
- [ ] Listed which approvals my model needs
