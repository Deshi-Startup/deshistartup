# I Want to Build an Edtech Startup – From Teaching to Earning Trust

> **Summary:** In edtech, the user and payer may be different people. Learn what the student should achieve, what evidence the parent expects and who decides. Test teaching, payment, outcomes and refunds through one subject and one batch.

Coaching, tutors, books and admission preparation may be the current alternatives. Interview the specific customer to learn what they use, pay and dislike. Use your own data to test whether the exam calendar changes demand.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the market.** Where edtech stands in Bangladesh, who the big players are, and where the gaps sit – see the big picture first. → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem)
2. **Validate with both sides.** Where students get stuck and what parents will pay for are often not the same thing. Talk to at least 20 parents and 20 students. → [Idea validation](/en/validation)
3. **Test with one batch.** Do not build the platform yet. Start with one subject, one batch, live classes and a clear fee. Measure use, payment and return for a second batch separately. → [Test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand)
4. **Bring in the first students.** Parents' Facebook groups, the streets around schools and colleges, referrals from past students – trust channels first. → [Finding customers](/en/customers)
5. **Organize fee collection.** Monthly fees via bKash/Nagad or bank? What's the refund rule? Write it down before you need it. → [Payments](/en/payments)
6. **Do the paperwork in stages.** Regular fee collection means a trade license and TIN. And be careful: claiming to issue "certificates" or "degrees" raises accreditation questions – don't promise what you don't have. → [Legal roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap)
7. **Go deeper into the sector.** There's a dedicated playbook on winning parents' trust (the guide is still to be written – its page lists sources). → [Edtech trust & parent acquisition playbook](/en/industries/edtech)
8. **Think about growth money.** Batches filling, students returning – now grants or investors are worth considering. → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Have interview notes from 20 parents and 20 students
- [ ] Ran a one-batch pilot and charged a fee
- [ ] Decided how to show student results (test scores, progress reports)
- [ ] Written fee, refund and payment rules
- [ ] Planned the trade license and TIN
- [ ] Tracking how many students return for the second batch
