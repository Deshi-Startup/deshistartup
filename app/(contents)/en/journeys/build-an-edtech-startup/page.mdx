# I Want to Build an Edtech Startup – From Teaching to Earning Trust

> **Summary:** Edtech's central puzzle: one person pays (the parent), another uses the product (the student). You have to win both. And if you can't show results, the second month's fee never arrives. This path takes you from picking the problem through a one-batch pilot, payments and paperwork to funding.

Bangladeshi families spend heavily on education — coaching centers, private tutors, guide books, admission prep. Demand is proven. The real question is different: can you offer something better or cheaper than what coaching and tutors already deliver? Demand also swings with the exam calendar — keep that in your math.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the market.** Where edtech stands in Bangladesh, who the big players are, and where the gaps sit — see the big picture first. → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem-overview)
2. **Validate with both sides.** Where students get stuck and what parents will pay for are often not the same thing. Talk to at least 20 parents and 20 students. → [Idea validation](/en/idea-validation)
3. **Test with one batch.** Don't build the platform. One subject, one batch, live classes — teach for a fee. Twenty paying students beat a thousand likes. → [Test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand)
4. **Bring in the first students.** Parents' Facebook groups, the streets around schools and colleges, referrals from past students — trust channels first. → [Finding customers](/en/customers)
5. **Organize fee collection.** Monthly fees via bKash/Nagad or bank? What's the refund rule? Write it down before you need it. → [Payments](/en/payments)
6. **Do the paperwork in stages.** Regular fee collection means a trade license and TIN. And be careful: claiming to issue "certificates" or "degrees" raises accreditation questions — don't promise what you don't have. → [Legal roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap)
7. **Go deeper into the sector.** There's a dedicated playbook on winning parents' trust (the guide is still to be written — its page lists sources). → [Edtech trust & parent acquisition playbook](/en/phase-four/edtech-trust-and-parent-acquisition-playbook)
8. **Think about growth money.** Batches filling, students returning — now grants or investors are worth considering. → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding-roadmap)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Have interview notes from 20 parents and 20 students
- [ ] Ran a one-batch pilot and charged a fee
- [ ] Decided how to show student results (test scores, progress reports)
- [ ] Written fee, refund and payment rules
- [ ] Planned the trade license and TIN
- [ ] Tracking how many students return for the second batch
