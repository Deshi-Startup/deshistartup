# I want to import products

> **In short:** Before importing, understand the IRC (Import Registration Certificate), HS code, LC, and duty/VAT math — otherwise goods get stuck at the port and costs balloon. This path lays out the steps from choosing a product to clearance.

This path is for anyone who wants to bring products from abroad to sell or use in Bangladesh. Every step involves a government process, so relying on official sources and a C&F agent is the smart move.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand import/export registration basics.** Learn what registrations you need first. → [Import/export registration basics](/en/phase-one/importexport-registration-basics)
2. **Match the IRC/ERC checklist.** Verify what documents the IRC requires. → [IRC/ERC checklist](/en/phase-one/ircerc-checklist)
3. **Get the IRC/ERC step by step.** Follow the application process. → [Step-by-step IRC/ERC guide](/en/phase-one/step-by-step-guide-to-ircerc)
4. **Find the right HS code.** Duties and rules depend on the HS code. → [Finding the right HS code](/en/phase-one/how-to-find-the-right-hs-code)
5. **Prepare the LC and bank documents.** Understand LC/LCAF and documents to pay the supplier. → [LC, LCAF & bank documentation basics](/en/phase-one/lc-lcaf-and-bank-documentation-basics)
6. **Choose a freight forwarder.** Compare and pick air, sea, or courier. → [Choosing a freight forwarder](/en/phase-one/choosing-a-freight-forwarder-air-sea-and-courier)
7. **Calculate landed cost.** Work out the true cost with duty, VAT, freight, and clearing. → [Landed cost calculator](/en/phase-one/landed-cost-calculator-duty-vat-freight-and-clearing)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Fixed the product category and supplier
- [ ] Got (or applied for) an IRC
- [ ] Found the correct HS code
- [ ] Prepared the LC and bank documents
- [ ] Calculated landed cost and set the price
