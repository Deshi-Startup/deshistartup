# I am a diaspora founder building in Bangladesh

> **In short:** A diaspora founder's biggest strengths are network and capital; the biggest risks are distance and trust. This path takes you from market thesis through a local operator, company/FDI setup, and funding.

This path is for non-resident Bangladeshi (NRB) founders who want to build a business or invest at home. The keys to success are a reliable local operator/co-founder and understanding FDI and foreign-exchange rules in advance.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand diaspora-founder constraints.** Recognize the real challenges of distance and rules. → [Diaspora founder constraints](/en/founder-life/diaspora-founder-constraints)
2. **Use your advantages.** Learn how to leverage network, capital, and experience. → [Diaspora founder advantages](/en/founder-life/diaspora-founder-advantages)
3. **Set up a local operator/co-founder.** A trustworthy partner on the ground is the foundation. → [Diaspora co-founder & local operator relationship](/en/founder-life/diaspora-co-founder-and-local-operator-relationship)
4. **Understand foreign-owned company setup.** Learn the rules for opening a foreign-owned company. → [Foreign-owned company basics](/en/phase-one/basics-of-starting-a-foreign-owned-company-in-bangladesh)
5. **Comply with FDI rules.** Understand foreign investment and diaspora-funding rules. → [FDI rules & diaspora funding](/en/phase-three/foreign-investmentfdi-rules-and-diaspora-funding)
6. **Set up banking and foreign exchange.** Learn the rules for moving money and shareholding. → [Foreign exchange basics](/en/phase-one/foreign-exchange-basics-for-saas-export-remittance-and-foreign-shareholders)
7. **Use the diaspora-funding path.** Explore NRB/diaspora investment opportunities. → [NRB/diaspora funding path](/en/phase-three/nrb-or-diaspora-funding-path)

- [ ] Understood company and FDI setup rules
- [ ] Prepared for banking and foreign exchange
- [ ] Planned for trust and distribution
