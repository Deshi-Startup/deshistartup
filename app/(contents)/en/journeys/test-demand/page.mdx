# I want to test demand – from landing page to pre-orders

> **In short:** "Will people buy?" is answered by small tests, not by building the product. This path takes you from an assumption through a landing page, interviews, and pre-orders to a clear go/no-go decision.

This path is for founders whose idea is fairly clear but who want evidence before spending serious time or money. Record “nice idea” as praise; base the decision on use, an advance, a pilot or another concrete commitment.

## The path, step by step

1. **Write the assumption and plan the demand test.** Decide what evidence counts as a "yes." → [Validate demand without building](/en/validation/demand-without-building)
2. **Prepare your interview script.** Use Bangla and English questions that surface real behavior. → [Customer interview scripts](/en/validation/interview-scripts)
3. **Build a simple landing page.** Include the offer, price, and a way to capture interest. → [Landing page template](/en/customers/landing-page-template)
4. **Test demand in Facebook groups.** Gauge response where your potential customers already are. → [Testing demand in Facebook groups](/en/validation/facebook-group-tests)
5. **Run a small-budget Facebook ad test.** Read interest from click and sign-up rates. → [Facebook ad test planner](/en/customers/facebook-ad-planner)
6. **Choose pre-orders vs a waitlist.** Separate strong signals (advance payment) from weak ones (just a name). → [Pre-orders vs waitlists](/en/validation/preorders-vs-waitlists)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Defined "success" as a number (e.g. 10 pre-orders)
- [ ] Published a landing page or post
- [ ] Talked to at least 10 people and kept notes
- [ ] Tried to collect advance payment or a written commitment
- [ ] Made a continue / pivot / stop decision from the results
