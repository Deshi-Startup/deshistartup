# I Want to Build a Travel Tech Startup – Where Trust Is the Product

> **Summary:** Domestic travel is growing — Cox's Bazar, Sajek, Sylhet, the Sundarbans. But in the travel business the customer pays first and gets the service later. That gap is the biggest obstacle and the biggest opportunity at once: whoever wins trust wins the market. This path takes you from segment selection through a one-route pilot and refund policy to funding.

Imagine someone before the Eid holidays hunting for a family package to Sajek. Ten Facebook pages, all shouting "best offer." Who gets the 10,000-taka advance? The pain of that decision is travel tech's real problem — not price, trust.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the market.** Domestic tours, corporate trips, Hajj-Umrah, visa support, hotel booking — travel contains several distinct businesses. Which gap is yours? → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem-overview)
2. **Pick one traveller type and validate.** A student group tour and a family resort holiday differ in demand, budget and fears. Talk to 20–30 travellers of one type: what went wrong on your last trip? → [Idea validation](/en/idea-validation)
3. **Pilot one route, one package.** Don't build the whole OTA (online travel agency). Sell one route's package through a Facebook page and Messenger — and run the operations yourself. → [Test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand)
4. **Stockpile proof of trust.** Real photos from every trip, reviews with names, a phone number, an address — all public. The first ten trips are for reviews, not profit. → [Finding customers](/en/customers)
5. **Make advance and refund rules explicit.** What percentage in advance, who gets how much back if the trip is cancelled, within how many days — in writing, before booking. When a storm cancels a trip, your refund behaviour *is* your brand. → [Payments](/en/payments)
6. **Do the paperwork in stages.** Start with the trade license and TIN. Operating as a travel agency (selling tickets and packages) has government registration rules — verify what your specific services require. → [Legal roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap)
7. **Go deeper into the sector.** There's a dedicated tourism and travel business guide (still to be written — its page lists sources). → [Tourism & travel startup guide](/en/phase-four/tourism-and-travel-startup-guide)
8. **Think about growth money.** Bookings arriving steadily through the seasonal swings — now run the funding math. → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding-roadmap)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Written in one line which traveller I'm building for
- [ ] Talked to 20–30 travellers about their last trip's problems
- [ ] Ran a one-route pilot and personally handled at least 10 bookings
- [ ] Advance, cancellation and refund rules written and published on the page
- [ ] Collecting reviews and photos from every trip
- [ ] Tracking seasonal demand swings (Eid, winter, exam season)
