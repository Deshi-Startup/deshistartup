# I Want to Build a Travel Tech Startup – Where Trust Is the Product

> **Summary:** In travel, a customer may pay before receiving the service. Identity checks, written booking terms, supplier confirmation, cancellation and refunds are therefore part of the product. Test one traveller type and one route before deciding to expand.

Imagine someone before the Eid holidays hunting for a family package to Sajek. Ten Facebook pages, all shouting "best offer." Who gets the 10,000-taka advance? The pain of that decision is travel tech's real problem – not price, trust.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the market.** Domestic tours, corporate trips, Hajj-Umrah, visa support, hotel booking – travel contains several distinct businesses. Which gap is yours? → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](../ecosystem-overview)
2. **Pick one traveller type and validate.** A student group tour and a family resort holiday differ in demand, budget and fears. Talk to 20–30 travellers of one type: what went wrong on your last trip? → [Idea validation](../idea-validation)
3. **Pilot one route, one package.** Don't build the whole OTA (online travel agency). Sell one route's package through a Facebook page and Messenger – and run the operations yourself. → [Test demand](../journeys/test-demand)
4. **Build evidence of trust.** Show genuine trip images and reviews with permission. Make business contact, booking confirmation and supplier information clear. Understand the pilot's costs as well. → [Finding customers](../customers)
5. **Make advance and refund rules explicit.** What percentage in advance, who gets how much back if the trip is cancelled, within how many days – in writing, before booking. When a storm cancels a trip, your refund behaviour *is* your brand. → [Payments](../payments)
6. **Do the paperwork in stages.** Start with the trade license and TIN. Operating as a travel agency (selling tickets and packages) has government registration rules – verify what your specific services require. → [Legal roadmap](../legal-roadmap)
7. **Go deeper into the sector.** There's a dedicated tourism and travel business guide (still to be written – its page lists sources). → [Tourism & travel startup guide](../phase-four/tourism-and-travel-startup-guide)
8. **Think about growth money.** Bookings arriving steadily through the seasonal swings – now run the funding math. → [Funding roadmap](../funding-roadmap)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Written in one line which traveller I'm building for
- [ ] Talked to 20–30 travellers about their last trip's problems
- [ ] Ran a one-route pilot and personally handled at least 10 bookings
- [ ] Advance, cancellation and refund rules written and published on the page
- [ ] Collecting reviews and photos from every trip
- [ ] Tracking seasonal demand swings (Eid, winter, exam season)
