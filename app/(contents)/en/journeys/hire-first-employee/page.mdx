# I want to hire my first employee

> **TL;DR:** Before a first hire, write the work, result, budget and supervision. “Employee”, “freelancer” and “intern” are not labels chosen only for convenience; the real working relationship and labour law also matter. This path covers role, contract, pay and onboarding.

This path is for founders paying someone for work for the first time. Determine whether the work is ongoing under the company's control or an independent project with a defined result. Changing the label does not remove an employee's legal rights.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand hiring basics.** Learn what to consider before your first hire. → [Hiring your first employees](/en/team/first-hires)
2. **Build a role scorecard.** Write down what success in this role means. → [Hiring scorecard](/en/team/hiring-scorecard)
3. **Decide employee vs freelancer/intern.** Check which fits best right now. → [Employee vs freelancer vs intern](/en/team/employee-vs-freelancer)
4. **Do the employment contract.** Keep role, pay, and terms clear in writing. → [Employment contracts](/en/team/employment-contracts)
5. **Know the labor-law basics.** Follow the core rules of the Labour Act 2006. → [Labor law basics (Labour Act 2006)](/en/team/labor-law)
6. **Set up payroll.** Get the salary sheet, bank transfer, and tax right. → [Payroll process](/en/team/payroll)
7. **Onboard them.** Arrange the offer letter, probation, and joining steps. → [Offer letter, probation & joining checklist](/en/team/offer-letter)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Wrote the role and success measures
- [ ] Decided employee/freelancer/intern
- [ ] Prepared a written employment contract
- [ ] Prepared for pay, tax, and labor law
- [ ] Set the onboarding steps
