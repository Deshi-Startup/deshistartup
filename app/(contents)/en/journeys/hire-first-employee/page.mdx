# I want to hire my first employee

> **In short:** Get the first hire wrong and you lose time, money, and morale. This path takes you from clarifying the role through choosing the right type, contract, pay, and onboarding – within labor law.

This path is for founders hiring someone on salary for the first time. Decide up front whether you truly need a full-time employee, or whether a freelancer or intern is enough.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand hiring basics.** Learn what to consider before your first hire. → [Hiring your first employees](/en/phase-two/hiring-first-employees)
2. **Build a role scorecard.** Write down what success in this role means. → [Hiring scorecard](/en/phase-two/hiring-scorecard)
3. **Decide employee vs freelancer/intern.** Check which fits best right now. → [Employee vs freelancer vs intern](/en/phase-two/hiring-first-employee-vs-freelancer-vs-intern)
4. **Do the employment contract.** Keep role, pay, and terms clear in writing. → [Employment contracts](/en/phase-two/employment-contracts)
5. **Know the labor-law basics.** Follow the core rules of the Labour Act 2006. → [Labor law basics (Labour Act 2006)](/en/phase-two/labor-law-basics-bangladesh-labour-act-2006)
6. **Set up payroll.** Get the salary sheet, bank transfer, and tax right. → [Payroll process](/en/phase-two/payroll-process-salary-sheet-bank-transfer-and-tax)
7. **Onboard them.** Arrange the offer letter, probation, and joining steps. → [Offer letter, probation & joining checklist](/en/phase-two/offer-letter-probation-and-joining-checklist)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Wrote the role and success measures
- [ ] Decided employee/freelancer/intern
- [ ] Prepared a written employment contract
- [ ] Prepared for pay, tax, and labor law
- [ ] Set the onboarding steps
