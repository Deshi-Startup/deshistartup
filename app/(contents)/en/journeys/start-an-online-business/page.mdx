# I want to start an online business – license to launch

> **In short:** An online business is more than a Facebook page — you have to set up payments, courier, returns, bookkeeping, and the minimum legal paperwork together. This path takes you from choosing a business type through licensing, payments, and delivery to launch.

This path is for anyone who wants to start selling a product or service online — via a Facebook page, WhatsApp, or their own site. In Bangladesh most online sales run on cash-on-delivery and mobile wallets, so the legal and practical steps must be planned together.

## The path, step by step

1. **Decide your business type.** Startup, SME, or ordinary commerce — settle this first. → [Startup, SME, agency or e-commerce](/en/start-here/startup-vs-sme-vs-agency-vs-e-commerce-business-in-bangladesh)
2. **Get a trade license.** Get a license from the city corporation/union before selling regularly. → [Trade license](/en/trade-license)
3. **Understand e-TIN and VAT/BIN.** Handle the minimum tax and VAT prep. → [e-TIN & VAT/BIN](/en/e-tin-vat-bin)
4. **Set up payments.** Choose among bKash/Nagad, cash-on-delivery, and a gateway. → [Payments](/en/payments)
5. **Set up courier and delivery.** Pick a provider like Pathao, Steadfast, or RedX. → [Courier/logistics options](/en/phase-three/courierlogistics-options)
6. **Organize Facebook commerce and launch.** Arrange the page, inbox, order, and support flow. → [Facebook commerce playbook](/en/phase-three/facebook-commerce-playbook)
7. **Comply with the Digital Commerce policy.** Learn the mandatory rules for an online shop. → [Digital Commerce policy](/en/phase-three/digital-commerce-policy-and-what-it-requires-of-an-online-shop)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Fixed the business type and first product
- [ ] Got (or started) a trade license and e-TIN
- [ ] Chose payments (wallet/COD/gateway)
- [ ] Picked a courier provider and wrote a return policy
- [ ] Wrote out the refund, support, and order flow
