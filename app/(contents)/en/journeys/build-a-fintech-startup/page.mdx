# I Want to Build a Fintech Startup – Rules First, Product Second

> **Summary:** In other sectors you build the product first and sort the paperwork later – fintech is the reverse. Before touching people's money you need Bangladesh Bank approval, or a partnership with a licensed bank/MFS. A fintech built without understanding the rules isn't a business – it's a liability. This path takes you from the regulatory map through the partner-vs-license decision and validation to funding.

The upside is just as large. bKash and Nagad brought tens of millions into mobile banking, yet credit, savings, insurance and business bookkeeping remain full of unsolved pain. The small shopkeeper's credit ledger, the freelancer's foreign income, the garment worker's savings – each is a real problem. You just need to know which ones you're allowed to touch.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the sector.** Who's doing what in Bangladeshi fintech – payments, lending, insurance, accounting software – and where the gaps are. → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem-overview)
2. **Look at the regulatory map first.** Which services need which approvals, what a PSP/PSO license means – raise these questions at the start of the road, not the end (the guide is still to be written – its page lists sources). → [Fintech regulatory map](/en/phase-four/fintech-regulatory-map)
3. **Validate which money pain you're solving.** Whose money, at which moment, gets stuck how – talk to 20–30 potential users. Not "a wallet for everyone"; a specific pain for specific people. → [Idea validation](/en/idea-validation)
4. **Decide the model: license or partner.** Getting your own license is a long, expensive road. Most new fintechs start by riding on a bank, an MFS or a licensed gateway. And remember – business models that hold customers' money in your own account carry the highest risk of all. → [Payments](/en/payments)
5. **Set up the company structure early.** Bank agreements, Bangladesh Bank approvals, investor money – all of them require a registered company. In fintech this is not on the "can wait" list. → [Company types](/en/company-types) and [Register a private limited company](/en/journeys/register-a-private-limited-company)
6. **Bring in the first users.** In fintech, trust is everything – people don't put money into an unknown app. Start with a small, familiar community and give a receipt for every transaction. → [Finding customers](/en/customers)
7. **Prepare for funding.** Fintech investors check compliance before traction – which approvals you have, which you'll need, who your lawyer is. → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding-roadmap)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Written down which approvals my service needs – verified, not guessed
- [ ] Decided: own license or partnership
- [ ] Heard 20–30 users describe their money pain first-hand
- [ ] Clear on where customer money sits and in whose custody
- [ ] Planned the company registration
- [ ] Lined up an experienced lawyer or advisor
