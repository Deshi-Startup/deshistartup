# I Want to Build a Fintech Startup – Rules First, Product Second

> **Summary:** “Fintech” does not imply one license. Accounting software, money transmission, custody of customer funds, lending and insurance have different rules. Before building, define which part of the money flow the service touches, which regulator applies and whether your own approval or a licensed partner is required.

A small shop's credit ledger, a freelancer's foreign income or a worker's savings may be worth researching. Validate the problem separately from whether the proposed solution is legally permitted.

## The path, step by step

1. **Know the sector.** Who's doing what in Bangladeshi fintech – payments, lending, insurance, accounting software – and where the gaps are. → [Bangladesh startup ecosystem](/en/ecosystem)
2. **Look at the regulatory map first.** Which services need which approvals, what a PSP/PSO license means – raise these questions at the start of the road, not the end (the guide is still to be written – its page lists sources). → [Fintech regulatory map](/en/industries/fintech-regulations)
3. **Validate which money pain you're solving.** Whose money, at which moment, gets stuck how – talk to 20–30 potential users. Not "a wallet for everyone"; a specific pain for specific people. → [Idea validation](/en/validation)
4. **Verify license or partner options.** A regulated service may require your own approval or a licensed bank, MFS or payment partner. Do not hold customer money in an ordinary business account without advice from a qualified lawyer and the relevant regulator. → [Payments](/en/payments)
5. **Choose the entity.** Ask a bank or regulated partner which entity and records it requires before contracting. → [Company types](/en/company-types) and [Register a private limited company](/en/journeys/register-a-private-limited-company)
6. **Test without holding money.** Where possible, use interviews, sample reports or a licensed partner's sandbox to test demand. → [Finding customers](/en/customers)
7. **Close compliance gaps before funding.** Record which approvals exist, which remain and which professional advised you. → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Written down which approvals my service needs – verified, not guessed
- [ ] Decided: own license or partnership
- [ ] Heard 20–30 users describe their money pain first-hand
- [ ] Clear on where customer money sits and in whose custody
- [ ] Planned the company registration
- [ ] Lined up an experienced lawyer or advisor
