# I want to sell to SMEs

> **TL;DR:** SMEs do not all buy the same way. An owner may decide in one business; an accountant, manager or procurement team may be involved in another. Write the ideal customer and decision path, then test use and payment through a small pilot.

This path is for founders selling products or software to shops, factories, trading houses or service firms. Learn the current ledger or software, decision-maker, budget and buying cycle for the specific customer.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand the reality of SME sales.** Learn why selling to SMEs is different. → [SME sales in Bangladesh](/en/b2b/sme-sales)
2. **Recognize the digitization barriers.** Understanding the hesitation makes your offer easier to shape. → [SME digitization barriers](/en/b2b/sme-digitization)
3. **Build a trust-first offer.** The first bit of trust is the hardest in a low-trust market. → [Trust-building in low-trust markets](/en/customers/trust-building)
4. **Set your sales call script.** Use a script that speaks the owner's language. → [SME sales call script](/en/b2b/sme-call-script)
5. **Follow up with B2B scripts.** Fix the structure of your emails and messages. → [B2B sales scripts](/en/b2b/sales-scripts)
6. **Propose a paid contract after the pilot.** State the success condition, duration and next price before the pilot begins. → [From corporate pilot to paid contract](/en/b2b/pilot-to-contract)
7. **Build a retention dashboard.** Track who stayed and who left, month over month. → [Monthly founder dashboard](/en/metrics/founder-dashboard)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Wrote the ideal customer (ICP) in one line
- [ ] Prepared a trust-building offer/proof
- [ ] Set the sales script and follow-up
- [ ] Ran at least one pilot
- [ ] Started a dashboard to measure retention
