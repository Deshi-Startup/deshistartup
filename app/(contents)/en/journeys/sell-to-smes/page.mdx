# I want to sell to SMEs

> **In short:** In Bangladesh, SMEs decide slowly, the owner decides personally, and there's hesitation about going digital. This path takes you from picking the right customer through building trust, running a pilot, and reaching recurring revenue.

This path is for founders selling a product or software to small and medium businesses (shops, factories, trading houses, service firms). The biggest barriers are low digitization and owner-led decisions — so trust and proof come first.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand the reality of SME sales.** Learn why selling to SMEs is different. → [SME sales in Bangladesh](/en/phase-three/sme-sales-in-bangladesh)
2. **Recognize the digitization barriers.** Understanding the hesitation makes your offer easier to shape. → [SME digitization barriers](/en/phase-three/sme-digitization-barriers)
3. **Build a trust-first offer.** The first bit of trust is the hardest in a low-trust market. → [Trust-building in low-trust markets](/en/phase-three/trust-building-in-low-trust-markets)
4. **Set your sales call script.** Use a script that speaks the owner's language. → [SME sales call script](/en/phase-three/sme-sales-call-script)
5. **Follow up with B2B scripts.** Fix the structure of your emails and messages. → [B2B sales scripts](/en/phase-three/b2b-sales-scripts)
6. **Move from pilot to paid contract.** Convert a free pilot into paid revenue. → [From corporate pilot to paid contract](/en/phase-three/from-corporate-pilot-to-paid-contract)
7. **Build a retention dashboard.** Track who stayed and who left, month over month. → [Monthly founder dashboard](/en/phase-three/monthly-founder-dashboard)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Wrote the ideal customer (ICP) in one line
- [ ] Prepared a trust-building offer/proof
- [ ] Set the sales script and follow-up
- [ ] Ran at least one pilot
- [ ] Started a dashboard to measure retention
