# I Want to Build a Delivery, Ride or Local Services Startup

> **Summary:** Delivery, rides and home services require field operations alongside software. Scaling before understanding the income and cost of one trip or order can scale the loss too. Test in a small area, study both customers and providers, then decide whether to expand.

Bike servicing, house moving, finding a technician, laundry and medicine delivery are possible areas to research. A marketplace model must match customers and providers in the same place and time.

## The path, step by step

1. **Read Pathao's story first.** How this model got built in Bangladesh and which decisions worked – start from the homegrown example. → [Pathao case study](/en/case-studies/pathao)
2. **Validate the problem and the area.** Who do people call today for this service, what do they pay, what goes wrong? Talk to 20–30 customers and 10 providers (riders, technicians, delivery workers). → [Idea validation](/en/validation)
3. **Run a dense single-area pilot.** Not all of Dhaka – pick one area (say Mirpur, or Nasirabad in Chattogram) and run it by hand. Orders come by phone and Messenger; the app comes later. → [Test demand](/en/journeys/test-demand)
4. **Make the per-trip math work.** Revenue per trip, the provider's cut, what's left for you – including fuel, commission and phone costs. If that math doesn't point to profit, fix the model before growing. → [Start here](/en/start-here)
5. **Listen to providers.** Ask about payment timing, commission, safety, availability of work and complaint handling. → [Hire your first employee](/en/journeys/hire-first-employee)
6. **Organise cash movement.** If a provider collects cash, state who deposits it, when, what evidence is kept and who reconciles it. → [Payments](/en/payments)
7. **Do the paperwork in stages.** Start with the trade license and TIN. Regulated services like ride-sharing have separate rules – verify before launching. → [Legal roadmap](/en/legal-roadmap)
8. **Go deeper and think about funding.** There's a dedicated logistics playbook (still to be written – its page lists sources) → [Logistics startup playbook](/en/industries/logistics). Once the math works, look for growth money → [Funding roadmap](/en/funding)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Talked to both sides – customers and providers
- [ ] Ran a hand-operated single-area pilot
- [ ] Written per-trip or per-order income and cost numbers
- [ ] Set provider commission and payout rules
- [ ] Built cash deposit and reconciliation rules
- [ ] Verified whether the service is regulated
