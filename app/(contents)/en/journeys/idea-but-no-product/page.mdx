# I have an idea but no product – the first validation path

> **In short:** Don't jump straight from an idea to building an app or website. First validate cheaply whether the problem is real and whether people will pay for a solution. This path takes you from a bare idea to a first validated MVP.

This path is for founders who have an idea but no product, customers, or proof yet. The goal is simple – learn the most for the least cost, so you don't burn months on the wrong idea.

## The path, step by step

At each step, read the linked guide and get the task done, then move to the next step.

1. **Understand the idea of validation.** Why validate first, build later. → [Idea validation](/en/validation)
2. **Check that your idea sits on a real problem.** → [Finding startup ideas](/en/ideas/finding-ideas)
3. **Talk to potential customers.** Use twenty to thirty conversations as a starting target, then look for repeated experience and disagreement rather than chasing the number. → [Customer discovery interviews](/en/validation/customer-interviews)
4. **Decide which problem to solve first.** Pick the most urgent among several. → [Problem ranking scorecard](/en/ideas/problem-ranking-scorecard)
5. **Test demand without building.** Use a small test to see if people are truly interested. → [Validate demand without building](/en/validation/demand-without-building)
6. **Plan the smallest MVP experiment.** Run a test small enough to check one key assumption. → [MVP experiment planner](/en/validation/mvp-experiment-planner)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Wrote the customer and problem in one line
- [ ] Listed at least 20 people to talk to
- [ ] Ranked the problems and picked one
- [ ] Ran one demand test without building
- [ ] Wrote the next decision (continue / pivot / stop)
