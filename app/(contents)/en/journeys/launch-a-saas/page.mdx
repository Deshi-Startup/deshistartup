# I want to launch a SaaS from Bangladesh

> **In short:** Building SaaS from Bangladesh is possible, but you must understand up front how to sell to foreign customers and bring the money home. This path runs from market selection through company setup, foreign exchange, international payments, and customer acquisition.

This path is for diaspora and export-oriented founders who want to build a software service (SaaS) and sell mainly to foreign markets. Here the biggest challenge isn't the product — it's foreign exchange, remittance, and the limits of international payments.

## The path, step by step

1. **Understand the reality of export SaaS.** Decide the market and the problem up front. → [Export-oriented SaaS from Bangladesh](/en/phase-one/export-oriented-saas-from-bangladesh)
2. **Read the SaaS export playbook.** Get the full picture of sales, billing, and operations. → [SaaS export playbook](/en/phase-four/saas-export-playbook-from-bangladesh)
3. **Register a company.** Foreign customers and payments need a formal structure. → [Business registration](/en/registration)
4. **Learn the foreign-exchange rules.** Understand remittance and foreign-shareholder rules. → [Foreign exchange basics for SaaS export](/en/phase-one/foreign-exchange-basics-for-saas-export-remittance-and-foreign-shareholders)
5. **Understand international payment limits.** Know the real constraints on cards and payment links. → [International payment & card constraints](/en/phase-one/international-payment-card-and-payment-link-constraints)
6. **Track SaaS metrics.** Measure MRR, churn, and payback from day one. → [SaaS metrics](/en/phase-three/saas-metrics)
7. **Use export incentives.** Check the government incentives for IT/ITES. → [IT/ITES export incentives](/en/phase-four/export-incentives-for-itites)

## Checklist for this path

- [ ] Fixed the target market and customer
- [ ] Set up the company structure and registration
- [ ] Understood remittance/foreign-exchange rules
- [ ] Decided how to accept international payments
- [ ] Started tracking core SaaS metrics
