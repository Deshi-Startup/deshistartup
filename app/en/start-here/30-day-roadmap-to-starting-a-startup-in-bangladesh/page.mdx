# 30-Day Roadmap to Starting a Startup in Bangladesh

> **In short:** Turning a startup idea into reality does not have to take years. With the right plan, you can prepare to validate your idea in the market within 30 days.

This roadmap is designed for the Bangladeshi context, where entering the market quickly and learning from customer reactions is especially important.

## Week 1: Idea and Problem Validation

In the first week, do not build a product. Work only on the problem.
- **Days 1-2: Identify the problem.** What specific problem are you trying to solve? Is it truly a serious problem, or does it only feel important to you?
- **Days 3-4: Customer interviews.** Talk to at least 10-15 people who face this problem. Ask how they currently solve it. See: [Customer Discovery Interviews](../phase-one/customer-discovery-interviews-in-bangladesh).
- **Days 5-6: Competitor analysis.** Is anyone in Bangladesh or abroad working on this? How are they doing it? What are their weaknesses?
- **Day 7: Make a decision.** Move forward only if customer conversations show that the problem is real and people are willing to pay for a solution.

## Week 2: Solution Design and Legal Basics

This week, give your idea a basic structure.
- **Days 8-9: MVP planning.** Instead of building a full app or website, think about the cheapest way to get your solution in front of customers, such as a Facebook page or WhatsApp group.
- **Days 10-11: Business name and branding.** Choose a simple name. If needed, create a basic logo for free using Canva.
- **Days 12-14: Initial legal and payment setup.** You do not need RJSC company registration at the very beginning. But you can start the process for a [trade license](../phase-one/trade-license-guide) and prepare to accept payments through a merchant bKash/Nagad account or a bank account.

## Week 3: Build the MVP and Prepare for Market

Now prepare your basic product for launch.
- **Days 15-17: Build the MVP.** If you need a landing page, create one with WordPress, Wix, or Framer. If it is a service, define your service packages and pricing.
- **Days 18-19: Marketing plan.** Where will your first 10 customers come from: your personal network, Facebook groups, or a small-budget Facebook boost?
- **Days 20-21: Content and copywriting.** Create short posts or messages that explain your product’s value proposition.

## Week 4: Launch and First Customers

In the final week, take your MVP to market.
- **Days 22-24: Soft launch.** First share the product with people close to you, friends, or specific Facebook groups. Observe how they react.
- **Days 25-27: First 5-10 paid customers.** Do not rely only on free usage. Encourage customers to pay for your service or product. The first sales will prove whether real demand exists.
- **Days 28-29: Collect feedback.** Talk to people who used your product. Write down in detail what they liked and disliked.
- **Day 30: Plan the next step.** Decide what changes you need to make based on feedback. If people clearly do not want it, drop the idea and start working on a new one. This is called failing fast.

## Next Step
After 30 days, if your idea is working and some revenue has started coming in, you can move toward bigger steps such as [company registration](../phase-one/private-limited-company-registration-step-by-step) and fundraising.
